# YouTube Episode Format — Thai with Nine

## Guiding principle

Every segment of the video is introduced with **English narration that Nine reads aloud**, telling the viewer exactly what is about to happen and how to participate. This is what makes "You Too Can Learn Thai" work as long-form content — the English explanations are not optional scaffolding, they ARE the content alongside the Thai.

---

## Part-by-part structure (target 10-15 minutes)

### PART 1 — HOOK (0:00–0:30)

**Purpose:** Stop the scroll. Thai phrase on screen, immediate intrigue.

Nine says a Thai phrase at natural speed → 1-beat pause → English translation appears → Nine delivers a one-line promise for the episode.

**English narration:** Minimal. Just the promise line.

**Example:**
> สั่งอะไรดีคะ
> *(pause — English appears: "What would you like to order?")*
> "By the end of this video, you'll be able to order food, ask for recommendations, and get the bill — all in Thai."

---

### PART 2 — PERSONAL STORY + TOPIC INTRO (0:30–2:00)

**Purpose:** Build connection. Nine shares a personal anecdote related to today's topic, then sets up what the viewer will learn.

**English narration (read aloud by Nine):**
- A short personal story (30-60 seconds) — relatable, funny, grounded in real Thai life
- Embed 1-2 Thai phrases naturally in the story
- End with a clear setup: "Today I'm going to teach you [X phrases / how to do Y]. We'll go through each one, I'll explain what it means and how to use it, then you'll hear them all together in a real conversation, and finally we'll practise together."

**This section is mostly English** with Thai phrases woven in. It's the warm-up.

---

### PART 3 — VOCABULARY DEEP DIVE (2:00–5:30)

**Purpose:** Teach each word/phrase in depth. This is NOT a flash-card list — it's Nine explaining each word conversationally in English, with the Thai on screen.

**English narration (read aloud by Nine) — PER VOCAB ITEM:**

1. **Say the Thai word/phrase** clearly, twice
2. **English meaning** — not just a translation but an explanation: "This means X. You use it when Y."
3. **Usage notes** — grammar position, formality, common mistakes, cultural context
4. **Example sentence** — say the Thai example, then explain it in English
5. **Quick comparison** (if relevant) — "This is different from Z because..."

**English narration between vocab items:** Brief transitions like "OK, next word..." or "This next one is really useful..."

**English narration to open the section:**
> "Let's start with the vocabulary. I'm going to go through each word one by one. For each one, I'll say the Thai, explain what it means, and show you how to use it in a sentence. Ready? Let's go."

**Example (one vocab item):**
> "Our first word is สั่ง — sàng. สั่ง. This means 'to order'. You use it when you're ordering food, drinks, anything really. If someone asks you สั่งอะไรดี — 'what would you like to order?' — that's this word.
> Let me give you an example: คุณอยากสั่งอะไร — 'What would you like to order?' You hear สั่ง in there? คุณอยากสั่งอะไร."

**Target:** 30-45 seconds per vocab item. With 8 items = 4-6 minutes.

---

### PART 4 — NATURAL SPEED LISTEN (5:30–7:00)

**Purpose:** Hear all the vocab in a realistic context at natural conversational speed.

**English narration BEFORE the Thai (read aloud by Nine):**
> "OK, so now you know all eight phrases. What I'm going to do now is put them together into a short conversation — imagine you're sitting down at a street food stall in Bangkok. I'm going to speak at natural speed, the way a Thai person would actually talk. Don't pause the video. Just listen and see how much you can understand. Ready? Here we go."

**Then:** Nine delivers the Thai paragraph/dialogue at natural speed. No English. Thai script on screen.

**English narration AFTER the Thai:**
> "How was that? Could you catch some of those phrases? Don't worry if you didn't get everything — that's completely normal. Now let's break it down."

---

### PART 5 — SENTENCE-BY-SENTENCE BREAKDOWN (7:00–9:30)

**Purpose:** Go through the same conversation slowly, explaining each sentence.

**English narration to open (read aloud by Nine):**
> "I'm going to go through that same conversation again, but this time sentence by sentence. I'll say the Thai, and then I'll explain exactly what each part means."

**Per sentence:**
1. Nine says the Thai sentence slowly
2. Transliteration appears on screen
3. Nine explains in English: literal meaning, word order, any grammar worth noting
4. English translation appears
5. Nine says the Thai one more time

**Example:**
> แนะนำอะไรดีคะ
> "So this first one — แนะนำอะไรดีคะ. แนะนำ means 'to recommend'. อะไร means 'what'. ดี means 'good'. So literally it's 'recommend what good?' — which in natural English is 'What do you recommend?' Notice the question word อะไร goes in the middle in Thai, not at the beginning like in English."
> *(English translation appears: "What do you recommend?")*
> "One more time: แนะนำอะไรดีคะ"

---

### PART 6 — SLOW LISTEN / SHADOWING (9:30–11:00)

**Purpose:** Viewer practises saying each sentence along with Nine.

**English narration to open (read aloud by Nine):**
> "Now it's your turn to practise. I'm going to say each sentence slowly, and I want you to repeat after me. Don't be shy — say it out loud! Speaking is how you learn. I'll leave a gap after each sentence for you to repeat."

**Then:** Nine says each sentence slowly. 2-3 second pause after each for the viewer to repeat. Split-word Thai on screen with karaoke highlighting.

**English narration to close:**
> "Great job! If that felt hard, that's totally fine — rewind and do it again. Repetition is how you get better."

---

### PART 7 — PRODUCTION DRILL / QUIZ (11:00–12:30)

**Purpose:** Test recall. Viewer has to produce the Thai from an English prompt.

**English narration to open (read aloud by Nine):**
> "OK, quiz time! I'm going to give you a situation in English, and you need to say the Thai phrase. I'll give you a few seconds to think, and then I'll give you the answer. Ready?"

**Per drill:**
1. Nine gives the English prompt/situation
2. "Try it now..." appears on screen
3. 3-second silence
4. Nine gives the answer in Thai
5. Brief English confirmation: "That's right — [Thai phrase] means [English]."

---

### PART 8 — RECAP + NEXT EPISODE TEASER (12:30–13:30)

**Purpose:** Quick review of all vocab, build anticipation for next episode.

**English narration:**
> "Let's do a quick recap of everything you learned today."

Quick flash through all vocab cards (Thai → English, no deep explanation this time).

**Teaser:**
> "In the next episode, we're going to learn [topic]. Here's a sneak peek..."
> *(Nine says one Thai phrase from the next episode)*
> "If you want to know what that means, make sure you subscribe so you don't miss it."

---

## Key format rules

1. **Every section opens with English narration** — Nine always tells the viewer what's about to happen and how to participate before switching to Thai
2. **Vocabulary is explained, not listed** — Each word gets 30-45 seconds of English explanation with usage notes, not just a translation flash card
3. **The natural-speed listen is framed** — "Just listen, don't pause" before; "How was that?" after
4. **The breakdown teaches grammar and word order** — Not just "here's the translation" but "here's WHY it's structured this way"
5. **Shadowing gets instructions** — "Repeat after me, say it out loud, don't be shy"
6. **Drills get setup** — "Quiz time! I'll give you English, you say the Thai"
7. **This is long-form content** — The English explanations ARE the content. Don't compress them.

## What this means for the JSON schema

The current `mode` values in blocks are fine, but we need:

1. **A `section-intro` mode** — For the English narration that introduces each section. These blocks are mostly English, spoken by Nine, and don't have Thai on screen.
2. **Richer `vocab-card` blocks** — Each vocab item needs an `explanation` field (English text Nine reads aloud explaining usage, grammar, cultural notes) in addition to the bare `thai`/`translit`/`english` fields.
3. **`explain` blocks within breakdown** — After each Thai sentence in the breakdown, there should be an explain block with the grammar/word-order notes.
4. **A `transition` field on blocks** — Optional English line Nine says to bridge between blocks (e.g., "OK, next word..." or "How was that?").
