## PART 3 — VOCABULARY DEEP DIVE

### Section intro

> *Look to camera, clear and encouraging.*

OK, let's start with the vocabulary. I'm going to go through each of the eight phrases one by one. For each one, I'll say the Thai, explain what it means, and show you how to use it in a sentence. Don't worry about memorising everything right now — we'll practise later.

---

### Vocab 1: สั่ง (sàng) — to order

> *Say the Thai clearly, twice. Then explain conversationally.*

Our first word is สั่ง.

สั่ง.

This means "to order." You use it when you're ordering food, ordering drinks — ordering anything really. It's the most basic word you need at a restaurant or a food stall.

If someone asks you สั่งอะไรดีคะ — that's "what would you like to order?" — สั่ง is the key word in there.

Let me give you an example sentence: คุณอยากสั่งอะไร — "What would you like to order?" You hear สั่ง in the middle? คุณอยากสั่งอะไร.

---

### Vocab 2: เผ็ด (phèt) — spicy

> *Say it with emphasis. This is a fun one — lean into the spicy culture.*

Next up — เผ็ด.

เผ็ด.

This means "spicy." And if you're eating in Thailand, you NEED this word, because Thai food can be seriously spicy. Like, a completely different level from what you might be used to.

If someone asks you เผ็ดไหม — "Is it spicy?" — you want to know what that means before you say yes.

---

### Vocab 3: ไม่เผ็ด (mâi phèt) — not spicy

> *Immediately follow up with the negative form. Teach ไม่ as a pattern.*

Now, if you can't handle the heat — and there's no shame in that — you need ไม่เผ็ด.

ไม่เผ็ด.

ไม่ means "not" or "no." So ไม่เผ็ด is "not spicy." This is really useful because in Thai, you make something negative just by putting ไม่ in front of it. ไม่ plus whatever. ไม่เผ็ด — not spicy.

And if you want to tell the cook "no spicy, please" you say: ไม่เอาเผ็ดนะคะ. That's the polite way to say it. ไม่เอาเผ็ดนะคะ — "No spicy, please."

---

### Vocab 4: อร่อย (a-ròoy) — delicious

> *Say it with genuine enthusiasm. This word should feel like a compliment.*

This next one is one of my favourite Thai words — อร่อย.

อร่อย.

It means "delicious." Thai people LOVE it when you say this. If you eat something and you say อร่อย, the cook's face will light up. It's a compliment. Use it.

And if you really mean it, you can say อร่อยมาก — "very delicious." มาก means "very" or "a lot." So อร่อยมาก — "really delicious." That's the one that gets you extra portions.

---

### Vocab 5: ขอ (khǎaw) — may I have / I'd like

> *This is the most important structural word. Spend a bit more time here.*

OK this next one is really important. ขอ.

ขอ.

This is one of the most useful words in Thai. ขอ means "may I have" or "I'd like." You put it at the beginning of your request — ขอ, then whatever you want, then ค่ะ or ครับ at the end to be polite.

So for example: ขอผัดไทยหนึ่งจานค่ะ — "I'd like one Pad Thai, please." Let me break that down. ขอ — "I'd like." ผัดไทย — "Pad Thai." หนึ่ง — "one." จาน — that's the classifier for plates of food. ค่ะ — polite ending for women.

ขอผัดไทยหนึ่งจานค่ะ. That's a complete order. You can swap out ผัดไทย for any dish — ขอข้าวผัดหนึ่งจานค่ะ for fried rice, ขอส้มตำหนึ่งจานค่ะ for papaya salad. Same pattern every time.

---

### Vocab 6: เก็บเงิน (gèp ngern) — check please / bill please

> *Practical, everyday. Mime the universal "bill please" hand gesture.*

When you're done eating, you need this one — เก็บเงิน.

เก็บเงิน.

This means "check please" or "bill please." เก็บ means "to collect" and เงิน means "money." So literally it's "collect money" — which is a very direct way of saying "I'd like to pay."

The full polite version is: เก็บเงินด้วยค่ะ. ด้วย makes it softer — like "also" or "please." เก็บเงินด้วยค่ะ — "Can I have the bill, please?"

---

### Vocab 7: แนะนำ (náe-nam) — to recommend

> *Great for when you don't know what to order — which is most of the time as a beginner.*

This is a great one for when you look at a menu and have absolutely no idea what to order — แนะนำ.

แนะนำ.

It means "to recommend." So if you ask แนะนำอะไรดีคะ — "What do you recommend?" — the vendor will pick their best dish for you. This is honestly one of the best ways to eat in Thailand. Just let them choose.

แนะนำอะไรดีคะ — "What do you recommend?" Notice that the question word อะไร goes in the middle in Thai, not at the beginning like in English.

---

### Vocab 8: อิ่ม (ìm) — full (not hungry)

> *Nice closer for the meal. Quick and simple.*

Last one for today — อิ่ม.

อิ่ม.

This means "full" — as in "I'm not hungry anymore." After a big meal, you say อิ่มแล้วค่ะ — "I'm full." แล้ว means "already," so literally it's "full already." อิ่มแล้วค่ะ.

If someone offers you more food and you're done, this is the polite way to say no thanks.

---

### Vocab section close

> *Quick energy, look to camera.*

OK! So those are your eight phrases. สั่ง, เผ็ด, ไม่เผ็ด, อร่อย, ขอ, เก็บเงิน, แนะนำ, and อิ่ม.

Now let's hear them in action.

## PART 5 — SENTENCE-BY-SENTENCE BREAKDOWN

### Section intro

> *Clear, instructional.*

I'm going to go through that same conversation again, but this time one sentence at a time. I'll say the Thai slowly, explain what each part means, and then you'll see the English translation on screen.

---

### Sentence 1: แนะนำอะไรดีคะ

> *Say it slowly. Then explain in English. Then say it one more time.*

แนะนำอะไรดีคะ

So this first one — แนะนำอะไรดีคะ. We learned แนะนำ means "to recommend." อะไร means "what." ดี means "good." So literally it's "recommend what good?" — which in natural English is "What do you recommend?"

*(English appears: "What do you recommend?")*

One more time: แนะนำอะไรดีคะ.

---

### Sentence 2: ขอผัดไทยหนึ่งจานค่ะ

> *Break down each word.*

ขอผัดไทยหนึ่งจานค่ะ

This is our ordering phrase. ขอ — "I'd like." ผัดไทย — "Pad Thai." หนึ่ง — "one." จาน — "plate." ค่ะ — polite ending. So: "I'd like one Pad Thai, please."

*(English appears: "I'd like one Pad Thai, please.")*

ขอผัดไทยหนึ่งจานค่ะ.

---

### Sentence 3: ไม่เอาเผ็ดนะคะ

ไม่เอาเผ็ดนะคะ

ไม่ — "not." เอา — "want" or "take." เผ็ด — "spicy." นะคะ — a softening particle that makes it sound polite and friendly. So together: "I don't want spicy, please." Or more naturally: "No spicy, please."

*(English appears: "No spicy, please.")*

ไม่เอาเผ็ดนะคะ.

---

### Sentence 4: อร่อยมาก

อร่อยมาก

This one's simple. อร่อย — "delicious." มาก — "very." "Very delicious!" Remember, Thai people love hearing this.

*(English appears: "Very delicious!")*

อร่อยมาก.

---

### Sentence 5: อิ่มแล้วค่ะ

อิ่มแล้วค่ะ

อิ่ม — "full." แล้ว — "already." ค่ะ — polite ending. "I'm full already." You say this when you've had enough.

*(English appears: "I'm full.")*

อิ่มแล้วค่ะ.

---

### Sentence 6: เก็บเงินด้วยค่ะ

เก็บเงินด้วยค่ะ

And the last one — เก็บ — "collect." เงิน — "money." ด้วย — "also" or "please." ค่ะ — polite. "Can I have the bill, please?"

*(English appears: "Can I have the bill, please?")*

เก็บเงินด้วยค่ะ.

---

### Breakdown close

> *Look to camera. Encouraging.*

See? When you break it down, it's not so scary. Every sentence is just a few words put together in a pattern. Once you learn the pattern, you can swap in different dishes, different requests — and suddenly you can order anything.
