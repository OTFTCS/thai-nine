## PART 3 — VOCABULARY DEEP DIVE

### Section intro

> *Look to camera, clear.*

OK, let's learn your taxi vocabulary. Eight phrases. I'll go through each one, explain what it means, and show you exactly when to use it during a taxi ride. Let's start.

---

### Vocab 1: ไป (bpai) — to go

> *Simple but foundational. Show how it's the building block.*

First word — ไป.

ไป.

This means "to go." It's one of the most common words in Thai, and in a taxi it's the first thing you say. You get in, and you say ไป plus your destination. That's it. ไปสยามค่ะ — "go to Siam, please." ไปสุขุมวิทค่ะ — "go to Sukhumvit, please." ไป plus wherever you're going plus ค่ะ. That's your pattern.

You can use this for any destination. ไปสนามบินค่ะ — "go to the airport." ไปเซ็นทรัลค่ะ — "go to Central." Whatever you need — ไป plus the place.

---

### Vocab 2: เปิดมิเตอร์ (bpèrt mí-dtêr) — use the meter

> *This is THE phrase of the episode. Give it weight. Lean into the cultural context.*

This is the important one — the one that saves you money. เปิดมิเตอร์.

เปิดมิเตอร์.

This means "use the meter" or literally "turn on the meter." เปิด means "to open" or "to turn on" — you use it for anything you switch on, like a light or an air conditioner. มิเตอร์ — can you hear it? It's from the English word "meter." Thai borrows a lot of English words but gives them a Thai pronunciation. So "meter" becomes มิเตอร์ — mí-dtêr.

The full polite phrase is: เปิดมิเตอร์ด้วยค่ะ. We saw ด้วย in the last episode — it softens the request, like saying "please" or "also." เปิดมิเตอร์ด้วยค่ะ — "Please use the meter."

Here's a tip: you say this right after you tell the driver where you're going. Get in, say ไปสยามค่ะ, and then immediately follow it with เปิดมิเตอร์ด้วยนะคะ. นะ is another softening particle — it makes it sound friendly rather than demanding. You're not fighting — you're just politely setting the expectation.

---

### Vocab 3: จอดตรงนี้ (jàwt trong níi) — stop here

> *Mime pulling over. This is your "I've arrived" phrase.*

Next — จอดตรงนี้.

จอดตรงนี้.

This means "stop here." จอด means "to park" or "to stop." ตรงนี้ means "right here." So จอดตรงนี้ — "stop right here." You say this when you've arrived and you want the driver to pull over.

The polite version: จอดตรงนี้ด้วยค่ะ — "Please stop here." Again, ด้วย makes it polite.

---

### Vocab 4: ซ้ายมือ (sáai muu) — on the left side

> *Gesture left. Explain the มือ pattern.*

Now, sometimes you don't just want to say "stop here" — you want to say which side of the road. ซ้ายมือ.

ซ้ายมือ.

This means "on the left side" or "left-hand side." We learned ซ้าย means "left" in the last episode. มือ literally means "hand." So ซ้ายมือ — "left hand" — is how Thai says "the left side." You can say จอดซ้ายมือค่ะ — "stop on the left side, please."

---

### Vocab 5: ขวามือ (khwǎa muu) — on the right side

> *Quick pair with the previous one.*

And the opposite — ขวามือ.

ขวามือ.

"Right-hand side." ขวา — "right." มือ — "hand." ขวามือ — "right side." So จอดขวามือค่ะ — "stop on the right side." Same pattern.

---

### Vocab 6: เท่าไหร่ (thâo-rài) — how much?

> *This is a top-5 most useful Thai word. Give it the weight it deserves.*

OK this next one is one of the most useful words in the entire Thai language. Not just for taxis — for everything. เท่าไหร่.

เท่าไหร่.

This means "how much?" You use it at markets, restaurants, shops, and at the end of a taxi ride. เท่าไหร่คะ — "How much?" That's it. Two words. เท่าไหร่คะ. You'll use this one constantly.

In a taxi, you'd say it at the end of the ride if you didn't use the meter — or if you just want to confirm the fare.

---

### Vocab 7: แพง (phaeng) — expensive

> *Funny, useful. Set up the bargaining culture.*

Now, sometimes the answer to เท่าไหร่ is... not what you were hoping for. That's when you need แพง.

แพง.

"Expensive." If a driver quotes you a price and it sounds too high, you can say แพง — "that's expensive." Or even better — แพงไป — "too expensive." ไป here means "too much" — it's the same ไป as "to go" but in this context it means "excessively."

---

### Vocab 8: ลดได้ไหม (lót dâai mǎi) — can you reduce the price?

> *Teach the ได้ไหม pattern — it's hugely useful beyond just taxis.*

And the final one — this is your bargaining phrase — ลดได้ไหม.

ลดได้ไหม.

This means "can you reduce the price?" or "can you give a discount?" ลด means "to reduce." And then ได้ไหม — this is a really useful pattern in Thai. ได้ means "can" or "able to." ไหม is the question particle. So ได้ไหม means "can you?" or "is it possible?"

You can use ได้ไหม after almost any verb to ask "can you do X?" ลดได้ไหม — "Can you reduce?" เปิดได้ไหม — "Can you open?" ช่วยได้ไหม — "Can you help?" It's one of those patterns that unlocks a whole bunch of sentences once you know it.

ลดได้ไหมคะ — "Can you reduce the price, please?"

---

> *Quick energy.*

OK! Those are your eight taxi phrases. ไป, เปิดมิเตอร์, จอดตรงนี้, ซ้ายมือ, ขวามือ, เท่าไหร่, แพง, and ลดได้ไหม.

## PART 5 — SENTENCE-BY-SENTENCE BREAKDOWN

### Section intro

> *Clear, instructional.*

OK, same conversation, but this time sentence by sentence. I'll explain what's happening at each point in the ride and break down the Thai.

---

### Sentence 1: สวัสดีค่ะ ไปสยามค่ะ

> *Set the scene.*

สวัสดีค่ะ ไปสยามค่ะ

So you've just got in the taxi. First — สวัสดีค่ะ — "hello." Always greet the driver. Then straight into it — ไปสยามค่ะ — "go to Siam, please." ไป — "go." สยาม — "Siam," the area. ค่ะ — polite ending. Short, clear, and the driver knows exactly where you want to go.

*(English appears: "Hello. Go to Siam, please.")*

สวัสดีค่ะ ไปสยามค่ะ.

---

### Sentence 2: เปิดมิเตอร์ด้วยนะคะ

เปิดมิเตอร์ด้วยนะคะ

This is the money-saver. เปิดมิเตอร์ — "turn on the meter." ด้วย — softens it, like "please." นะคะ — this is interesting. นะ is a softening particle — it makes the whole sentence sound friendly and non-confrontational. Like you're saying "please use the meter, OK?" rather than demanding it. คะ is the polite question-like ending. Thai people use นะคะ a lot to keep things warm and friendly.

*(English appears: "Please use the meter.")*

เปิดมิเตอร์ด้วยนะคะ.

---

### Sentence 3: เลี้ยวซ้ายตรงนี้ค่ะ

เลี้ยวซ้ายตรงนี้ค่ะ

Now you're giving directions during the ride. เลี้ยวซ้าย — "turn left." ตรงนี้ — "right here." So: "Turn left right here, please." You'd say this when you're approaching the turn.

*(English appears: "Turn left here, please.")*

เลี้ยวซ้ายตรงนี้ค่ะ.

---

### Sentence 4: จอดตรงนี้ด้วยค่ะ

จอดตรงนี้ด้วยค่ะ

You've arrived. จอด — "stop." ตรงนี้ — "right here." ด้วย — "please." "Please stop here." The driver pulls over.

*(English appears: "Please stop here.")*

จอดตรงนี้ด้วยค่ะ.

---

### Sentence 5: เท่าไหร่คะ

เท่าไหร่คะ

And finally — เท่าไหร่คะ — "How much?" If you used the meter, this is just confirming the fare. If you didn't use the meter... well, now you know what to expect.

*(English appears: "How much?")*

เท่าไหร่คะ.

---
