Bangkok's streets have a numbering system that confuses everyone — even Thais. Side streets are called soi. Odd numbers on one side, even on the other. And if a new street gets built between Soi 7 and Soi 9? It becomes Soi 7/1. House numbers are assigned in registration order, not geographic order — so number 12 might be next to number 87.
In Thailand, people are incredibly helpful with directions. If you ask, most people will try their best to explain — even if their English is limited. So the phrase that opens everything is:
ขอโทษค่ะ สถานีรถไฟฟ้าอยู่ที่ไหนคะ
Excuse me, where is the BTS station? Today I'm going to teach you 8 essential phrases for asking directions in Thai. We'll go through each word, then you'll hear them in a real conversation, and finally we'll practise together.

## VOCAB CARD

อยู่ที่ไหน

---

## VOCAB EXPLAIN

อยู่ที่ไหน — yùu thîi nǎi. This means 'where is it?' or 'where is...?' อยู่ means 'to be located', and ที่ไหน means 'where'. The key thing: in Thai, the question word goes at the end. So instead of saying 'Where is the bathroom?', you say 'Bathroom — where is it?' — ห้องน้ำอยู่ที่ไหน. Just put the place first, then อยู่ที่ไหน at the end. Works for anything.
ร้านกาแฟอยู่ที่ไหนคะ
That means 'Where is the coffee shop?' ร้านกาแฟ is coffee shop. See how อยู่ที่ไหน comes right at the end? That's the pattern every time.

---

## VOCAB CARD

OK, the first direction word.
ตรงไป

---

## VOCAB EXPLAIN

ตรงไป — trong bpai. This means 'go straight'. ตรง means 'straight' and ไป means 'go'. Short, easy, you'll hear it constantly. People will often say it while pointing, so even if they say it fast you'll know what they mean. ตรงไปแล้วเลี้ยวซ้าย — 'go straight then turn left' — is probably the most common direction answer in Thailand.
ตรงไปแล้วก็เห็นเลยค่ะ
That means 'Go straight and you'll see it right away.' เห็น is 'see', เลย means 'right there'. Very common reassurance when you're close.

---

## VOCAB CARD

Next — turn left.
เลี้ยวซ้าย

---

## VOCAB EXPLAIN

เลี้ยวซ้าย — líiaw sáai. Turn left. เลี้ยว means 'turn', ซ้าย means 'left'. Falling tone on ซ้าย — your voice drops. I always tell my students: practise ซ้าย and ขวา together as a pair, with the gesture, until they're automatic. If you get them wrong you'll go the wrong direction — which I've seen happen to many students.
เลี้ยวซ้ายตรงสี่แยกครับ
That means 'Turn left at the intersection.' สี่แยก means 'four-way intersection' — สี่ is four, แยก is split. You'll hear this landmark constantly in directions.

---

## VOCAB CARD

And the opposite — turn right.
เลี้ยวขวา

---

## VOCAB EXPLAIN

เลี้ยวขวา — líiaw khwǎa. Turn right. ขวา has a rising tone — voice goes up. Compare: ซ้าย falls, ขวา rises. That tonal contrast is how you tell them apart. If you can nail those two tones you'll always go the right way. ขวา also means 'the right side' on its own — you'll see it on signs.
เลี้ยวขวาแล้วตรงไปเลยค่ะ
That means 'Turn right and then just go straight.' แล้ว means 'then', เลย makes it sound natural — 'just go right ahead from there'.

---

## VOCAB CARD

OK, next — near.
ใกล้

---

## VOCAB EXPLAIN

ใกล้ — glâi. This means 'near' or 'close'. Falling tone — voice goes down. After you get directions, the natural follow-up question is ใกล้ไหมคะ — 'Is it near?' — and if they say ใกล้ค่ะ you can walk, if not you might need a taxi. Really useful short question that Thai people use all the time.
ใกล้แค่นี้เองค่ะ
That means 'It's only this close.' แค่นี้เอง is a really natural Thai expression — 'just this much'. It's the kind of thing someone says to reassure you that you haven't walked too far.

---

## VOCAB CARD

And the opposite — far.
ไกล

---

## VOCAB EXPLAIN

ไกล — glai. This means 'far'. Mid tone — flat. Compare with ใกล้ which falls. They look almost identical on paper — ใกล้ and ไกล — but the tone makes all the difference. This is one of those pairs you just have to drill. A warning: when someone says ไกลนิดหน่อย — 'a little far' — it usually means very far. Thai people are polite.
ไกลมากไหมคะ
That means 'Is it very far?' มาก adds 'very', ไหม makes it a question. Useful when you're on foot and wondering whether you need transport.

---

## VOCAB CARD

Now — probably the most urgent direction question you'll ever ask.
ห้องน้ำ

---

## VOCAB EXPLAIN

ห้องน้ำ — hâwng náam. This literally means 'water room' — ห้อง is room, น้ำ is water. It's the everyday word for bathroom or toilet. You'll see it on signs everywhere — shopping malls, restaurants, petrol stations. Just add อยู่ที่ไหน after it and you have the most-used direction question in Thailand.
ขอถามหน่อยได้ไหมคะ ห้องน้ำอยู่แถวไหนคะ
That means 'Excuse me, which area is the bathroom in?' อยู่แถวไหน is a softer version — แถว means 'area around'. More casual than อยู่ที่ไหน. Both work perfectly.

---

## VOCAB CARD

And the last one — this one is your lifeline in Bangkok.
สถานีรถไฟฟ้า

---

## VOCAB EXPLAIN

สถานีรถไฟฟ้า — sa-thǎa-nii rót-fai-fáa. BTS Skytrain station. Break it down: สถานี is station, รถ is vehicle, ไฟฟ้า is electric — so it's literally 'electric train station'. The BTS is the easiest way to get around Bangkok and stations are visible landmarks on every main road. If you're lost, asking for the nearest station gets you to safety fast.
สถานีรถไฟฟ้าอยู่ใกล้ไหมคะ
That means 'Is the BTS station nearby?' Notice how this combines สถานีรถไฟฟ้า, อยู่, and ใกล้ไหม — three of your eight words working together. That's exactly how it sounds in a real conversation.

## EXPLAIN

ขอโทษค่ะ is 'excuse me' — a polite opener before asking a stranger anything. Then สถานีรถไฟฟ้า is the BTS station. อยู่ที่ไหน — where is it? คะ is the soft polite particle for women. Notice how the question word ที่ไหน comes right at the end — place first, then 'where' at the end. That's the rule.

---

## BREAKDOWN

> *Switch voice — this is the helper replying.*

ตรงไปแล้วเลี้ยวซ้ายค่ะ

---

## EXPLAIN

ตรงไป — go straight. แล้ว — then. เลี้ยวซ้าย — turn left. The structure in Thai is the same as English here: action, then next action. แล้ว is your 'then' connector. Very simple, very logical.

---

## BREAKDOWN

ใกล้ไหมคะ

---

## EXPLAIN

ใกล้ is 'near'. ไหม turns it into a yes/no question — it's the Thai question particle. So ใกล้ไหม is literally 'near?' — that's all you need. Super short, completely natural.

---

## BREAKDOWN

ใกล้ค่ะ ห้านาทีค่ะ

---

## EXPLAIN

ใกล้ค่ะ — it's close. ห้า is five, นาที is minutes. Thais often answer distance questions in walking time rather than metres — which is actually more useful. ห้านาที means you can walk. ยี่สิบนาที means get a taxi.
