## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-009

- `l-0022` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** ลดได้ไหม — lót dâai mǎi. This is 'can you reduce the price?' Let me break it apart: ลด means 'reduce' or 'lower', ได้ means 'can' or 'able to', and ไหม is the yes/no question particle — it turns any statement into a question. So you're literally asking 'lower, can?' It sounds direct but it's perfectly polite. At a market, this is the phrase that starts the negotiation.
- `l-0023` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** ถ้าซื้อสองอัน ลดได้ไหมคะ
  - **Translit:** thâa súue sǎawng an lót dâai mǎi khá
- `l-0024` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'If I buy two, can you reduce the price?' You can level up your haggling by adding a condition before ลดได้ไหม — it gives the vendor a reason to say yes.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-017

- `l-0042` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** ใบ — bai. This is a classifier. In Thai, when you count things, you can't just say a number — you need a special counting word that matches the type of object. ใบ is used for bags, leaves, tickets, and flat or hollow items. So if you want two bags, you say สองใบ, not just สอง. This is one of the biggest grammar differences from English, but once you get the pattern it becomes second nature.
- `l-0043` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** ขอถุงพลาสติกสองใบค่ะ
  - **Translit:** khǎaw thǔng-phlát-sà-tìk sǎawng bai khâ
- `l-0044` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'Can I have two plastic bags, please?' ถุง is bag, สองใบ is two (of bag-type things). The classifier ใบ always comes after the number.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-019
