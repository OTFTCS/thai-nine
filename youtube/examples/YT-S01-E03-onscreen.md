## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-005

- `l-0010` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** ไป — bpai. This means 'to go'. It's the first word out of your mouth when you get in a taxi. You just say ไป and then the destination — that's it. No grammar, no complicated structure. ไปสยาม — go to Siam. ไปสุขุมวิท — go to Sukhumvit. ไปสนามบิน — go to the airport. Simple.
- `l-0011` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** ไปสนามบินสุวรรณภูมิค่ะ
  - **Translit:** bpai sa-nǎam-bin su-wan-na-phuum khâ
- `l-0012` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'Go to Suvarnabhumi Airport, please.' สนามบิน is airport. สุวรรณภูมิ is the airport name. ไป + destination + คะ — that's your complete taxi instruction.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-007

- `l-0015` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** เปิดมิเตอร์ — bpèrt mí-dtêr. 'Use the meter' — literally 'turn on the meter'. เปิด means 'open' or 'turn on', and มิเตอร์ is borrowed from English. Say this as soon as you get in, before you tell the driver where to go. Most drivers will comply immediately. If they refuse, you just get out and find another taxi — there are always more.
- `l-0016` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** เปิดมิเตอร์ด้วยนะคะ
  - **Translit:** bpèrt mí-dtêr dûuay ná khá
- `l-0017` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'Please use the meter'. ด้วย adds 'also' which softens it, and นะ is a gentle particle. Together they make it sound polite and conversational rather than demanding.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-009

- `l-0020` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** จอดตรงนี้ — jàwt trong níi. This means 'stop here'. จอด means 'stop' or 'park', ตรงนี้ means 'right here'. You say this when you're close to your destination and want the driver to pull over. Add ด้วยค่ะ at the end — จอดตรงนี้ด้วยค่ะ — it's the standard polite version you'll hear Thai people use.
- `l-0021` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** จอดตรงหน้าร้านนั้นได้เลยค่ะ
  - **Translit:** jàwt trong nâa ráan nán dâai loei khâ
- `l-0022` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'You can stop right in front of that shop.' ตรงหน้า means 'right in front of', ได้เลย means 'you can go ahead'. A natural way to direct the driver to a specific spot.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-011

- `l-0025` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** ซ้ายมือ — sáai muu. 'On the left side' — literally 'left hand'. ซ้าย is left, มือ is hand. This tells the driver which side of the road to stop on. You'll say it once you're in the right street and need to indicate the exact side. Combine it with จอด: จอดซ้ายมือค่ะ — 'stop on the left side'.
- `l-0026` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** บ้านอยู่ซ้ายมือค่ะ
  - **Translit:** bâan yùu sáai muu khâ
- `l-0027` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'The house is on the left side.' บ้าน is house, อยู่ is located. Simple, clear. The driver knows exactly where to pull over.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-013

  - **English:** That means 'The shop is on the right side — the white building.' Adding a landmark like ตึกสีขาว — white building — makes it much clearer. Thai people always add a landmark.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-015

- `l-0035` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** เท่าไหร่ — thâo-rài. 'How much?' This is the most universal price question in Thai — you'll use it in markets, restaurants, taxis, everywhere. In a taxi it tells the driver you're checking the fare. If the meter has been running, the driver will just say the number. Question word goes at the end — เท่าไหร่คะ on its own is a complete sentence.
- `l-0036` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** ค่าโดยสารเท่าไหร่คะ
  - **Translit:** khâa doi-sǎan thâo-rài khá
- `l-0037` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'How much is the fare?' ค่าโดยสาร is the formal word for fare. You can also just say เท่าไหร่คะ and point at the meter — totally normal.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-017

- `l-0040` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** แพง — phaeng. 'Expensive'. If a driver quotes you a flat rate that seems high, say แพงไปค่ะ — 'that's too expensive'. ไป here means 'excessively' or 'too much'. It's polite, it's direct, and every driver understands it as the start of a negotiation. Don't be shy about saying it — bargaining is normal in Thailand, even for taxis.
- `l-0041` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** ร้อยห้าสิบบาทแพงไปหน่อยนะคะ
  - **Translit:** ráwi-hâa-sìp bàat phaeng bpai nàuy ná khá
- `l-0042` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'A hundred and fifty baht is a little expensive.' หน่อย and นะ soften it — you're not angry, you're just negotiating. Very natural Thai.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-019

- `l-0045` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** ลดได้ไหม — lót dâai mǎi. 'Can you come down on the price?' ลด is 'reduce', ได้ is 'can', ไหม is the yes/no question particle. You use this right after แพงไปค่ะ. Together they're your negotiation combo. Say แพงไปค่ะ, then ลดได้ไหมคะ — the driver will either say yes and name a lower price, or say no and you decide.
- `l-0046` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** สองร้อยบาทลดได้ไหมคะ
  - **Translit:** sǎawng ráwi bàat lót dâai mǎi khá
- `l-0047` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'Can you bring two hundred baht down?' Leading with the price you know is fair shows you're informed, not just a tourist. That changes the dynamic completely.
