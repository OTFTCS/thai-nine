## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-005

- `l-0010` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** อยู่ที่ไหน — yùu thîi nǎi. This means 'where is it?' or 'where is...?' อยู่ means 'to be located', and ที่ไหน means 'where'. The key thing: in Thai, the question word goes at the end. So instead of saying 'Where is the bathroom?', you say 'Bathroom — where is it?' — ห้องน้ำอยู่ที่ไหน. Just put the place first, then อยู่ที่ไหน at the end. Works for anything.
- `l-0011` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** ร้านกาแฟอยู่ที่ไหนคะ
  - **Translit:** ráan gaa-fae yùu thîi nǎi khá
- `l-0012` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'Where is the coffee shop?' ร้านกาแฟ is coffee shop. See how อยู่ที่ไหน comes right at the end? That's the pattern every time.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-007

- `l-0015` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** ตรงไป — trong bpai. This means 'go straight'. ตรง means 'straight' and ไป means 'go'. Short, easy, you'll hear it constantly. People will often say it while pointing, so even if they say it fast you'll know what they mean. ตรงไปแล้วเลี้ยวซ้าย — 'go straight then turn left' — is probably the most common direction answer in Thailand.
- `l-0016` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** ตรงไปแล้วก็เห็นเลยค่ะ
  - **Translit:** trong bpai láaew gâaw hěn loei khâ
- `l-0017` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'Go straight and you'll see it right away.' เห็น is 'see', เลย means 'right there'. Very common reassurance when you're close.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-009

- `l-0020` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** เลี้ยวซ้าย — líiaw sáai. Turn left. เลี้ยว means 'turn', ซ้าย means 'left'. Falling tone on ซ้าย — your voice drops. I always tell my students: practise ซ้าย and ขวา together as a pair, with the gesture, until they're automatic. If you get them wrong you'll go the wrong direction — which I've seen happen to many students.
- `l-0021` 🗣️

- `l-0022` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'Turn left at the intersection.' สี่แยก means 'four-way intersection' — สี่ is four, แยก is split. You'll hear this landmark constantly in directions.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-011

- `l-0025` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** เลี้ยวขวา — líiaw khwǎa. Turn right. ขวา has a rising tone — voice goes up. Compare: ซ้าย falls, ขวา rises. That tonal contrast is how you tell them apart. If you can nail those two tones you'll always go the right way. ขวา also means 'the right side' on its own — you'll see it on signs.
- `l-0026` 🗣️

- `l-0027` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'Turn right and then just go straight.' แล้ว means 'then', เลย makes it sound natural — 'just go right ahead from there'.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-013

- `l-0030` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** ใกล้ — glâi. This means 'near' or 'close'. Falling tone — voice goes down. After you get directions, the natural follow-up question is ใกล้ไหมคะ — 'Is it near?' — and if they say ใกล้ค่ะ you can walk, if not you might need a taxi. Really useful short question that Thai people use all the time.
- `l-0031` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** ใกล้แค่นี้เองค่ะ
  - **Translit:** glâi khâe níi eeng khâ
- `l-0032` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'It's only this close.' แค่นี้เอง is a really natural Thai expression — 'just this much'. It's the kind of thing someone says to reassure you that you haven't walked too far.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-015

- `l-0035` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** ไกล — glai. This means 'far'. Mid tone — flat. Compare with ใกล้ which falls. They look almost identical on paper — ใกล้ and ไกล — but the tone makes all the difference. This is one of those pairs you just have to drill. A warning: when someone says ไกลนิดหน่อย — 'a little far' — it usually means very far. Thai people are polite.
- `l-0036` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** ไกลมากไหมคะ
  - **Translit:** glai mâak mǎi khá
- `l-0037` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'Is it very far?' มาก adds 'very', ไหม makes it a question. Useful when you're on foot and wondering whether you need transport.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-017

- `l-0040` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** ห้องน้ำ — hâwng náam. This literally means 'water room' — ห้อง is room, น้ำ is water. It's the everyday word for bathroom or toilet. You'll see it on signs everywhere — shopping malls, restaurants, petrol stations. Just add อยู่ที่ไหน after it and you have the most-used direction question in Thailand.
- `l-0041` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** ขอถามหน่อยได้ไหมคะ ห้องน้ำอยู่แถวไหนคะ
  - **Translit:** khǎaw thǎam nàuy dâai mǎi khá hâwng náam yùu thǎaew nǎi khá
- `l-0042` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'Excuse me, which area is the bathroom in?' อยู่แถวไหน is a softer version — แถว means 'area around'. More casual than อยู่ที่ไหน. Both work perfectly.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-019

- `l-0045` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** สถานีรถไฟฟ้า — sa-thǎa-nii rót-fai-fáa. BTS Skytrain station. Break it down: สถานี is station, รถ is vehicle, ไฟฟ้า is electric — so it's literally 'electric train station'. The BTS is the easiest way to get around Bangkok and stations are visible landmarks on every main road. If you're lost, asking for the nearest station gets you to safety fast.
