## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-009

- `l-0021` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** หาร — hǎan. This is literally the Thai word for 'divide' — it comes from maths class. But Thais use it for splitting the bill too. The most common form is หารกัน — divide together — let's split it. You can also say หารครึ่ง to split in half. Younger Thais split bills more and more, but heads up: if there's a senior person at the table, they might insist on paying for everyone. And the polite move is to let them.
- `l-0022` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** หารกันสามคนนะ
  - **Translit:** hǎan gan sǎam khon ná
- `l-0023` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'Let's split it three ways.' สามคน is 'three people'. The นะ at the end softens it into a friendly suggestion — not a command.

Put it together with หาร and you have the full splitting move: หารกันนะ คนละสองร้อย — 'let's split it, two hundred each'. You'll use this constantly when calculating your share.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-017

- `l-0041` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** ทอน — thaawn. The change. The money you get back when you've paid more than the price. But the really useful version is ไม่ต้องทอน — 'no need for change' — which is how Thais tip a taxi driver, a motorbike rider, or a small vendor. You're saying 'keep the difference'. This is THE standard Thai tipping move. Thais don't tip in restaurants the way Americans do, but they round up like this all the time.
- `l-0042` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** ทอนเหลือยี่สิบบาทค่ะ

- `l-0043` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That means 'Twenty baht in change for you.' เหลือ means 'remaining'. A vendor might say this to you when handing back your change — listen for the number that comes after.

## [VOCAB EXPLAIN] Block b-019

- `l-0046` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** เก็บไว้เลย — gèp wái loei. Literally 'keep it'. It's the warmer, friendlier version of ไม่ต้องทอน. If your taxi was 95 baht and you hand over 100, you can say เก็บไว้เลย — keep it. The เลย at the end gives it a casual, friendly feel — like you're brushing it off, not making a big deal. Use it with taxi drivers, motorbike riders, hairdressers, food delivery riders — anyone you want to tip a small amount.
- `l-0047` 🗣️ 
  - **Thai:** ไม่เป็นไรค่ะ เก็บไว้เลย

  - **Thai:** หารกันนะ คนละสองร้อย
  - **Translit:** hǎan gan ná khon lá sǎawng-róoi
- `l-0100` 🗣️ 
  - **English:** That's right — หารกันนะ คนละสองร้อย means 'Let's split it, two hundred each.' Notice no ค่ะ — it's friend-to-friend, so the polite particle gets dropped.
