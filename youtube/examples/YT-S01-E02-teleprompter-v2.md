## PART 3 — VOCABULARY DEEP DIVE

### Section intro

> *Clear, encouraging. Look to camera.*

---

### Vocab 1: อยู่ที่ไหน (yùu thîi nǎi) — where is...?

> *This is the star of the episode. Give it extra time.*

The most important phrase today — อยู่ที่ไหน.

อยู่ที่ไหน.

This means "where is it?" or "where is...?" Let me break it down. อยู่ means "to be" or "to be located." ที่ไหน means "where." So together — อยู่ที่ไหน — "where is it located?"

The way you use it is really simple. You put the thing you're looking for at the beginning, then อยู่ที่ไหน at the end. So: ร้านกาแฟอยู่ที่ไหนคะ — "Where is the coffee shop?" ร้านกาแฟ — "coffee shop" — อยู่ที่ไหน — "where is it?" ค่ะ at the end to be polite. That's the pattern. Thing plus อยู่ที่ไหน. You can ask where anything is.

---

### Vocab 2: ตรงไป (trong bpai) — go straight

> *Point forward with your hand. Keep it physical.*

Next one — ตรงไป.

ตรงไป.

This means "go straight." ตรง means "straight" and ไป means "go." So ตรงไป — "go straight." This is usually the first direction someone gives you. When you ask where something is, the most common answer starts with ตรงไป — "go straight."

---

### Vocab 3: เลี้ยวซ้าย (líiaw sáai) — turn left

> *Gesture left clearly.*

Now — เลี้ยวซ้าย.

เลี้ยวซ้าย.

This means "turn left." เลี้ยว means "to turn" and ซ้าย means "left." เลี้ยวซ้าย — "turn left."

---

### Vocab 4: เลี้ยวขวา (líiaw khwǎa) — turn right

> *Gesture right. Pair these two together naturally.*

And the opposite — เลี้ยวขวา.

เลี้ยวขวา.

"Turn right." Same เลี้ยว — "turn" — but now ขวา — "right." เลี้ยวขวา.

Now here's a really useful combination. When someone gives you directions, they'll often say: ตรงไปแล้วเลี้ยวซ้าย — "go straight then turn left." แล้ว means "then." So ตรงไป — "go straight" — แล้ว — "then" — เลี้ยวซ้าย — "turn left." ตรงไปแล้วเลี้ยวซ้ายค่ะ. That's a complete direction.

---

### Vocab 5: ใกล้ (glâi) — near / close

> *This is where the pronunciation lesson happens. Slow down. This matters.*

OK, these next two are really interesting. And a little tricky. ใกล้.

ใกล้.

This means "near" or "close." You'll want this because after someone gives you directions, the next thing you'll want to know is — how far is it? So you ask: ใกล้ไหมคะ — "Is it near?" ไหม is a question particle that turns any statement into a yes/no question. ใกล้ไหมคะ — "Is it near?"

---

### Vocab 6: ไกล (glai) — far

> *Emphasise the tone difference. Say both words slowly, back to back. This is a key teaching moment.*

Now here's why I said "tricky." The word for "far" is ไกล.

ไกล.

Listen carefully: ใกล้... ไกล. "Near"... "far." They sound almost the same to English ears, right? But in Thai, the tone is different. ใกล้ has a falling tone — it goes down. ไกล has a mid tone — it stays flat. ใกล้... ไกล. This is a perfect example of why tones matter in Thai. If you get the tone wrong, you might say "it's near" when you mean "it's far" — and end up walking in completely the wrong direction.

Don't stress about this too much right now. Just be aware that these two words sound similar but the tone makes them different. ใกล้ — near, falling tone. ไกล — far, mid tone.

---

### Vocab 7: ห้องน้ำ (hâwng náam) — bathroom / toilet

> *Practical, essential. Everyone needs this one.*

OK, let's move to some places you might actually need to find. ห้องน้ำ.

ห้องน้ำ.

This means "bathroom" or "toilet." ห้อง means "room" and น้ำ means "water." So literally — "water room." That's the bathroom.

And now you can use our pattern: ห้องน้ำอยู่ที่ไหนคะ — "Where is the bathroom?" See? Thing plus อยู่ที่ไหน. ห้องน้ำอยู่ที่ไหนคะ. This might be the single most useful sentence you learn in Thai, honestly.

---

### Vocab 8: สถานีรถไฟฟ้า (sa-thǎa-nii rót-fai-fáa) — BTS station

> *Acknowledge this is a long word. Break it down. Make it feel achievable.*

Last one, and this one's a bit of a mouthful — สถานีรถไฟฟ้า.

สถานีรถไฟฟ้า.

OK, I know that sounds long. But let me break it down. สถานี means "station." You'll recognise it because it sounds a bit like "station" in English. Then รถไฟฟ้า — this is the fun part. รถ means "vehicle." ไฟฟ้า means "electric." So รถไฟฟ้า is literally "electric vehicle" — that's the BTS Skytrain. สถานีรถไฟฟ้า — "electric train station."

And our full sentence: สถานีรถไฟฟ้าอยู่ที่ไหนคะ — "Where is the BTS station?" That's the sentence I wish I knew my first week in Bangkok.

---

> *Quick energy, look to camera.*

OK! Those are your eight direction phrases. อยู่ที่ไหน, ตรงไป, เลี้ยวซ้าย, เลี้ยวขวา, ใกล้, ไกล, ห้องน้ำ, and สถานีรถไฟฟ้า.

## PART 5 — SENTENCE-BY-SENTENCE BREAKDOWN

### Section intro

> *Clear, instructional.*

I'm going to go through that same conversation one more time, but now sentence by sentence. I'll say the Thai slowly, explain what each part means, and then say it one more time.

---

### Sentence 1: ขอโทษค่ะ สถานีรถไฟฟ้าอยู่ที่ไหนคะ

> *Slow. Break it into two chunks.*

ขอโทษค่ะ สถานีรถไฟฟ้าอยู่ที่ไหนคะ

So this is actually two parts. First — ขอโทษค่ะ — "excuse me." This is how you politely get someone's attention. ขอโทษ literally means "I apologise" but it's used the same way as "excuse me" in English.

Then — สถานีรถไฟฟ้าอยู่ที่ไหนคะ — "Where is the BTS station?" That's our pattern — thing plus อยู่ที่ไหน. Notice that the polite particle at the end changes from ค่ะ to คะ when you're asking a question. ค่ะ with a falling tone for statements, คะ with a high tone for questions.

*(English appears: "Excuse me, where is the BTS station?")*

One more time: ขอโทษค่ะ สถานีรถไฟฟ้าอยู่ที่ไหนคะ.

---

### Sentence 2: ตรงไปแล้วเลี้ยวซ้ายค่ะ

ตรงไปแล้วเลี้ยวซ้ายค่ะ

This is the answer. ตรงไป — "go straight." แล้ว — "then." เลี้ยวซ้าย — "turn left." So: "Go straight then turn left." This is the most common type of direction you'll hear in Thailand. Straight plus a turn.

*(English appears: "Go straight then turn left.")*

ตรงไปแล้วเลี้ยวซ้ายค่ะ.

---

### Sentence 3: ใกล้ไหมคะ / ใกล้ค่ะ ห้านาทีค่ะ

ใกล้ไหมคะ

This is the follow-up question — "Is it near?" Remember, ไหม turns any statement into a question. ใกล้ — "near." ใกล้ไหม — "is it near?"

And the answer: ใกล้ค่ะ ห้านาทีค่ะ — "It's near. Five minutes." ห้า means "five." นาที means "minutes." So ห้านาที — "five minutes." Short and simple.

*(English appears: "Is it near? — It's near. Five minutes.")*

ใกล้ไหมคะ. ใกล้ค่ะ ห้านาทีค่ะ.

---

### Breakdown close

> *Look to camera.*

That's the whole conversation. Five sentences. You asked for directions, you got an answer, you confirmed how far it is. That's all you need. And notice — the grammar is simpler than English. No "is it" or "how far" — just ใกล้ไหม — "near, yes/no?"
