# Audit: E02 and E03 vs. New Episode Format

## What's missing from BOTH episodes

### 1. No section intro narration
**Problem:** Both episodes jump straight from EXPLAIN → VOCAB CARD → NATURAL LISTEN → BREAKDOWN with no English narration introducing each section.

**Fix needed:** Add `section-intro` blocks before every major section:
- Before vocab: "Let's start with the vocabulary. I'm going to go through each word one by one..."
- Before natural listen: "Now I'm going to put all of those together into a short conversation. Just listen..."
- Before breakdown: "Let's go through that again, sentence by sentence..."
- Before shadowing: "Now it's your turn. Repeat after me..."
- Before drills: "Quiz time!..."
- Before recap: "Quick recap of everything you learned..."

### 2. Vocab cards are bare flash cards
**Problem:** Each vocab item is just: Thai word → example sentence. No English explanation.

**Fix needed (per vocab item):**
- Say the Thai word twice
- 2-4 sentences of English explanation (meaning, usage, grammar position, cultural notes)
- Example sentence with English explanation of what's happening
- This should take 30-45 seconds per word, not 5 seconds

### 3. No post-listen English check-in
**Problem:** After the natural-speed listen, there's no "How was that? Don't worry if you didn't catch everything."

**Fix needed:** Add a warm encouragement block after every natural-speed listen.

### 4. Breakdown has no English grammar explanations
**Problem:** Breakdown is just Thai → transliteration → English translation appearing on screen. Nine doesn't explain the word order, grammar, or interesting features.

**Fix needed:** Each sentence in the breakdown needs 2-3 sentences of English explanation between the Thai and the translation. See E01-v2 for examples.

### 5. No drill confirmation after answers
**Problem:** Drill answers are just the Thai phrase. No English confirmation like "That's right — [phrase] means [English]."

**Fix needed:** Add brief English confirmation after each drill answer.

### 6. No closing/sign-off
**Problem:** Both episodes end abruptly with the teaser. No "thanks for watching" or sign-off.

**Fix needed:** Add a brief friendly close before สวัสดีค่ะ.

### 7. Topic intro doesn't preview the episode structure
**Problem:** The EXPLAIN blocks share a personal story (good!) but don't tell the viewer what the episode structure will be ("We'll go through each word, then hear them together, then break it down, then practise").

**Fix needed:** Add 2-3 sentences at the end of the topic intro that map out the episode.

## Episode-specific notes

### E02 — Asking for Directions

**Personal story:** Good — the Bangkok BTS story is relatable.

**Vocab items that especially need explanation:**
- **ใกล้ vs. ไกล** — These are a tonal minimal pair. Nine should explain that they look similar, sound similar, but the tone is different. This is a great teaching moment.
- **ตรงไป** — Needs hand gesture instruction and explanation that ตรง means "straight" and ไป means "go"
- **สถานีรถไฟฟ้า** — This is a long compound word. Break it down: สถานี (station) + รถไฟฟ้า (electric train). Explain that รถ = vehicle, ไฟฟ้า = electric.

**Natural listen dialogue:** Good — it's a realistic street conversation. Keep it.

**Breakdown:** Needs grammar notes, especially:
- Word order in ห้องน้ำอยู่ที่ไหน (noun + verb + question word — different from English)
- How ที่ไหน works as "where"

### E03 — Taking a Taxi

**Personal story:** Great — the taxi scam story is universally relatable for Bangkok visitors.

**Vocab items that especially need explanation:**
- **เปิดมิเตอร์** — Loanword! มิเตอร์ is from English "meter." Explain that Thai borrows lots of English words but pronounces them differently. Great cultural note.
- **เท่าไหร่** — This is THE most important question word for shopping and transport. Needs emphasis.
- **ลดได้ไหม** — Explain the ได้ไหม pattern (can + question particle = "can you?"). This is a hugely useful pattern beyond just taxis.
- **ซ้ายมือ vs. ขวามือ** — Explain that มือ literally means "hand" — left-hand side, right-hand side. Fun language insight.

**Natural listen dialogue:** Good — complete taxi ride from start to finish.

**Breakdown:** Needs explanation of:
- Why ด้วย is added to make requests polite
- How นะคะ differs from ค่ะ (softening particle vs. polite particle)
