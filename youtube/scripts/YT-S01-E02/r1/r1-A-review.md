# PART 3: Vocab Cards

### Block `b-003` -- mode: `section-intro`

> *Upbeat. You're introducing the vocabulary section now.*

Let's start with the vocabulary. Eight words — that's everything you need. For each one I'll say the Thai, explain how to use it, and give you a real example. Ready? Let's go.

### Block `b-004` -- mode: `vocab-card`

**Vocab card:** **อยู่ที่ไหน** — *yùu thîi nǎi* — where is (it)?

**อยู่ที่ไหน** — *yùu thîi nǎi*

### Block `b-005` -- mode: `vocab-explain`

อยู่ที่ไหน — yùu thîi nǎi. This means 'where is it?' or 'where is...?' อยู่ means 'to be located', and ที่ไหน means 'where'. The key thing: in Thai, the question word goes at the end. So instead of saying 'Where is the bathroom?', you say 'Bathroom — where is it?' — ห้องน้ำอยู่ที่ไหน. Just put the place first, then อยู่ที่ไหน at the end. Works for anything.

**ร้านกาแฟอยู่ที่ไหนคะ** — *ráan gaa-fae yùu thîi nǎi khá*

That means 'Where is the coffee shop?' ร้านกาแฟ is coffee shop. See how อยู่ที่ไหน comes right at the end? That's the pattern every time.

### Block `b-006` -- mode: `vocab-card`

**Vocab card:** **ตรงไป** — *trong bpai* — go straight

OK, the first direction word.

**ตรงไป** — *trong bpai*

### Block `b-007` -- mode: `vocab-explain`

ตรงไป — trong bpai. This means 'go straight'. ตรง means 'straight' and ไป means 'go'. Short, easy, you'll hear it constantly. People will often say it while pointing, so even if they say it fast you'll know what they mean. ตรงไปแล้วเลี้ยวซ้าย — 'go straight then turn left' — is probably the most common direction answer in Thailand.

**ตรงไปแล้วก็เห็นเลยค่ะ** — *trong bpai láaew gâaw hěn loei khâ*

That means 'Go straight and you'll see it right away.' เห็น is 'see', เลย means 'right there'. Very common reassurance when you're close.

### Block `b-008` -- mode: `vocab-card`

**Vocab card:** **เลี้ยวซ้าย** — *líiaw sáai* — turn left

Next — turn left.

**เลี้ยวซ้าย** — *líiaw sáai*

### Block `b-009` -- mode: `vocab-explain`

เลี้ยวซ้าย — líiaw sáai. Turn left. เลี้ยว means 'turn', ซ้าย means 'left'. Falling tone on ซ้าย — your voice drops. I always tell my students: practise ซ้าย and ขวา together as a pair, with the gesture, until they're automatic. If you get them wrong you'll go the wrong direction — which I've seen happen to many students.

**เลี้ยวซ้ายตรงสี่แยกครับ** — *líiaw sáai trong sìi-yâaek khráp*

That means 'Turn left at the intersection.' สี่แยก means 'four-way intersection' — สี่ is four, แยก is split. You'll hear this landmark constantly in directions.

### Block `b-010` -- mode: `vocab-card`

**Vocab card:** **เลี้ยวขวา** — *líiaw khwǎa* — turn right

And the opposite — turn right.

**เลี้ยวขวา** — *líiaw khwǎa*

### Block `b-011` -- mode: `vocab-explain`

เลี้ยวขวา — líiaw khwǎa. Turn right. ขวา has a rising tone — voice goes up. Compare: ซ้าย falls, ขวา rises. That tonal contrast is how you tell them apart. If you can nail those two tones you'll always go the right way. ขวา also means 'the right side' on its own — you'll see it on signs.

**เลี้ยวขวาแล้วตรงไปเลยค่ะ** — *líiaw khwǎa láaew trong bpai loei khâ*

That means 'Turn right and then just go straight.' แล้ว means 'then', เลย makes it sound natural — 'just go right ahead from there'.

### Block `b-012` -- mode: `vocab-card`

**Vocab card:** **ใกล้** — *glâi* — near / close

OK, next — near.

**ใกล้** — *glâi*

### Block `b-013` -- mode: `vocab-explain`

ใกล้ — glâi. This means 'near' or 'close'. Falling tone — voice goes down. After you get directions, the natural follow-up question is ใกล้ไหมคะ — 'Is it near?' — and if they say ใกล้ค่ะ you can walk, if not you might need a taxi. Really useful short question that Thai people use all the time.

**ใกล้แค่นี้เองค่ะ** — *glâi khâe níi eeng khâ*

That means 'It's only this close.' แค่นี้เอง is a really natural Thai expression — 'just this much'. It's the kind of thing someone says to reassure you that you haven't walked too far.

### Block `b-014` -- mode: `vocab-card`

**Vocab card:** **ไกล** — *glai* — far

And the opposite — far.

**ไกล** — *glai*

### Block `b-015` -- mode: `vocab-explain`

ไกล — glai. This means 'far'. Mid tone — flat. Compare with ใกล้ which falls. They look almost identical on paper — ใกล้ and ไกล — but the tone makes all the difference. This is one of those pairs you just have to drill. A warning: when someone says ไกลนิดหน่อย — 'a little far' — it usually means very far. Thai people are polite.

**ไกลมากไหมคะ** — *glai mâak mǎi khá*

That means 'Is it very far?' มาก adds 'very', ไหม makes it a question. Useful when you're on foot and wondering whether you need transport.

### Block `b-016` -- mode: `vocab-card`

**Vocab card:** **ห้องน้ำ** — *hâwng náam* — bathroom / toilet

Now — probably the most urgent direction question you'll ever ask.

**ห้องน้ำ** — *hâwng náam*

### Block `b-017` -- mode: `vocab-explain`

ห้องน้ำ — hâwng náam. This literally means 'water room' — ห้อง is room, น้ำ is water. It's the everyday word for bathroom or toilet. You'll see it on signs everywhere — shopping malls, restaurants, petrol stations. Just add อยู่ที่ไหน after it and you have the most-used direction question in Thailand.

**ขอถามหน่อยได้ไหมคะ ห้องน้ำอยู่แถวไหนคะ** — *khǎaw thǎam nàuy dâai mǎi khá hâwng náam yùu thǎaew nǎi khá*

That means 'Excuse me, which area is the bathroom in?' อยู่แถวไหน is a softer version — แถว means 'area around'. More casual than อยู่ที่ไหน. Both work perfectly.

### Block `b-018` -- mode: `vocab-card`

**Vocab card:** **สถานีรถไฟฟ้า** — *sa-thǎa-nii rót-fai-fáa* — BTS station

And the last one — this one is your lifeline in Bangkok.

**สถานีรถไฟฟ้า** — *sa-thǎa-nii rót-fai-fáa*

### Block `b-019` -- mode: `vocab-explain`

สถานีรถไฟฟ้า — sa-thǎa-nii rót-fai-fáa. BTS Skytrain station. Break it down: สถานี is station, รถ is vehicle, ไฟฟ้า is electric — so it's literally 'electric train station'. The BTS is the easiest way to get around Bangkok and stations are visible landmarks on every main road. If you're lost, asking for the nearest station gets you to safety fast.

**สถานีรถไฟฟ้าอยู่ใกล้ไหมคะ** — *sa-thǎa-nii rót-fai-fáa yùu glâi mǎi khá*

That means 'Is the BTS station nearby?' Notice how this combines สถานีรถไฟฟ้า, อยู่, and ใกล้ไหม — three of your eight words working together. That's exactly how it sounds in a real conversation.

### Vocab list (with example sentences)

#### v-001: **อยู่ที่ไหน** — *yùu thîi nǎi* — where is (it)?

อยู่ที่ไหน is your 'where is...?' question. อยู่ means 'to be located' or 'to live', and ที่ไหน means 'where'. Put them together after the place you're asking about — so ห้องน้ำอยู่ที่ไหน literally means 'bathroom is located where?' In Thai, the question word always comes at the end, not the beginning like in English. You'll use this one constantly.

- **ร้านกาแฟอยู่ที่ไหนคะ** — *ráan gaa-fae yùu thîi nǎi khá*
  - *Translation:* Where is the coffee shop?
  - *Note:* Same pattern for any place — just say the place, then อยู่ที่ไหน at the end. คะ is the polite particle for women.

#### v-002: **ตรงไป** — *trong bpai* — go straight

ตรงไป means 'go straight'. ตรง means 'straight' or 'direct', and ไป means 'go'. It's short, clear, and works alone as a full direction — locals will often just say ตรงไป while pointing. You'll hear this most when you ask for directions and the answer is simple.

- **ตรงไปแล้วก็เห็นเลยค่ะ** — *trong bpai láaew gâaw hěn loei khâ*
  - *Translation:* Go straight and you'll see it right away.
  - *Note:* แล้วก็ links two actions — 'then'. เห็นเลย means 'you can see it right there'. A really natural direction answer.

#### v-003: **เลี้ยวซ้าย** — *líiaw sáai* — turn left

เลี้ยวซ้าย means 'turn left'. เลี้ยว means 'turn' and ซ้าย means 'left'. These two words almost always go together. An important point: in Thai, ซ้าย (left) and ขวา (right) are easy to confuse because the tones are close — ซ้าย has a falling tone, ขวา has a rising tone. Pair them with the gesture every time you say them until it's automatic.

- **เลี้ยวซ้ายตรงสี่แยกครับ** — *líiaw sáai trong sìi-yâaek khráp*
  - *Translation:* Turn left at the intersection.
  - *Note:* สี่แยก means 'four-way intersection' — a very common landmark in direction-giving.

#### v-004: **เลี้ยวขวา** — *líiaw khwǎa* — turn right

เลี้ยวขวา means 'turn right'. ขวา has a rising tone — your voice goes up. Compare this with ซ้าย which falls. This tone difference is how Thai people tell them apart instantly. If you get the tones wrong you could end up going the wrong direction, so it's worth drilling these two as a pair until they're solid.

- **เลี้ยวขวาแล้วตรงไปเลยค่ะ** — *líiaw khwǎa láaew trong bpai loei khâ*
  - *Translation:* Turn right and then just go straight.
  - *Note:* แล้ว here means 'then' or 'after that'. เลย intensifies it — 'just go straight on from there'.

#### v-005: **ใกล้** — *glâi* — near / close

ใกล้ means 'near' or 'close'. It has a falling tone — your voice drops. This is one of those words you'll want as a yes/no check: ใกล้ไหม means 'is it near?' and the answer can simply be ใกล้ค่ะ — 'it's close'. You'll also hear ใกล้มาก meaning 'very close' when someone wants to reassure you.

- **ใกล้แค่นี้เองค่ะ** — *glâi khâe níi eeng khâ*
  - *Translation:* It's only this close.
  - *Note:* แค่นี้เอง is a very natural phrase — it means 'just this much' or 'only this close'. Thai people use it to reassure someone that something isn't far.

#### v-006: **ไกล** — *glai* — far

ไกล means 'far'. Notice how similar ใกล้ (near) and ไกล (far) look in writing — they differ by just one vowel mark and the tone. ใกล้ has a falling tone, ไกล has a mid tone. This is a classic Thai pair that trips up beginners, so always practise them together. If someone says ไกลนิดหน่อย it means 'a little far' — that usually means quite far.

- **ไกลมากไหมคะ** — *glai mâak mǎi khá*
  - *Translation:* Is it very far?
  - *Note:* มาก adds intensity — 'very far'. ไหม makes it a yes/no question. Useful to check whether you need a taxi or can walk.

#### v-007: **ห้องน้ำ** — *hâwng náam* — bathroom / toilet

ห้องน้ำ literally means 'water room' — ห้อง means 'room' and น้ำ means 'water'. This is the everyday word for bathroom or toilet in Thai. You'll see it on signs everywhere. If you're in a restaurant or a shopping mall, ห้องน้ำอยู่ที่ไหน is probably the most-used direction question of all. Learn it first.

- **ขอถามหน่อยได้ไหมคะ ห้องน้ำอยู่แถวไหนคะ** — *khǎaw thǎam nàuy dâai mǎi khá hâwng náam yùu thǎaew nǎi khá*
  - *Translation:* Excuse me, which area is the bathroom in?
  - *Note:* อยู่แถวไหน is a softer way to ask — แถว means 'area around' or 'in the vicinity of'. More casual than อยู่ที่ไหน.

#### v-008: **สถานีรถไฟฟ้า** — *sa-thǎa-nii rót-fai-fáa* — BTS station

สถานีรถไฟฟ้า means 'BTS Skytrain station'. สถานี means 'station', รถ means 'vehicle', ไฟฟ้า means 'electric'. So it's literally 'electric train station'. Thais often shorten it to just สถานี or even call specific stations by their name. If you're lost in Bangkok this is probably the first direction question you'll need — BTS stations are visible landmarks everywhere.

- **สถานีรถไฟฟ้าอยู่ใกล้ไหมคะ** — *sa-thǎa-nii rót-fai-fáa yùu glâi mǎi khá*
  - *Translation:* Is the BTS station nearby?
  - *Note:* Combining two vocab items — อยู่ที่ไหน pattern with ใกล้ไหม. This is exactly how they'd be used together in real life.

ขอโทษค่ะ is 'excuse me' — a polite opener before asking a stranger anything. Then สถานีรถไฟฟ้า is the BTS station. อยู่ที่ไหน — where is it? คะ is the soft polite particle for women. Notice how the question word ที่ไหน comes right at the end — place first, then 'where' at the end. That's the rule.

### Block `b-026` -- mode: `breakdown`

> *Switch voice — this is the helper replying.*

**ตรงไปแล้วเลี้ยวซ้ายค่ะ** — *trong bpai láaew líiaw sáai khâ*

*trong bpai láaew líiaw sáai khâ*

Go straight then turn left.

### Block `b-027` -- mode: `explain`

ตรงไป — go straight. แล้ว — then. เลี้ยวซ้าย — turn left. The structure in Thai is the same as English here: action, then next action. แล้ว is your 'then' connector. Very simple, very logical.

### Block `b-028` -- mode: `breakdown`

**ใกล้ไหมคะ** — *glâi mǎi khá*

*glâi mǎi khá*

Is it near?

### Block `b-029` -- mode: `explain`

ใกล้ is 'near'. ไหม turns it into a yes/no question — it's the Thai question particle. So ใกล้ไหม is literally 'near?' — that's all you need. Super short, completely natural.

### Block `b-030` -- mode: `breakdown`

**ใกล้ค่ะ ห้านาทีค่ะ** — *glâi khâ hâa naa-thii khâ*

*glâi khâ hâa naa-thii khâ*

It's close. Five minutes.

### Block `b-031` -- mode: `explain`

ใกล้ค่ะ — it's close. ห้า is five, นาที is minutes. Thais often answer distance questions in walking time rather than metres — which is actually more useful. ห้านาที means you can walk. ยี่สิบนาที means get a taxi.
