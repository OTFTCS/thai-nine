# Thai with Nine — YouTube Series Strategy

## Research source: "You Too Can Learn Thai" by Khru Nan

Based on analysis of the full episode catalogue (280+ episodes), transcripts, FAQs, and format structure of the "You Too Can Learn Thai" podcast by Khru Nan (แนน) — a native Thai speaker with a PhD in linguistics.

---

## 1. What makes "You Too Can Learn Thai" work

### The creator
Khru Nan is a linguistics PhD, university professor, and published author. She's a native Thai speaker based partly in the UK (references to London life appear throughout later episodes). The academic credibility combined with a warm, personal tone is a big part of the appeal.

### Core format (audio-first, adapted to video)
The podcast was built for audio but translates well to video because of its layered repetition structure. Each episode delivers the same content multiple times in different modes:

1. **Natural speed listen** — full Thai paragraph or dialogue, no pauses
2. **Slow speed listen** — same content, spoken slowly for comprehension
3. **Sentence-by-sentence with English translation** — Thai sentence, then English meaning
4. **Shadowing practice** — slow speed again, with pauses for the listener to repeat
5. **Vocabulary/grammar explanation** — English explanations of key words and patterns

Not every episode uses all five passes, but the principle of "hear it multiple times in different ways" is consistent throughout.

### Episode types (identified from the 280+ episode catalogue)

| Type | Example episodes | Structure |
|---|---|---|
| **Listening practice (levelled)** | EP 1, 2, 7, 40 | Thai paragraphs at different speeds + translation |
| **Short stories** | EP 3, 29, 71-72 | Narrative in Thai → slow → sentence-by-sentence |
| **Practical phrases** | EP 4, 11, 12, 15, 19 | Key phrases → explanation → dialogue examples |
| **Grammar focus** | EP 16-17, 24-25, 101 | Grammar point → examples → exercises |
| **Topic monologue** | EP 49-53, 54-58, 151+ | Nan talks about a topic in Thai (hobbies, food, travel) |
| **Interview/conversation** | EP 36, 41, 44-45 | Nan interviews a Thai learner or friend in Thai |
| **Cultural explainer** | EP 46-47, 111-112 | Thai culture, loanwords, festivals explained bilingually |
| **Word-building** | EP 21-23, 31 | Morpheme or root word → compound words → usage |

### Content layers per episode (later episodes, EP 151+)
Later episodes evolved to a clean three-layer format per paragraph:

1. **Natural Thai script** (unsplit) — for intermediate/advanced readers
2. **Split-word Thai** (spaces between words) — for beginners learning to read
3. **Thai + English sentence-by-sentence** — for comprehension check

This triple-layer approach is brilliant for YouTube subtitles because you can show different layers as different subtitle tracks or on-screen text.

### Thematic series structure
Episodes are grouped into multi-part series around a single theme:

- EP 49-53: Hobbies (5 episodes)
- EP 54-58: Western and Thai breakfast (5 episodes)
- EP 83-88: Seasons (summer, rain, winter — 6 episodes)
- EP 151-160: Travel series (airport, shuttle, sightseeing, souvenirs, etc.)
- EP 161-170: Supermarket series (cleaning, food delivery, shopping, products)
- EP 171-175: London series
- EP 191-195: Personal finance (budgeting, savings, taxes, credit, emergency fund)
- EP 211-215: Regional Thai food
- EP 216-220: Museums
- EP 221-225: Rainy season
- EP 226-230: Home maintenance
- EP 231-235: Growing vegetables
- EP 261-270: Time units (one second → one hundred years)
- EP 276-283: Nature (games, trees, rivers, mountains)

The 5-episode mini-series format is ideal for YouTube playlists and creates a natural "next episode" pull.

### Tone and personality
Nan speaks in first person, shares personal anecdotes (her hobbies, her London experiences, her preferences), and directly addresses the listener as a friend. The content feels like a conversation, not a lecture. She often says things like "เพื่อน ๆ รู้ไหมคะว่าคืออะไร" (Do you all know what it is?) to create engagement.

## 2. What "Thai with Nine" can learn and differentiate

### What to replicate
- **Multi-pass repetition**: present each chunk at natural speed, slow speed, and with translation
- **Topic-based mini-series**: 3-5 episodes per theme for playlist momentum
- **Personal storytelling**: Nine talks about her life, Thailand, relatable topics
- **Bilingual scaffolding**: Thai first, English support — not the other way around
- **Practical, everyday topics**: food, travel, shopping, health, work — these perform best
- **Consistent episode structure**: listeners know what to expect each time

### Where to differentiate (our advantages)
- **Video-native with animation**: Khru Nan's content is audio-first. Ours can be built for the screen from day one with animated vocabulary cards, scene illustrations, and visual grammar breakdowns
- **Subtitles as a core feature**: Thai script + transliteration + English as togglable subtitle layers, or burned-in dual subtitles
- **Nine's teaching style**: 1:1 conversational feel, production drills, pause-and-produce prompts — more interactive than Nan's listen-and-absorb model
- **Structured curriculum**: We have 180 lessons across A0-B2 mapped out. Nan's episodes are loosely progressive. We can explicitly label levels and learning outcomes
- **Short-form repurposing**: Design every long-form video to yield 3-5 short-form clips (TikTok/Reels/Shorts)
- **AI-generated visuals**: Watercolour illustrations, Manim animations for grammar, vocabulary image carousels — visual richness Nan's audio-first approach doesn't have

## 3. Proposed YouTube series format

### Series name
**"Thai with Nine"** (consistent with existing brand)

### Episode structure (target: 8-15 minutes long-form)

| Segment | Duration | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | 0:00-0:30 | Quick Thai phrase or question with on-screen text + translation. Designed to also work as a standalone Short |
| **Topic intro** | 0:30-1:30 | Nine introduces today's topic in simple Thai with English subtitles. Personal anecdote or cultural context |
| **Vocabulary block** | 1:30-3:30 | 5-8 key words/phrases with animated cards: Thai script → transliteration → English → example sentence. Each word shown visually (watercolour illustration or icon) |
| **Natural speed listen** | 3:30-5:00 | Nine delivers a paragraph or dialogue in natural Thai. Subtitles: Thai script only. Viewer tests their comprehension |
| **Breakdown** | 5:00-8:00 | Same paragraph, sentence by sentence. Thai on screen, transliteration below, English translation appears after a pause. Grammar notes pop up for key patterns |
| **Production drill** | 8:00-10:00 | Nine prompts the viewer to respond in Thai. Pause. Then Nine gives the model answer. 3-4 prompts per episode |
| **Shadowing round** | 10:00-12:00 | Slow-speed repeat of the full paragraph. On-screen: split-word Thai. Viewer shadows along |
| **Recap + teaser** | 12:00-13:00 | Quick vocab review (flash cards), preview of next episode in the series |

### Visual design
- **16:9 landscape** for YouTube, with safe zones for 9:16 crop (Shorts)
- **Nine in PiP** (picture-in-picture) or half-screen when speaking to camera
- **Animated text overlays**: Thai script large and centred, transliteration in smaller font below, English translation fades in
- **Watercolour scene illustrations** for topic context (food, market, travel scenes)
- **Manim-style animations** for grammar breakdowns (sentence diagrams, word order, tone charts)
- **Consistent colour palette**: match existing Thai with Nine brand colours

### Subtitle strategy
- **Burned-in**: Thai script + English translation (two lines)
- **YouTube CC tracks**: separate tracks for Thai-only, English-only, and transliteration
- **Short-form clips**: larger text, single-language per clip (Thai with English burned-in)

## 4. Long-form → Short-form repurposing plan

Every long-form episode should yield **3-5 short-form clips** (30-90 seconds each):

| Clip type | Source segment | Format |
|---|---|---|
| **"How to say X in Thai"** | Hook + 1 vocab item | 15-30s, vertical, big text |
| **"Thai phrase of the day"** | Vocabulary block, single phrase | 30-45s, animated card + Nine saying it |
| **"Can you understand this?"** | Natural speed listen (shortened) | 30-60s, Thai audio + reveal translation |
| **"Repeat after Nine"** | Shadowing round, 2-3 sentences | 45-60s, practice format |
| **"Thai culture moment"** | Topic intro, cultural anecdote | 30-60s, storytelling clip |
| **Grammar hack** | Breakdown segment, single pattern | 45-90s, animated grammar visual |

### Short-form optimisation
- Film in both 16:9 and 9:16, or use safe-zone cropping
- Every clip should have a text hook in the first 1 second
- Thai script visible on screen at all times (pattern recognition even for beginners)
- End each clip with a question or CTA ("Try saying this — comment your attempt!")

## 5. Thematic series plan (first 6 series)

Based on what performed well in Khru Nan's catalogue and what aligns with our curriculum:

### Series 1: "Thai Survival Phrases" (5 episodes)
Ordering food, asking for directions, taxi/Grab, shopping, saying thank you / sorry. Maps to M01-M02 content.

### Series 2: "Thai Food Adventures" (5 episodes)
Street food vocabulary, restaurant ordering, breakfast culture, spicy food, Thai desserts. Maps to food-related lessons.

### Series 3: "Bangkok Life" (5 episodes)
Transport, markets, temples, nightlife vocabulary, weather. Cultural immersion series.

### Series 4: "Thai Grammar Unlocked" (5 episodes)
Classifiers, ถ้า (if), conjunctions, question particles, tenses. Animated grammar breakdowns.

### Series 5: "Thai Conversations" (5 episodes)
Meeting someone, compliments, small talk, making plans, saying goodbye. Dialogue-heavy with role-play.

### Series 6: "Thai Culture Deep Dives" (5 episodes)
Festivals, Thai TV shows, Thai-ified English words, proverbs, regional differences.

## 6. Production pipeline

### Per-episode workflow
1. **Script** — write Thai paragraph(s), English translations, vocab list, drill prompts (from course blueprint or new topic)
2. **Record Nine** — film Nine delivering all segments (PiP and direct-to-camera)
3. **Generate visuals** — AI watercolour illustrations for topic, animated vocab cards
4. **Animate grammar** — Manim or motion graphics for grammar breakdowns
5. **Edit long-form** — assemble 8-15 min YouTube video with all segments, subtitles, animations
6. **Cut short-form** — extract 3-5 clips from long-form, reformat for 9:16
7. **Subtitle pass** — burn in Thai + English for long-form, create CC tracks, format short-form text
8. **Publish** — YouTube (long-form) → TikTok / Reels / Shorts (short-form) → podcast audio extract

### Tools
- **Video**: DaVinci Resolve or CapCut for editing
- **Animation**: Manim (already in TikTok pipeline), After Effects for polish
- **AI images**: Gemini/DALL-E for watercolour illustrations (existing pipeline)
- **Subtitles**: YouTube Studio for CC, CapCut for burned-in
- **Slides**: Google Slides pipeline (existing) for supplementary materials

## 7. Key metrics to track

- **Watch time and retention curve** — especially drop-off at each segment transition
- **Shorts → Long-form conversion** — are shorts driving subscribers to the channel?
- **Comment engagement** — especially on production drill segments ("comment your answer")
- **Playlist completion rate** — are viewers watching full 5-episode series?
- **Subtitle track usage** — which language tracks are most used?

## 8. Lessons from Khru Nan's content evolution

1. **Start simple, get richer**: Early episodes (1-30) were short phrase lists. Later episodes (150+) became rich topic monologues with triple-layer text. Don't over-produce episode 1 — find the format, then polish.

2. **Personal stories win**: Nan's hobby episodes, London episodes, and breakfast episodes feel the most engaging because she shares herself. Nine should do the same — talk about her life, Thailand, her perspective.

3. **Series create habit**: The 5-episode thematic arcs create a natural "I need to watch the next one" pull. Always end with a teaser.

4. **Vocabulary in context, not in lists**: Nan rarely does pure vocab lists. Words are always embedded in stories or situations. Our animated vocab cards should always show the word in a sentence.

5. **The split-word technique is gold**: Nan's innovation of showing Thai text with spaces between words is a massive aid for reading practice. We should include this as an on-screen mode.

6. **Biweekly is sustainable**: Nan publishes biweekly and has maintained 280+ episodes over several years. For video (higher production cost), biweekly or weekly is realistic.

7. **Romanisation helps but shouldn't dominate**: Nan uses romanisation in earlier episodes but the later ones lean more on Thai script + English translation. Our transliteration (PTM standard) should be available but not the primary on-screen text.
