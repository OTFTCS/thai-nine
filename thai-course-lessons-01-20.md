# Thai Course — Lessons 01–20 (MVP)

Covers Module 0 (Onboarding), Module 1 (Script Fundamentals), Module 2 (Survival Thai), and Module 3 intro (Sentence Building).

Each lesson targets 12–18 min and follows the standard template: Hook → Core Input → Pattern Breakdown → Guided Practice → Micro-Quiz → Recap + CTA.

---

## Module 0 — Onboarding + Pronunciation Prep

### Lesson 01: How Thai Works
- **Objective:** Understand what makes Thai different (tones, no conjugation, no spaces) and set realistic expectations.
- **Key points:** Thai is a tone language; 5 tones change meaning; script is an alphabet (not pictographic); word order matters more than endings.
- **Vocab:** สวัสดี (sà-wàt-dii), ขอบคุณ (khàwp-khun), ครับ/ค่ะ (khráp/khâ)
- **Practice:** Identify tone differences in 3 minimal pairs (audio).

### Lesson 02: The Thai Sound System
- **Objective:** Hear and distinguish Thai consonant sounds, vowels, and the 5 tones.
- **Key points:** 44 consonants map to 21 sounds; vowels come in short/long pairs; tones = mid, low, falling, high, rising.
- **Vocab:** ไก่ (gài), ไข่ (khài), ใกล้ (glâi), ไกล (glai)
- **Practice:** Tone listening drill — match audio to tone name (5 items).

### Lesson 03: How to Use This Course
- **Objective:** Learn the study cadence, quiz system, and how to get the most from each lesson.
- **Key points:** Watch → practice → quiz → review weak items; aim for 3–4 lessons/week; use quizzes honestly (no skip).
- **Vocab:** เรียน (riian), ฝึก (fùek), ทบทวน (thóp-thuan)
- **Practice:** Set a personal study schedule (worksheet prompt).

## Module 1 — Thai Script Fundamentals

### Lesson 04: Consonant Classes — Mid Class
- **Objective:** Recognize and pronounce the 9 mid-class consonants.
- **Key points:** Mid class = simplest tone rules; memorize these first. ก จ ด ต บ ป อ ฎ ฏ
- **Vocab:** กา (gaa), จาน (jaan), ตา (taa), บ้าน (bâan)
- **Practice:** Read 8 mid-class syllables aloud; match consonant to sound.

### Lesson 05: Consonant Classes — High & Low
- **Objective:** Distinguish high-class and low-class consonants and understand why the class matters for tones.
- **Key points:** High class (11): ข ฃ ฉ ฐ ถ ผ ฝ ศ ษ ส ห; Low class (24): remaining. Class determines default tone of a syllable.
- **Vocab:** ขา (khǎa), คา (khaa), ผม (phǒm), พ่อ (phâw)
- **Practice:** Sort 10 consonants into high/mid/low columns.

### Lesson 06: Core Vowels — Short & Long
- **Objective:** Read and pronounce the 9 essential vowel pairs (short + long).
- **Key points:** Short/long changes meaning; vowels can appear above, below, before, or after the consonant. Core pairs: -ะ/-า, -ิ/-ี, -ุ/-ู, เ-ะ/เ-, แ-ะ/แ-, โ-ะ/โ-, -ือ/-ือ
- **Vocab:** มา (maa), มะ (má), ดี (dii), ดิ (dì — colloquial)
- **Practice:** Pair-matching drill — hear short vs long and select correct written form.

### Lesson 07: Tone Rules Basics
- **Objective:** Apply basic tone rules to read a syllable with the correct tone.
- **Key points:** Default tone depends on consonant class + syllable type (live/dead) + vowel length. Tone marks override default.
- **Vocab:** น้ำ (náam), หมา (mǎa), ปลา (plaa), ก้อน (gâwn)
- **Practice:** Read 6 syllables and predict tone before hearing answer.

### Lesson 08: Final Consonants + Live/Dead Syllables
- **Objective:** Understand which final consonant sounds exist and how they create live vs dead syllables.
- **Key points:** Only 8 final sounds: -k, -t, -p, -n, -m, -ng, -y, -w. Dead syllables end in stop (-k, -t, -p) or have short vowel + no final.
- **Vocab:** มาก (mâak), จบ (jòp), กิน (gin), ลม (lom)
- **Practice:** Label 8 words as live or dead; read aloud.

### Lesson 09: Reading Drills — Syllables to Words
- **Objective:** Combine consonant + vowel + tone + final into real words fluently.
- **Key points:** Sound-out strategy: identify class → vowel → final → determine tone → say it. Speed comes from repetition.
- **Vocab:** ประเทศ (prà-thêet), สนุก (sà-nùk), อร่อย (à-ràwy), ภาษา (phaa-sǎa)
- **Practice:** Timed reading — 10 common words, progressive speed.

## Module 2 — Survival Thai

### Lesson 10: Greetings & Politeness Particles
- **Objective:** Greet, introduce yourself, and use ครับ/ค่ะ correctly.
- **Key points:** สวัสดี + particle; ชื่อ…(name); ยินดีที่ได้รู้จัก; when to use ครับ vs ค่ะ vs คะ.
- **Vocab:** สวัสดี, ชื่อ (chûue), ยินดี (yin-dii), ครับ, ค่ะ, คะ
- **Practice:** Introduce yourself in Thai (spoken prompt + pause).

### Lesson 11: Numbers 0–100 & Counting
- **Objective:** Count, say prices, and understand numbers in context.
- **Key points:** 0–10 base; teens pattern (สิบ + unit); tens pattern (unit + สิบ); บาท for money.
- **Vocab:** หนึ่ง–สิบ, ยี่สิบ (yîi-sìp = 20 exception), ร้อย (ráwy), บาท (bàat)
- **Practice:** Listen to 5 prices and write the number.

### Lesson 12: Time, Days & Simple Dates
- **Objective:** Tell time, name days of the week, and state simple dates.
- **Key points:** …โมง for o'clock; วัน + day name; วันที่ + number for dates.
- **Vocab:** วันจันทร์–วันอาทิตย์, โมง (moong), วันนี้ (wan-níi), พรุ่งนี้ (phrûng-níi)
- **Practice:** Answer "วันนี้วันอะไร?" and "กี่โมง?" prompts.

### Lesson 13: Asking & Answering Simple Questions
- **Objective:** Form yes/no questions with ไหม and wh-questions with อะไร/ที่ไหน/เท่าไหร่.
- **Key points:** Statement + ไหม = yes/no question; question word usually goes where the answer would be.
- **Vocab:** ไหม (mǎi), อะไร (à-rai), ที่ไหน (thîi-nǎi), เท่าไหร่ (thâo-rài), ทำไม (tham-mai)
- **Practice:** Convert 5 statements into questions; answer 3 audio questions.

### Lesson 14: Ordering Food & Drinks
- **Objective:** Order at a restaurant or street stall confidently.
- **Key points:** ขอ + item + quantity; เอา…(I'll have…); ไม่เผ็ด/เผ็ดนิดหน่อย; เช็คบิล.
- **Vocab:** ขอ (khǎw), ข้าว (khâaw), น้ำ (náam), ไม่ (mâi), เผ็ด (phèt), อร่อย (à-ràwy)
- **Practice:** Roleplay — order a dish, specify spice level, ask for the bill.

### Lesson 15: Paying, Prices & Basic Transactions
- **Objective:** Handle money exchanges, ask prices, and understand receipts.
- **Key points:** เท่าไหร่ครับ/คะ; แพงไป = too expensive; ลดได้ไหม = can you reduce?; จ่าย = pay.
- **Vocab:** เท่าไหร่, แพง (phaeng), ถูก (thùuk), จ่าย (jàai), เงิน (ngoen)
- **Practice:** Listen to 4 price dialogues and identify the final price.

### Lesson 16: Getting Around — Directions & Transport
- **Objective:** Give/understand basic directions and use transport vocab.
- **Key points:** ไปทางไหน; ซ้าย/ขวา/ตรงไป; BTS/รถเมล์/แท็กซี่; ไปที่…
- **Vocab:** ไป (pai), ซ้าย (sáai), ขวา (khwǎa), ตรงไป (trong-pai), ใกล้ (glâi), ไกล (glai)
- **Practice:** Follow audio directions on a simple map; give directions to a landmark.

## Module 3 — Sentence Building Essentials (Intro)

### Lesson 17: Thai Word Order Basics
- **Objective:** Build correct simple sentences using Thai SVO + modifier order.
- **Key points:** Subject + Verb + Object; adjectives follow nouns; adverbs follow verbs. No articles.
- **Vocab:** กิน (gin), ไป (pai), ชอบ (châwp), อาหาร (aa-hǎan), เก่ง (gèng)
- **Practice:** Arrange scrambled words into correct sentences (5 items).

### Lesson 18: Pronouns & When to Drop Them
- **Objective:** Use Thai pronouns appropriately and know when omission is natural.
- **Key points:** ผม/ฉัน/คุณ/เขา; Thai drops pronouns when context is clear; nickname > pronoun in casual speech.
- **Vocab:** ผม (phǒm), ฉัน (chǎn), คุณ (khun), เขา (khǎo), เรา (rao)
- **Practice:** Identify which sentences sound more natural with or without pronouns.

### Lesson 19: Verbs in Context — No Conjugation Needed
- **Objective:** Use verbs in past, present, and future without conjugation stress.
- **Key points:** Thai verbs don't change form; time is shown by context + markers: แล้ว (already), กำลัง (currently), จะ (will).
- **Vocab:** แล้ว (láew), กำลัง (gam-lang), จะ (jà), เคย (khoei), ยัง (yang)
- **Practice:** Add the correct time marker to 5 sentences.

### Lesson 20: Negation & Simple Questions Review
- **Objective:** Negate any sentence with ไม่ and reinforce question patterns from Lesson 13.
- **Key points:** ไม่ + verb = negation; ไม่ได้ for "didn't"; ไม่ใช่ for "is not (noun)". Review: ไหม, ใช่ไหม, หรือเปล่า.
- **Vocab:** ไม่ (mâi), ไม่ได้ (mâi dâai), ไม่ใช่ (mâi châi), ใช่ไหม (châi mǎi), หรือเปล่า (rǔue plào)
- **Practice:** Negate 5 affirmative sentences; convert 3 statements to tag questions.
