Episode 01 - Why Thai Says "Water Two Bottles"

Target runtime: 110-140 seconds
Goal: show viewers that literal strange-English glosses help them remember Thai classifier order

Full script:

ขอ 2 น้ำเปล่า | khǎaw 2 náam bplào | ask 2 plain waters | please can I have 2 waters
X wrong.

ผมชอบหนังสือนี้ | phǒm châwp nǎng-sěuu níi | I like book this | I like this book
X wrong.

มันเป็นหมาใหญ่มาก | man bpen mǎa yài mâak | it is dog big very | that's a big dog
X wrong.

What was wrong with these sentences?

They all sound like direct translations from English.

Thai wants a classifier in all three.

ขอน้ำเปล่าสองขวด | khǎaw náam bplào sǎawng khùat | ask plain water two bottles | please can I have 2 bottles of water

ผมชอบหนังสือเล่มนี้ | phǒm châwp nǎng-sěuu lèm níi | I like book volume this | I like this book

มันเป็นหมาตัวใหญ่มาก | man bpen mǎa dtua yài mâak | it is dog body big very | that's a very big dog

That weird English is the memory trick.

If you remember the order, Thai gets easier.

เห็นตั๋วสามใบ | hěn dtǔua sǎam bai | see tickets three flat-things | I see three tickets

โทรศัพท์เครื่องนั้น | thoo-rá-sàp khrêuang nán | phone machine that | that phone

กาแฟแก้วใหญ่ | gaa-faae gâaeo yài | coffee glass big | a big coffee

รถคันนี้ | rót khan níi | car vehicle this | this car

So classifiers show up when you count, when you point, and when you describe.

This is the shortcut.

Do not only memorize the meaning.

Memorize the order.

Ticket three flat-things.

Phone machine that.

Coffee glass big.

Car vehicle this.

Quick check.

เห็นปลาสองตัว | hěn bplaa sǎawng dtua | see fish two bodies | I see two fish

Transfer check.

กาแฟแก้วนี้ | gaa-faae gâaeo níi | coffee glass this | this coffee
