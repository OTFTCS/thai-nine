Episode 02 - คน vs ตัว

Target runtime: 70-100 seconds
Goal: make the people-vs-animal split feel automatic through performed glosses

Full script:

[Flash on screen fast, no explanation yet]

**นักเรียนหนึ่งคน** | nák-riian nùeng khon | student one PERSON | one student
**แมวหนึ่งตัว** | maaew nùeng dtua | cat one BODY | one cat

[To camera]

**One student. One cat. Different word at the end. Why?**

**[PERFORM] "Student one PERSON."**
**[PERFORM] "Cat one BODY."**

**Thai splits people and animals with two different classifiers.**

**คน for people. ตัว for animals.**

**คน -- say "con," flat even tone. ตัว -- say "dtua," flat tone, starts with a D-T combo.**

**หมอสองคน** | mǎaw sǎawng khon | doctor two PEOPLE | two doctors
**[PERFORM] "Doctor two PEOPLE." Makes sense.**

**But it's not just for counting. You also use it to point at someone.**

**หมอคนนั้น** | mǎaw khon nán | doctor PERSON that | that doctor
**[PERFORM] "Doctor PERSON that."**

**เป็ดหนึ่งตัว** | bpèt nùeng dtua | duck one BODY | one duck
**[PERFORM] "Duck one BODY." Weird, but that's the trick.**

**And you use it to describe things too.**

**ช้างตัวใหญ่** | cháang dtua yài | elephant BODY big | big elephant
**[PERFORM] "Elephant BODY big."**

**นกสองตัว** | nók sǎawng dtua | bird two BODIES | two birds
**[PERFORM] "Bird two BODIES."**

**Now here's the fun one.**

**ตุ๊กตาหนึ่งตัว** | dtúk-dtaa nùeng dtua | doll one BODY | one doll
**[PERFORM] "Doll one BODY." Yeah. Thai thinks dolls have bodies.**

**ตัว also covers insects, fish, clothing, and even fictional characters. Basically -- if it has a body shape.**

[MINI-SCENE: Pointing at animals at a market or zoo]

**You're at the market. You point at a cat.**

**แมวตัวนี้** | maaew dtua níi | cat BODY this | this cat
**[PERFORM] "Cat BODY this."**

**You see three ducks.**

**เห็นเป็ดสามตัว** | hěn bpèt sǎam dtua | see duck three BODIES | I see three ducks
**[PERFORM] "See duck three BODIES."**

Human? คน.
Has a body? ตัว.

[PAUSE 3s — "YOUR TURN" on screen]

**Quick check. "One horse"?**

[Reveal]
**ม้าหนึ่งตัว** | máa nùeng dtua | horse one BODY | one horse
**[PERFORM] "Horse one BODY."**

[PAUSE 3s — "YOUR TURN" on screen]

**Transfer check. New noun. "One friend"?**

[Reveal]
**เพื่อนหนึ่งคน** | phêuuan nùeng khon | friend one PERSON | one friend
**[PERFORM] "Friend one PERSON."**

**So คน is people, ตัว is animals. But what do you use for everything else?**

**Duet this and say the weird English before I show the answer.**
