Episode 07 - แก้ว vs ขวด

Target runtime: 70-100 seconds
Goal: finish the arc with a practical cafe/shop use case and performed glosses

Full script:

[Pour water into a glass. Then hold up a bottle of water.]

**Same water. Watch.**

**น้ำหนึ่งแก้ว** | náam nùeng gâaeo | water one GLASS | one glass of water
**[PERFORM] "Water one GLASS."**

**น้ำหนึ่งขวด** | náam nùeng khùat | water one BOTTLE | one bottle of water
**[PERFORM] "Water one BOTTLE."**

**The noun is identical. The classifier changes because the container changes.**

**แก้ว is for a glass or cup serving. ขวด is for a bottle.**

**แก้ว -- sounds like "gaaeo," rhymes with "wow" with a G. Falling tone. ขวด -- say "koo-at" and drop it down. Also falling.**

**กาแฟหนึ่งแก้ว** | gaa-faae nùeng gâaeo | coffee one GLASS | one coffee
**[PERFORM] "Coffee one GLASS."**

**ชาไทยหนึ่งแก้ว** | chaa-thai nùeng gâaeo | Thai-tea one GLASS | one Thai tea
**[PERFORM] "Thai-tea one GLASS." You're at a cafe, this is what you say.**

**โค้กหนึ่งขวด** | khóok nùeng khùat | Coke one BOTTLE | one bottle of Coke
**[PERFORM] "Coke one BOTTLE."**

**น้ำผลไม้หนึ่งขวด** | náam phǒn-lá-mái nùeng khùat | fruit-juice one BOTTLE | one bottle of juice
**[PERFORM] "Fruit-juice one BOTTLE."**

**And for pointing and describing:**

**ชาเย็นแก้วนี้** | chaa yen gâaeo níi | iced-tea GLASS this | this iced tea
**[PERFORM] "Iced-tea GLASS this."**

**ชาเย็นแก้วใหญ่** | chaa yen gâaeo yài | iced-tea GLASS big | big iced tea
**[PERFORM] "Iced-tea GLASS big."**

[MINI-SCENE: Ordering at a Thai cafe]

**You walk into a cafe. You want one iced coffee.**

**ขอกาแฟเย็นหนึ่งแก้ว** | khǎaw gaa-faae yen nùeng gâaeo | ask iced-coffee one GLASS | one iced coffee please
**[PERFORM] "Ask iced-coffee one GLASS."**

**And one bottle of water.**

**กับน้ำหนึ่งขวด** | gàp náam nùeng khùat | with water one BOTTLE | and one bottle of water
**[PERFORM] "With water one BOTTLE."**

**แก้ว covers any cup or glass serving -- iced, hot, blended. ขวด is any bottled liquid -- water, beer, soy sauce, even shampoo.**

Poured in a glass? แก้ว.
Sealed in a bottle? ขวด.

**But what about things you can't even see or hold? Times, turns, rounds — Thai has classifiers for those too.**
