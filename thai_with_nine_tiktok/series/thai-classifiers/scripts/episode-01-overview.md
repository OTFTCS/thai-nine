Episode 01 - Why Thai Says "Water Two Bottles"

Target runtime: 110-140 seconds
Goal: show viewers that literal strange-English glosses help them remember Thai classifier order

### What [PERFORM] means

Every Thai phrase has a weird word-for-word English gloss (e.g. "dog BODY big very"). Lines tagged **[PERFORM]** mean: say that weird English out loud to camera with exaggerated comedic delivery — big energy, like you're amazed by how strange it sounds. This is the hook of the whole series. The gloss should also appear as large on-screen text at the same time. Don't rush past it — let the weirdness land. The [PERFORM] line IS the punchline.

Full script:

[Say the wrong Thai confidently, like you think it's right]

**ขอ 2 น้ำเปล่า** | khǎaw 2 náam bplào | ask 2 plain waters | please can I have 2 waters
(X) incorrect buzzer

**ผมชอบหนังสือนี้** | phǒm châwp nǎng-sěuu níi | I like book this | I like this book
(X) incorrect buzzer

**มันเป็นหมาใหญ่มาก** | man bpen mǎa yài mâak | it is dog big very | that's a big dog
(X) incorrect buzzer

[Beat. Deadpan to camera.]

**Every single one of those is wrong.**

**They sound like you badly Google Translated English into Thai.**

**Thai wants an extra word in all three. Watch.**

**ขอน้ำเปล่าสองขวด** | khǎaw náam bplào sǎawng khùat | ask plain-water two BOTTLES | please can I have 2 bottles of water
**[PERFORM] "Ask plain-water two BOTTLES."**

**ผมชอบหนังสือเล่มนี้** | phǒm châwp nǎng-sěuu lèm níi | I like book VOLUME this | I like this book
**[PERFORM] "I like book VOLUME this."**

**มันเป็นหมาตัวใหญ่มาก** | man bpen mǎa dtua yài mâak | it is dog BODY big very | that's a very big dog
**[PERFORM] "It is dog BODY big very."**

[Beat. Look at camera.]

**Yeah. That weird English? That IS the memory trick.**

**That extra word is called a classifier. Thai uses it every time you count, point at something, or describe something.**

[MINI-SCENE: At a fruit stall]

**You're at a market. You want two mangoes.**

**ขอมะม่วงสองลูก** | khǎaw má-mûang sǎawng lûuk | ask mango two ROUND-THINGS | two mangoes please
**[PERFORM] "Ask mango two ROUND-THINGS." Not "two mangoes please."**

[Back to camera]

**Here's how it works in all three situations.**

Counting:
**ซื้อเสื้อสามตัว** | séuu sêuua sǎam dtua | buy shirt three BODIES | bought three shirts
**[PERFORM] "Buy shirt three BODIES." Yeah — ตัว again, but for clothes this time.**

Pointing:
**โทรศัพท์เครื่องนั้น** | thoo-rá-sàp khrêuang nán | phone MACHINE that | that phone
**[PERFORM] "Phone MACHINE that."**

Describing:
**กาแฟแก้วใหญ่** | gaa-faae gâaeo yài | coffee GLASS big | a big coffee
**[PERFORM] "Coffee GLASS big."**

**Do not just memorize what the classifier means.**

**Memorize the weird English order.**

[PERFORM, big text, one at a time]
**"Shirt three BODIES."**
**"Phone MACHINE that."**
**"Coffee GLASS big."**
**"Car VEHICLE this."**

**That order is what makes you sound Thai.**

[PAUSE 3s — "YOUR TURN" on screen]

**Quick check. How do you say "I see two fish"?**

[Reveal]
**เห็นปลาสองตัว** | hěn bplaa sǎawng dtua | see fish two BODIES | I see two fish
**[PERFORM] "See fish two BODIES."**

[PAUSE 3s — "YOUR TURN" on screen]

**Transfer check. How would you say "this coffee"?**

[Reveal]
**กาแฟแก้วนี้** | gaa-faae gâaeo níi | coffee GLASS this | this coffee
**[PERFORM] "Coffee GLASS this."**
