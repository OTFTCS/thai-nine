Episode 04 - ใบ vs เล่ม

Target runtime: 70-100 seconds
Goal: teach the flat-item vs bound-item split through visual contrast and performed glosses

Full script:

[Hold up / show a ticket in one hand and a book in the other]

**In English, these are both "one."**

**In Thai? Completely different word.**

**ตั๋วหนึ่งใบ** | dtǔua nùeng bai | ticket one FLAT-THING | one ticket
**[PERFORM] "Ticket one FLAT-THING."**

**หนังสือหนึ่งเล่ม** | nǎng-sěuu nùeng lèm | book one VOLUME | one book
**[PERFORM] "Book one VOLUME."**

**ใบ is for flat, paper-like items. เล่ม is for books and bound things.**

**ใบ -- say "buy" with a flat tone. เล่ม -- say "lem" but drop your voice down. Falling tone.**

**บัตรหนึ่งใบ** | bàt nùeng bai | card one FLAT-THING | one card
**[PERFORM] "Card one FLAT-THING."**

**ใบเสร็จหนึ่งใบ** | bai-sèt nùeng bai | receipt one FLAT-THING | one receipt
**[PERFORM] "Receipt one FLAT-THING." Thai really said flat-thing.**

**สมุดหนึ่งเล่ม** | sà-mùt nùeng lèm | notebook one VOLUME | one notebook
**[PERFORM] "Notebook one VOLUME."**

**พาสปอร์ตหนึ่งเล่ม** | phâat-spàwt nùeng lèm | passport one VOLUME | one passport
**[PERFORM] "Passport one VOLUME." Because it's bound, like a tiny book.**

**And just like the other classifiers, these work for pointing and describing too.**

**สมุดเล่มนี้** | sà-mùt lèm níi | notebook VOLUME this | this notebook
**[PERFORM] "Notebook VOLUME this."**

**สมุดเล่มหนา** | sà-mùt lèm nǎa | notebook VOLUME thick | thick notebook
**[PERFORM] "Notebook VOLUME thick."**

**ตั๋วใบนั้น** | dtǔua bai nán | ticket FLAT-THING that | that ticket
**[PERFORM] "Ticket FLAT-THING that."**

[MINI-SCENE: Getting a receipt at 7-Eleven]

**You're at 7-Eleven. You get your receipt.**

**ใบเสร็จหนึ่งใบ** | bai-sèt nùeng bai | receipt one FLAT-THING | one receipt
**[PERFORM] "Receipt one FLAT-THING."**

**ใบ also covers leaves, bags, plates, and boxes -- anything flat or sheet-like. เล่ม works for magazines, passports, even swords.**

Flat and loose? ใบ.
Bound together? เล่ม.

[PAUSE 3s — "YOUR TURN" on screen]

**Quick check. "One notebook"?**

[Reveal]
**สมุดหนึ่งเล่ม** | sà-mùt nùeng lèm | notebook one VOLUME | one notebook
**[PERFORM] "Notebook one VOLUME."**

[PAUSE 3s — "YOUR TURN" on screen]

**Transfer check. New noun. "One queue ticket"?**

[Reveal]
**บัตรคิวหนึ่งใบ** | bàt khiu nùeng bai | queue-ticket one FLAT-THING | one queue ticket
**[PERFORM] "Queue-ticket one FLAT-THING."**

**Now what about your car and your phone? Same classifier? Nope.**

**Save this if ใบ and เล่ม always trip you up.**

On-screen beats:
- Open on visual split: flat item vs bound item, side by side
- [PERFORM] glosses in large text — let "FLAT-THING" land as comedy
- Mini-scene: 7-Eleven receipt moment
- Challenge beats: "YOUR TURN" graphic with 3s countdown
- End on forward hook: "Your car and your phone..."
