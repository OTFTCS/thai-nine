Episode 04 - ใบ vs เล่ม

Target runtime: 70-90 seconds
Goal: teach the flat-item vs bound-item split fast

Full script:

A ticket and a book are not the same kind of "one" in Thai.

Use "ใบ" for flat paper-like items.

ตั๋วหนึ่งใบ | dtǔua nùeng bai | ticket one flat-thing | one ticket

บัตรหนึ่งใบ | bàt nùeng bai | card one flat-thing | one card

ใบเสร็จหนึ่งใบ | bai-sèt nùeng bai | receipt one flat-thing | one receipt

Use "เล่ม" for books and bound items.

หนังสือหนึ่งเล่ม | nǎng-sěuu nùeng lèm | book one volume | one book

สมุดหนึ่งเล่ม | sà-mùt nùeng lèm | notebook one volume | one notebook

พาสปอร์ตหนึ่งเล่ม | phâat-spàwt nùeng lèm | passport one volume | one passport

Fast shortcut.

Loose flat item, think "ใบ".

Book-like unit you hold as one bound thing, think "เล่ม".

The weird English helps here too.

Ticket one flat-thing.

Book one volume.

Quick check.

สมุดหนึ่งเล่ม | sà-mùt nùeng lèm | notebook one volume | one notebook

Transfer check.

บัตรคิวหนึ่งใบ | bàt khiu nùeng bai | queue-ticket one flat-thing | one queue ticket

Save this if "ใบ" and "เล่ม" always trip you up.

On-screen beats:
- Open on a ticket card and a book cover side by side
- Stack the "ใบ" examples first, then the "เล่ม" examples
- End on a single card with a short pause:
  บัตรคิวหนึ่งใบ | bàt khiu nùeng bai | queue-ticket one flat-thing | one queue ticket
