Thai with Nine — Screen Layout Mockups

Six screen modes showing how text, Nine, and images work together

Mode 1 — Natural Speed Listen

Nine speaks Thai at natural speed. The viewer is testing their comprehension. Thai script is on screen, no English. The AI background image sets the scene for the topic.

Option A — Bottom text, Nine in corner

Natural Listen
[ AI image: Thai street food market scene ]
Nine (camera)
คุณชอบอาหารไทยไหม

Clean and simple. Thai only. Forces the viewer to listen and read simultaneously. The background image gives context without words.


Option B — Centre text, no Nine visible (full immersion)

Natural Listen
[ AI image: Thai street food market scene ]
คุณชอบอาหารไทยไหม

More cinematic. Nine's voice is heard but not seen. Good for pure listening immersion. Could alternate with Option A.

Mode 2 — Sentence Breakdown

The core teaching moment. Nine says a Thai sentence, it appears on screen. Then transliteration. Then English fades in after a beat. Three stacked lines. This is what needs to be perfectly synced.

Option A — Three-line stack, bottom of screen

Breakdown
[ AI image: softly blurred ]
Nine (camera)
ฉันชอบอาหารไทยมาก
chǎn chɔ̂ɔp aa-hǎan thai mâak
"I like Thai food very much."

The money shot. Thai script is always the largest. Transliteration in gold sits between. English in blue is the smallest — it's the safety net, not the focus. The three colours (white / gold / blue) make it instantly scannable.


Option B — Three-line stack, centred (no Nine visible during breakdown)

Breakdown
ฉันชอบอาหารไทยมาก
chǎn chɔ̂ɔp aa-hǎan thai mâak
"I like Thai food very much."

Cleaner. Easier to read. No visual distraction from Nine's face. Could use this for the actual sentence display, then cut back to Nine when she explains the grammar.


Option C — Staged reveal (how the three lines appear over time)

STEP 1
ฉันชอบอาหารไทยมาก
—
—
STEP 2
ฉันชอบอาหารไทยมาก
chǎn chɔ̂ɔp aa-hǎan thai mâak
—
STEP 3
ฉันชอบอาหารไทยมาก
chǎn chɔ̂ɔp aa-hǎan thai mâak
"I like Thai food very much."

The staged reveal is key. Thai appears when Nine says it. Transliteration fades in after 1 second. English reveals after another beat. This gives the viewer a moment to try reading before getting the answer. Whisper timestamps drive when each line appears.

Mode 3 — Vocabulary Card

When a new word is introduced. Full screen takeover with the word, transliteration, meaning, and an AI image of the thing. Like a flashcard that appears mid-video.

Vocab Card
[ AI image: shrimp ]
กุ้ง
gûng
shrimp
ฉันแพ้กุ้ง กินกุ้งไม่ได้เลย
"I'm allergic to shrimp. I can't eat shrimp at all."

Image on left, text stack on right. The example sentence anchors the word in context. These frames also work perfectly as standalone Instagram/TikTok image posts or carousel slides.

Mode 4 — Production Drill

Nine gives a prompt in English. The viewer has a few seconds to try saying it in Thai. Then Nine gives the answer. Two-stage reveal.

PROMPT
Nine
How would you say:
"I like Thai food very much"
⏸ Try saying it now...
ANSWER
Nine
ฉันชอบอาหารไทยมาก
chǎn chɔ̂ɔp aa-hǎan thai mâak
"I like Thai food very much."

The pause moment is crucial — it's what separates this from passive watching. The prompt-then-reveal format also makes perfect standalone Shorts ("Can you say this in Thai?").

Mode 5 — Shadowing Practice

Nine speaks slowly. The full paragraph is on screen in split-word Thai. The current word/phrase highlights as she speaks it. The viewer repeats along.

Shadowing
Nine (camera)
ฉัน ชอบ อาหาร ไทย มาก
คุณ ชอบ อาหาร ไทย ไหม
คุณ กิน เผ็ด ได้ ไหม
chǎn chɔ̂ɔp aa-hǎan thai mâak

Split-word Thai with the current word highlighted in gold. Upcoming sentences are dimmed. Transliteration for the active sentence only. This is karaoke-style word tracking — Whisper word-level timestamps drive the highlight position.

Mode 6 — Nine Explains (English)

Nine switches to English to explain a grammar point or cultural note. The Thai word/phrase she's referencing stays pinned on screen as a reference.

Explanation
Nine (half screen)
ชอบ
chɔ̂ɔp
to like
Used for preferences.
Always comes before the object.
ชอบ + noun = like (something)
ชอบ + verb = like doing

Nine takes up more screen space here — she's the focus, talking to camera. The reference card on the right keeps the Thai visible so the viewer connects her English explanation to the Thai word. Grammar notes can be bullet points or pattern formulas.

Colour System Summary

Consistent colour coding across all modes so viewers instantly know what language they're looking at.

White
Thai script (always largest)
Gold
Transliteration + highlights
Light blue
English translation
Font hierarchy
อาหาร Thai — Sarabun Bold, largest
aa-hǎan Transliteration — Inter Medium, mid-size
"food" English — Inter Medium, same or smaller
