SLIDE TYPES IN DETAIL

1. Lesson opener
- lesson id and title
- very clean
- clearly branded for the course
- should feel premium and calm
- no clutter

2. Key phrase focus slide
- large Thai
- transliteration below
- English gloss below that
- concise teacher-support explanation
- enough breathing room

3. Contrast slide
Used for distinctions like:
- male / female polite endings
- thank you vs excuse me
- yes / no / no problem
Make the contrast instantly understandable.

4. Explanation slide
Use for:
- grammar function
- politeness explanation
- usage nuance
- interaction logic
Should feel clear and teacherly, not like a dense grammar book page.

5. Drill slide
Use for:
- repeat-after-me
- substitution
- choose-the-right-ending
- complete-the-response
Should be easy to follow visually.

6. Roleplay slide
- make dialogue readable
- clearly separate speakers
- show exchange logic
- avoid cramped speech bubbles
- preserve realistic social flow

7. Recap slide
- concise review
- not a wall of text
- should leave learner feeling oriented

8. Quiz/checkpoint slide
- one quick prompt or a small set of prompts
- should look like an embedded knowledge check, not a school exam
- useful for later quiz conversion

9. Closing slide
- what the learner can now say / notice
- calm, satisfying end
