# M01-L001 — Hello and Basic Courtesy

- **Module:** M01 — First Contact and Courtesy
- **Level:** A0
- **Target runtime:** 9-12 minutes
- **Primary objective:** Learner can greet people politely, thank others, apologise, and answer yes/no in a friendly first encounter.
- **Secondary objective:** Learner can choose the right basic polite ending and recognise when ไม่เป็นไร is the correct social response.
- **Teaching frame:** First meeting at a reception desk or service counter.

## Must-teach outcomes
1. Say hello politely with a correct basic ending.
2. Use ขอบคุณ and ขอโทษ in the right social moments.
3. Distinguish ครับ, ค่ะ, and คะ at a practical beginner level.
4. Answer with ใช่ or ไม่ใช่ and soften with ไม่เป็นไร when appropriate.
5. Deliver a short first-contact exchange without dropping politeness.

## Core vocabulary and chunks
| Thai | Transliteration | English |
|---|---|---|
| สวัสดี | sà-wàt-dii | hello |
| ขอบคุณ | khàawp-khun | thank you |
| ขอโทษ | khǎaw-thôot | excuse me / sorry |
| ใช่ | châi | yes / that is right |
| ไม่ใช่ | mâi châi | no / not right |
| ครับ | khráp | male polite ending |
| ค่ะ | khâ | female statement ending |
| คะ | khá | female question ending |
| ไม่เป็นไร | mâi bpen rai | it is okay / no problem |

## Teaching constraints
- Keep this spoken-first and socially practical.
- Explain what each courtesy line does in the interaction, not just its dictionary meaning.
- Keep visuals text-first and left-panel only.
- Do not overload the learner with edge-case particle theory.

## Likely learner mistakes
1. Treating polite endings as optional decoration instead of meaning-carrying social tools.
2. Mixing ค่ะ and คะ without hearing the statement/question difference.
3. Using ไม่เป็นไร as a yes/no answer instead of a social softener.
4. Saying hello without warmth because the ending is dropped too early.
