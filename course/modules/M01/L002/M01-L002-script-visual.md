# Visual Script — M01-L002 (Names and Countries)

## Frame rule
- 16:9 canvas (1920 x 1080)
- Right third reserved for Nine's camera (PiP zone)
- All teaching content in left two-thirds
- Font: Sarabun for Thai, transliteration, and English

## Slide 1: Opener
- Lesson title: "Names and Countries — Introducing Yourself"
- Subtitle: M01-L002
- Clean, minimal — no teaching content on opener

## Slide 2: Section 1 — Your name in Thai (focus-card)
- **Focus:** Individual word cards for ชื่อ and อะไร
- Card 1: ชื่อ | chûue | name (large, centered in teaching area)
- Card 2: อะไร | à-rai | what
- Then combined chunk: คุณชื่ออะไร | khun chûue à-rai | what is your name?
- Each word gets its own visual moment before combining
- Keep cards stacked vertically, large type

## Slide 3: Section 2 — Say your name (drill-stack)
- **Focus:** คน individual card, then ผม/ฉันชื่อ... pattern
- Card 1: คน (khon, mid tone) = person
- Pattern stack:
  - ผมชื่อ... | phǒm chûue... | my name is... (male)
  - ฉันชื่อ... | chǎn chûue... | my name is... (female)
- Highlight: no "is" in Thai — pattern = I + ชื่อ + name
- Drill prompts visible below pattern cards

## Slide 4: Section 3 — Where you come from (focus-card)
- **Focus:** Individual cards for มาจาก and ประเทศ
- Card 1: มาจาก | maa-jàak | come from
- Card 2: ประเทศ | bprà-thêet | country
- Combined pattern: มาจากประเทศ... | maa-jàak bprà-thêet... | come from (country)...
- Show blank slot for country name

## Slide 5: Section 4 — Thailand, England, America (contrast-board)
- **Focus:** Three country name cards
- ไทย (thai, mid tone) = Thailand / Thai
- อังกฤษ | ang-grìt | England / English
- อเมริกา | à-mee-rí-kaa | America
- Optional: small flag icons beside each name
- Below: คนไทย (khon thai, mid tones) = Thai person
- Below: คนอังกฤษ | khon ang-grìt | English person
- Pattern frame visible: ผม/ฉันมาจากประเทศ___ครับ/ค่ะ

## Slide 6: Section 5 — Pronunciation: bp sound (contrast-board)
- **Focus:** bp vs ph contrast
- Two-column layout:
  - Left: bp (no air puff) — ประเทศ bprà-thêet, ป่า bpàa (forest)
  - Right: ph (air puff) — ผม phǒm, ผ้า phâa (cloth)
- Clear labels: "no air" vs "air puff"
- Minimal pair highlighted: ป่า vs ผ้า

## Slide 7: Roleplay (dialogue-ladder)
- Title: "Roleplay"
- Turn-by-turn dialogue ladder
- Speaker labels: You / New person
- Each line shows Thai, transliteration, and English
- Highlight production turns with "your turn" markers
- Keep within left teaching area

## Slide 8: Recap
- 5 retrieval prompts as numbered list
- No answers visible — learner recalls first
- Clean layout, large readable type

## Slide 9: Closing
- Lesson complete message
- Preview of next lesson
