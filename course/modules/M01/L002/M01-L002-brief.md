# M01-L002 — Names and Countries

- **Module:** M01 — First Contact and Courtesy
- **Level:** A0
- **Target runtime:** 10-13 minutes
- **Primary objective:** Learner can ask someone’s name, say your own name, and state where you are from.
- **Secondary objective:** Learner can reuse the previous greeting/courtesy lesson while building a two- or three-line self-introduction.
- **Teaching frame:** First meeting at a class, event, or reception desk.

## Must-teach outcomes
1. Ask and answer the name question.
2. Recognise and use ชื่อ as the core name word.
3. Say where you are from with มาจาก.
4. Produce a short self-introduction with hello, name, and country.
5. Reuse at least two previous courtesy items naturally.

## Teaching constraints
- Keep the name and country patterns highly scaffolded.
- Reuse greeting/courtesy naturally instead of introducing identity language in isolation.
- Keep map/flag support small and secondary to the sentence frame.

## Likely learner mistakes
1. Translating word by word instead of treating the name question as a fixed chunk.
2. Freezing over pronoun choice instead of committing to one clear beginner frame.
3. Memorising country words without learning the มาจาก pattern.
4. Dropping the greeting so the self-introduction sounds abrupt.

## Image and visual notes
- Most sections should stay text-first.
- Only the country section benefits from a light visual anchor such as simple flags or map cues.
