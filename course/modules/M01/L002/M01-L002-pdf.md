# M01-L002 — Names and Countries: Simple Self-Introduction Thai

## What you will be able to say after this lesson
- Target runtime: 10-13 minutes
- Scenario: A learner meets someone for the first time and wants to introduce themselves in two or three clean lines.
- Learner takeaway: By the end, the learner can greet, ask a name, give a name, and say where they are from in a compact polite exchange.

## Core phrases (Thai / Transliteration / English)
- ชื่อ | chûue | name
- คุณชื่ออะไร | khun chûue à-rai | what is your name?
- ฉันชื่อ... | chǎn chûue... | my name is ...
- ผมชื่อ... | phǒm chûue... | my name is ... (male speaker)
- ฉัน | chǎn | I
- ผม | phǒm | I (male)
- ประเทศ | bprà-thêet | country
- มาจาก | maa-jàak | come from / be from
- มาจากไทย | maa-jàak thai | be from Thailand
- มาจากอังกฤษ | maa-jàak ang-grìt | be from England
- มาจากอเมริกา | maa-jàak à-mee-rí-gaa | be from America
- สวัสดี | sà-wàt-dii | hello
- ขอบคุณ | khàawp-khun | thank you
- ฉันมาจาก... | chǎn maa-jàak... | I am from ...
- ผมมาจาก... | phǒm maa-jàak... | I am from ... (male)

## Teaching notes and explanation cues
- Ask and answer the name question: Teach the core name pattern with ชื่อ and อะไร so the learner can ask and answer the most basic identity question immediately.
- The first new question is คุณชื่ออะไร khun chûue à-rai. Literally, it is your name what, but functionally it means what is your name?
- The answer is simple and formulaic: ฉันชื่อ... chǎn chûue... or ผมชื่อ... phǒm chûue.... This is good news for a beginner because the pattern is very stable.
- Use ฉัน and ผม naturally: Explain the beginner-level pronoun choice so the learner can produce self-introduction lines without freezing over identity wording.
- Thai gives you more than one way to say I. In this course at beginner level, we use ฉัน chǎn as a common all-purpose learning form and ผม phǒm for a clearly male self-reference.
- One small support word here is คน khon, person. It helps you hear that this is a conversation between people, not just a set of labels, and it keeps the pronoun choice grounded in who is speaking.
- Say where you are from with มาจาก: Teach the origin pattern so the learner can complete a basic introduction with country information instead of stopping after the name.
- Before the country examples, learn ประเทศ bprà-thêet. It means country, and it gives you the category that ties the place words together.
- To say where you are from, Thai often uses มาจาก maa-jàak, literally come from. So the practical beginner frame is ฉันมาจาก... or ผมมาจาก....
- Build a full two-line self-introduction: Combine the greeting from lesson 1 with the new identity patterns so the learner can produce a practical mini-introduction.
- Now connect the old lesson and the new one. Start with สวัสดี, then give your name, and then give your country.
- If you need to begin politely, you can still borrow ขอโทษ from the last lesson to get attention before the question. If you are checking what you heard, ใช่ helps you confirm it.

## Mini dialogues / role-play
- A: สวัสดีค่ะ | sà-wàt-dii khâ | Hello.
- B: สวัสดีครับ | sà-wàt-dii khráp | Hello.
- A: ขอโทษค่ะ คุณชื่ออะไรคะ | khǎaw-thôot khâ khun chûue à-rai khá | Excuse me, what is your name?
- B: ผมชื่อแดนครับ | phǒm chûue Daen khráp | My name is Dan.
- A: มาจากประเทศอะไรคะ | maa-jàak bprà-thêet à-rai khá | What country are you from?
- B: ผมมาจากอังกฤษครับ | phǒm maa-jàak ang-grìt khráp | I am from England.
- A: อังกฤษใช่ไหมคะ | ang-grìt châi mái khá | England, right?
- B: ใช่ครับ | châi khráp | Yes.
- A: ขอบคุณค่ะ | khàawp-khun khâ | Thank you.

## Practice drills (self-study)
- Teacher asks คุณชื่ออะไร; learner answers with ฉันชื่อ... or ผมชื่อ....
- Substitution drill: change only the name while keeping the full pattern intact.
- Prompt drill: teacher gives the role, learner starts the sentence with ฉัน or ผม and finishes the name line.
- Quick recognition drill: hear คน and identify who the speaking person is in the line before repeating it.
- Category drill: teacher says ประเทศ and the learner points to the country words that belong under it.
- Country substitution drill: keep ฉันมาจาก... or ผมมาจาก... and swap only the country.
- Listening drill: hear a country line and point to ไทย, อังกฤษ, or อเมริกา before repeating it.
- Build drill: learner says hello, name, and country as one three-step sequence.
- Partner drill: one learner starts with ขอโทษ and คุณชื่ออะไร, the other answers with name and country, then the first learner confirms with ใช่.

## Quick recap + memory aids
- Learn คุณชื่ออะไร and its answer as one fixed beginner pair.
- Choose one stable self-pronoun and focus on producing the full line confidently.
- Use มาจาก to say where you are from and keep the country slot flexible.
- Carry over สวัสดี and ขอบคุณ so the introduction sounds social, not robotic.
