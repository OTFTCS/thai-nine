# Spoken Script — M01-L003: Pronouns and Who Questions

## Opening

You can already say hello and introduce your name. But what about "I", "you", "he", "she"? And how do you ask who someone is? Let's learn the words that let you talk about people.

---

## Section 1: Two ways to say I

In English, everyone says "I". In Thai, you choose which "I" to use. Let's learn both.

Listen first. [THAI: ฉัน chǎn] This means "I". Female speakers use ฉัน in everyday conversation. Say it with me: [THAI: ฉัน]

Now the second one. Listen: [THAI: ผม phǒm] This also means "I", but male speakers use ผม in polite conversation. Say it with me: [THAI: ผม]

So if you are female, your safe "I" is ฉัน. If you are male, your safe "I" is ผม. Both have a rising tone — your voice goes up at the end.

Quick check: someone asks your name. If you are male, you start with [THAI: ผมชื่อ... phǒm chûue...] If you are female, you say [THAI: ฉันชื่อ... chǎn chûue...] Try it now with your own name. [PAUSE 5s]

**Drill — Listen and repeat:** [THAI: ฉัน chǎn] Again. [THAI: ผม phǒm] Again.

**Drill — Choose the right I:** I describe a speaker. Say ฉัน or ผม before I show the answer. Male teacher introducing himself? [PAUSE 3s] ผม. Female student talking about herself? [PAUSE 3s] ฉัน.

**Drill — Substitution:** Say "my name is..." using ผม first — [THAI: ผมชื่อ... phǒm chûue...] Now switch to ฉัน — [THAI: ฉันชื่อ... chǎn chûue...]

---

## Section 2: Talking about you and someone else

You know how to say "I". Now let's add "you" and "he" or "she".

Listen first. [THAI: คุณ khun] This means "you". It is polite and safe in any situation. Say it with me: [THAI: คุณ]

คุณ is also the word you heard in lesson two: [THAI: คุณชื่ออะไร khun chûue à-rai] — what is your name? That คุณ is the same word — "you".

Now listen: [THAI: เขา khǎo] This means "he" or "she". Thai does not separate he and she — เขา covers both. Say it with me: [THAI: เขา]

Let's use all four. [THAI: ฉันชื่อนิ chǎn chûue ní] — I am called Ni. [THAI: คุณชื่ออะไร khun chûue à-rai] — what is your name? [THAI: เขาชื่อมาร์ค khǎo chûue máak] — he is called Mark. Your turn — say เขาชื่อ with any name. [PAUSE 5s]

**Drill — Listen and repeat:** [THAI: คุณ khun] [THAI: เขา khǎo] Again faster.

**Drill — Response-building:** I point to someone and ask [THAI: คุณชื่ออะไร]. Pause and answer with ฉันชื่อ or ผมชื่อ using your real name. [PAUSE 5s]

**Drill — Substitution:** Say "his/her name is..." — [THAI: เขาชื่อ... khǎo chûue...] Try it with three different names. [PAUSE 5s]

---

## Section 3: This is... — introducing someone

Now you can say I, you, and he/she. Let's learn to introduce someone: "This is..."

Listen first. [THAI: นี่ nîi] This means "this". You use it when pointing to something or someone close by. Say it with me: [THAI: นี่]

Now listen. [THAI: คือ khuu] This means "is" — it connects two things. X คือ Y means X is Y. Say it with me: [THAI: คือ]

Put them together: [THAI: นี่คือ... nîi khuu...] This is... Listen: [THAI: นี่คือเพื่อน nîi khuu phûuean] — this is a friend. Now you try: นี่คือ... and add a name or word you know. [PAUSE 5s]

Let's recycle. [THAI: นี่คือคุณมาร์ค nîi khuu khun máak] — this is Khun Mark. [THAI: นี่คือคนไทย nîi khuu khon thai] — this is a Thai person. Your turn — introduce someone using นี่คือ. [PAUSE 5s]

**Drill — Listen and repeat:** [THAI: นี่ nîi] [THAI: คือ khuu] [THAI: นี่คือ nîi khuu]

**Drill — Substitution:** I give you a word, you build the introduction. เพื่อน — say นี่คือเพื่อน. [PAUSE 3s] คนไทย — say นี่คือคนไทย. [PAUSE 3s] คุณนิ — say นี่คือคุณนิ. [PAUSE 3s]

**Drill — Pause-and-produce:** I point to someone. Say "this is..." in Thai before I show the answer. Your turn. [PAUSE 5s]

---

## Section 4: Who is that? — asking identity questions

You can introduce someone with นี่คือ. But what if you want to ask who someone is?

Listen first. [THAI: ใคร khrai] This means "who". Say it with me: [THAI: ใคร]

In Thai, the question word goes at the end. So "who is he?" becomes [THAI: เขาคือใคร khǎo khuu khrai] — literally "he is who". Listen again: [THAI: เขาคือใคร] Now say it. [PAUSE 3s]

You can also confirm identity with a tag question. [THAI: คุณคือคุณมาร์คใช่ไหม khun khuu khun máak châi mái] — you are Khun Mark, right? You already know ใช่ from lesson one — ใช่ไหม means "right?" or "is that so?" Listen: [THAI: คุณคือคุณนิใช่ไหม] Your turn — try it with any name. [PAUSE 5s]

Let's put it all together. Someone walks in. You ask [THAI: เขาคือใคร] — who is he? Someone answers [THAI: นี่คือคุณมาร์ค]. You confirm [THAI: คุณคือคุณมาร์คใช่ไหม]. They say [THAI: ใช่ครับ]. Now you have a complete identity exchange.

**Drill — Listen and repeat:** [THAI: ใคร khrai] [THAI: เขาคือใคร khǎo khuu khrai] [THAI: คุณคือ...ใช่ไหม khun khuu... châi mái]

**Drill — Response-building:** I point to a person. Ask เขาคือใคร. [PAUSE 3s] I answer. Then confirm with คุณคือ...ใช่ไหม. Build the full exchange. [PAUSE 5s]

**Drill — Substitution:** Ask "who is he/she?" — เขาคือใคร. [PAUSE 3s] Now confirm identity: คุณคือคุณนิใช่ไหม. [PAUSE 3s] Try with another name: คุณคือคุณมาร์คใช่ไหม. [PAUSE 3s]

---

## Section 5: Pronunciation — rising tones in pronouns

Three of today's pronouns share the same tone. Listen: [THAI: ฉัน chǎn] [THAI: ผม phǒm] [THAI: เขา khǎo] Can you hear it? They all rise up at the end. This is the rising tone.

The rising tone starts low and lifts up, like you are asking a question in English. Try it: chǎn. Your voice dips down then goes up. Again: phǒm. Again: khǎo. [PAUSE 5s]

Now compare. [THAI: คุณ khun] — that is a mid tone, flat and steady. [THAI: เขา khǎo] — rising, your voice goes up. Listen again: khun, khǎo. Can you hear the difference?

One more pair. [THAI: ใคร khrai] — mid tone, flat. [THAI: ไข่ khài] — low tone, your voice stays low. Different tones, different meanings. Listen: khrai — who. khài — egg. Which one means "who"? Point before I tell you. [PAUSE 3s]

**Drill — Tone echo:** I say [THAI: ฉัน chǎn]. Repeat it. [PAUSE 3s] Now [THAI: ผม phǒm]. Repeat. [PAUSE 3s] Now [THAI: เขา khǎo]. Repeat. [PAUSE 3s] Feel the rise.

**Drill — Minimal pair choice:** I say two words — khun and khǎo. Which one is "you" and which is "he/she"? Point to the answer. [PAUSE 3s]

## Roleplay: Meeting a friend's colleague

[SCREEN: Dialogue ladder with turn labels]

**You:** [THAI: ขอโทษครับ เขาคือใคร khǎaw-thôot khráp khǎo khuu khrai] — Excuse me, who is he/she?

**Friend:** [THAI: นี่คือคุณนิค่ะ nîi khuu khun ní khâ] — This is Khun Ni.

**You:** [THAI: คุณคือคุณนิใช่ไหมครับ khun khuu khun ní châi mái khráp] — You are Khun Ni, right? *(Say this line before I show it — your turn)*

**Friend:** [THAI: ใช่ค่ะ châi khâ] — Yes.

**You:** [THAI: สวัสดีครับ ผมชื่อมาร์คครับ sà-wàt-dii khráp phǒm chûue máak khráp] — Hello, my name is Mark. *(Your turn — introduce yourself)*

**Friend:** [THAI: สวัสดีค่ะ คุณมาจากประเทศอะไรคะ sà-wàt-dii khâ khun maa-jàak bprà-thêet à-rai khá] — Hello, what country are you from?

**You:** [THAI: ผมมาจากประเทศอังกฤษครับ phǒm maa-jàak bprà-thêet ang-grìt khráp] — I am from England.

**Friend:** [THAI: คุณมาจากประเทศอังกฤษใช่ไหมคะ khun maa-jàak bprà-thêet ang-grìt châi mái khá] — You are from England, right?

**You:** [THAI: ใช่ครับ ขอบคุณครับ châi khráp khàawp-khun khráp] — Yes. Thank you.

## Recap

1. How do you say "I" as a male speaker? Say it before looking.
2. Point to someone and say "who is he?" in Thai.
3. Introduce a friend: say "this is Khun Ni" in Thai.
4. Confirm identity: say "you are Khun Mark, right?" in Thai.
5. What word means "this"? And what word means "is"?
