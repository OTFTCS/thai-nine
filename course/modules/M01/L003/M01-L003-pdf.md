# M01-L003 — Pronouns and Who Questions: Identify People in Simple Thai

## What you will be able to say after this lesson
- Target runtime: 10-13 minutes
- Scenario: A learner is meeting people in a class or social setting and needs to identify, introduce, and confirm who someone is.
- Learner takeaway: By the end, the learner can use ฉัน, ผม, and เขา practically, introduce someone with นี่คือ..., and ask เขาคือใคร without freezing.

## Core phrases (Thai / Transliteration / English)
- ฉัน | chǎn | I
- ผม | phǒm | I (male speaker)
- เขา | khǎo | he / she
- คุณชื่ออะไร | khun chûue à-rai | what is your name?
- นี่คือ... | nîi kheuu... | this is ...
- เขาคือ... | khǎo kheuu... | he / she is ...
- คุณคือ...ใช่ไหม | khun kheuu... châi mái | you are ... right?
- ไม่ใช่ | mâi châi | no / not right
- เขาคือใคร | khǎo kheuu khrai | who is he / she?
- คนนี้คือใคร | khon níi kheuu khrai | who is this person?
- ชื่อ | chûue | name
- ใช่ | châi | yes / that is right
- ขอโทษนะ | khǎaw-thôot ná | excuse me
- ประเทศ | bprà-thêet | country
- เขามาจากไทย | khǎo maa-jàak thai | he / she is from Thailand

## Teaching notes and explanation cues
- Use pronouns as practical speaker labels: Teach pronouns as tools for basic interaction, not as abstract dictionary entries, so the learner can point to self and others naturally.
- Today we add a small set of pronouns, but we keep them practical. ฉัน chǎn and ผม phǒm are both ways to say I, while เขา khǎo points to another person as he or she.
- Thai beginners often try to memorise a long pronoun chart. That is not the goal here. The goal is to know which word helps you identify yourself and which word helps you refer to someone else.
- Use นี่คือ... to introduce and identify: Explain how คือ works in beginner identity lines so the learner can introduce someone or identify what is in front of them without treating it as a rigid English equals sign.
- The next frame is นี่คือ... nîi kheuu.... A natural translation is this is ..., and Thai uses it to introduce a person or identify something in front of you.
- The high-frequency word คือ kheuu is doing a linking job here. It connects the person or thing you are talking about with the name or identity that comes after it.
- Ask who someone is with ใคร: Teach the identity question with ใคร so the learner can ask who a person is in a short natural exchange and not confuse it with the earlier name question.
- Now we ask the new question: เขาคือใคร khǎo kheuu khrai. In natural English that is who is he or who is she, depending on context.
- This is different from คุณชื่ออะไร. The name question asks for a name directly, but เขาคือใคร asks for identity first, often when you are looking at or hearing about another person.
- Build a mini identity exchange with review: Combine the new who-question pattern with earlier name and country material so the learner can follow and produce a short first-meeting dialogue.
- Now we connect the new lesson to the earlier first-contact material. You can greet, ask who someone is, and then confirm one more detail such as the country.
- This is where review matters. We reuse ชื่อ chûue, ประเทศ bprà-thêet, ใช่ châi, and มาจากไทย maa-jàak thai so the learner builds a small network instead of isolated lesson boxes.

## Mini dialogues / role-play
- A: สวัสดีค่ะ | sà-wàt-dii khâ | Hello.
- B: สวัสดีครับ | sà-wàt-dii khráp | Hello.
- A: ขอโทษนะคะ คนนี้คือใครคะ | khǎaw-thôot ná khá khon níi kheuu khrai khá | Excuse me, who is this person?
- B: คนนี้คือไมค์ครับ | khon níi kheuu Mike khráp | This is Mike.
- A: เขามาจากประเทศอะไรคะ | khǎo maa-jàak bprà-thêet à-rai khá | What country is he from?
- B: เขามาจากอังกฤษครับ | khǎo maa-jàak ang-grìt khráp | He is from England.
- A: อังกฤษใช่ไหมคะ | ang-grìt châi mái khá | England, right?
- B: ใช่ครับ | châi khráp | Yes.

## Practice drills (self-study)
- Point-and-say drill: teacher points to self or another person and learner chooses ฉัน, ผม, or เขา aloud.
- Replacement drill: learner keeps the same sentence slot but swaps ฉัน / ผม / เขา depending on the speaker card.
- Introduction drill: teacher points to a photo card and learner says นี่คือ... with the supplied name or role.
- Confirmation drill: teacher says คุณคือ...ใช่ไหม and learner answers ใช่ or ไม่ใช่.
- Picture cue drill: teacher shows a person card and learner asks เขาคือใคร or คนนี้คือใคร.
- Repair drill: teacher gives a wrong identification and learner answers ไม่ใช่, then asks the who-question again.
- Chain drill: learner says สวัสดี, asks คนนี้คือใคร, then follows with a country question or confirmation question.
- Information-build drill: teacher reveals one detail at a time and learner turns it into an identity sentence or confirmation question.

## Quick recap + memory aids
- Use pronouns as speaking tools: ฉัน and ผม for yourself, เขา for another person.
- Use นี่คือ... and เขาคือ... to introduce or identify, not as rigid word-for-word translations only.
- Ask เขาคือใคร or คนนี้คือใคร when you want identity first, not just a name.
- Reuse ชื่อ, ประเทศ, and earlier greeting material so the dialogue grows naturally.
