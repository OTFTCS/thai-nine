M01-L003

Spoken Script — Pronouns and Who Questions

Use basic pronouns and ask who someone is using คือ and ใคร in short controlled exchanges.

S1

Two ways to say I

Teach ฉัน and ผม individually as the two ways to say 'I' in Thai, then drill choosing the right one.

In English, everyone says 'I'. In Thai, you choose which 'I' to use. Let's learn both.

Listen first. ฉัน chǎn. This means 'I'. Female speakers use ฉัน in everyday conversation. Say it with me: ฉัน.

Now the second one. Listen: ผม phǒm. This also means 'I', but male speakers use ผม in polite conversation. Say it with me: ผม.

So if you are female, your safe 'I' is ฉัน. If you are male, your safe 'I' is ผม. Both have a rising tone — your voice goes up at the end.

Quick check: someone asks your name. If you are male, you start with ผมชื่อ... phǒm chûue... If you are female, you say ฉันชื่อ... chǎn chûue... Try it now with your own name.

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
ฉันchǎnI (female / neutral)
ผมphǒmI (male polite)

Drills

  • Listen and repeat: ฉัน chǎn. Again. ผม phǒm. Again.
  • Choose the right 'I': I describe a speaker. Say ฉัน or ผม before I show the answer. Male teacher introducing himself? Your turn. Female student talking about herself? Your turn.
  • Substitution drill: say 'my name is...' using ผม first — ผมชื่อ... Now switch to ฉัน — ฉันชื่อ...
S2

Talking about you and someone else

Teach คุณ and เขา individually, then drill using all four pronouns in short sentences.

You know how to say 'I'. Now let's add 'you' and 'he' or 'she'.

Listen first. คุณ khun. This means 'you'. It is polite and safe in any situation. Say it with me: คุณ.

คุณ is also the word you heard in lesson two: คุณชื่ออะไร — what is your name? That คุณ is the same word — 'you'.

Now listen: เขา khǎo. This means 'he' or 'she'. Thai does not separate he and she — เขา covers both. Say it with me: เขา.

Let's use all four. ฉันชื่อนิ chǎn chûue ní — I am called Ni. คุณชื่ออะไร khun chûue à-rai — what is your name? เขาชื่อมาร์ค khǎo chûue máak — he is called Mark. Your turn — say เขาชื่อ with any name.

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
คุณkhunyou (polite)
เขาkhǎohe / she

Drills

  • Listen and repeat: คุณ khun. เขา khǎo. Again faster.
  • Response-building: I point to someone and ask คุณชื่ออะไร. Pause and answer with ฉันชื่อ... or ผมชื่อ... using your real name.
  • Substitution drill: say 'his/her name is...' — เขาชื่อ... Try it with three different names.
S3

This is... — introducing someone

Teach นี่ and คือ individually, then combine into the chunk นี่คือ... for making introductions.

Now you can say I, you, and he/she. Let's learn to introduce someone: 'This is...'

Listen first. นี่ nîi. This means 'this'. You use it when pointing to something or someone close by. Say it with me: นี่.

Now listen. คือ khuu. This means 'is' — it connects two things. X คือ Y means X is Y. Say it with me: คือ.

Put them together: นี่คือ... nîi khuu... This is... Listen: นี่คือเพื่อน nîi khuu phûuean — this is a friend. Now you try: นี่คือ... and add a name or word you know.

Let's recycle. นี่คือคุณมาร์ค nîi khuu khun máak — this is Khun Mark. นี่คือคนไทย nîi khuu khon thai — this is a Thai person. Your turn — introduce someone using นี่คือ.

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
นี่nîithis
คือkhuuis
นี่คือ...nîi khuu...this is...

Drills

  • Listen and repeat: นี่ nîi. คือ khuu. นี่คือ nîi khuu.
  • Substitution drill: I give you a word, you build the introduction. เพื่อน — say นี่คือเพื่อน. คนไทย — say นี่คือคนไทย. คุณนิ — say นี่คือคุณนิ.
  • Pause-and-produce: I point to someone. Say 'this is...' in Thai before I show the answer. Your turn.
S4

Who is that? — asking identity questions

Teach ใคร individually, then combine into เขาคือใคร and คุณคือ...ใช่ไหม for asking and confirming identity.

You can introduce someone with นี่คือ. But what if you want to ask who someone is?

Listen first. ใคร khrai. This means 'who'. Say it with me: ใคร.

In Thai, the question word goes at the end. So 'who is he?' becomes เขาคือใคร khǎo khuu khrai — literally 'he is who'. Listen again: เขาคือใคร. Now say it.

You can also confirm identity with a tag question. คุณคือคุณมาร์คใช่ไหม khun khuu khun máak châi mái — you are Khun Mark, right? You already know ใช่ from lesson one — ใช่ไหม means 'right?' or 'is that so?' Listen: คุณคือคุณนิใช่ไหม. Your turn — try it with any name.

Let's put it all together. Someone walks in. You ask เขาคือใคร — who is he? Someone answers นี่คือคุณมาร์ค. You confirm คุณคือคุณมาร์คใช่ไหม. They say ใช่ครับ. Now you have a complete identity exchange.

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
ใครkhraiwho
เขาคือใครkhǎo khuu khraiwho is he/she?
คุณคือ...ใช่ไหมkhun khuu... châi máiyou are... right?

Drills

  • Listen and repeat: ใคร khrai. เขาคือใคร khǎo khuu khrai. คุณคือ...ใช่ไหม khun khuu... châi mái.
  • Response-building: I point to a person. Ask เขาคือใคร. I answer. Then confirm with คุณคือ...ใช่ไหม. Build the full exchange.
  • Substitution drill: ask 'who is he/she?' — เขาคือใคร. Now confirm identity: คุณคือคุณนิใช่ไหม. Try with another name: คุณคือคุณมาร์คใช่ไหม.
S5

Pronunciation: rising tones in pronouns

Train the ear on the rising tone that appears in ฉัน, ผม, and เขา, with a minimal pair contrast.

Three of today's pronouns share the same tone. Listen: ฉัน chǎn, ผม phǒm, เขา khǎo. Can you hear it? They all rise up at the end. This is the rising tone.

The rising tone starts low and lifts up, like you are asking a question in English. Try it: chǎn. Your voice dips down then goes up. Again: phǒm. Again: khǎo.

Now compare. คุณ khun — that is a mid tone, flat and steady. เขา khǎo — rising, your voice goes up. Listen again: khun, khǎo. Can you hear the difference?

One more pair. ใคร khrai — mid tone, flat. ไข่ khài — low tone, your voice stays low. Different tones, different meanings. Listen: khrai — who. khài — egg. Which one means 'who'? Point before I tell you.

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
ฉันchǎnI (rising tone)

Drills

  • Tone echo: I say ฉัน chǎn. Repeat it. Now ผม phǒm. Repeat. Now เขา khǎo. Repeat. Feel the rise.
  • Minimal pair choice: I say two words — khun and khǎo. Which one is 'you' and which is 'he/she'? Point to the answer.

RECAP

What the learner should leave with

  • How do you say 'I' as a male speaker? Say it before looking.
  • Point to someone and say 'who is he?' in Thai.
  • Introduce a friend: say 'this is Khun Ni' in Thai.
  • Confirm identity: say 'you are Khun Mark, right?' in Thai.
  • What word means 'this'? And what word means 'is'?
