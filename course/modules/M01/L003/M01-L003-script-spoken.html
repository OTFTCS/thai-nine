M01-L003

Spoken Script — Pronouns and Who Questions

Use basic pronouns and ask who someone is using คือ and ใคร in short controlled exchanges.

S1

I and you — the first pronouns

Teach ฉัน, ผม, and คุณ as the building blocks for talking about people.

Let's start with the most important words in any conversation — I and you.

In Thai, there are different words for I. The general one is ฉัน chǎn. If you are male and want to sound polite, use ผม phǒm instead.

For you, the safe polite word is คุณ khun. You already know this from คุณชื่ออะไร — what is your name. คุณ means you.

So if someone asks who you are, you can say ฉันชื่อ... chǎn chûue... — I am called... Or if you are male: ผมชื่อ... phǒm chûue...

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
ฉันchǎnI (general)
ผมphǒmI (male, polite)
คุณkhunyou

Drills

  • Listen and repeat: ฉัน (chǎn), ผม (phǒm), คุณ (khun). Say each one twice.
  • Pause-and-produce: I say a situation — male speaker or female speaker. You say the right word for I before I show the answer.
  • Response-building: I say คุณชื่ออะไร (khun chûue à-rai). You answer with ฉันชื่อ... or ผมชื่อ... and your name.
S2

He, she, and this — pointing to people

Teach เขา, นี่, and คือ to build identity sentences.

Now let's talk about other people. เขา khǎo means he or she. Thai uses the same word for both.

นี่ nîi means this. And คือ khuue means is. So when you put them together: นี่คือ... nîi khuue... means this is...

Try it: นี่คือไมค์ nîi khuue Mike — this is Mike. Or: เขาคือครูนาย khǎo khuue khruu Naai — he is Teacher Naai.

คือ is special — it connects a name or identity to a person. Think of it as the equals sign.

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
เขาkhǎohe / she
นี่nîithis
คือkhuueis (identity)
นี่คือ...nîi khuue...this is...

Drills

  • Listen and repeat: เขา (khǎo), นี่ (nîi), คือ (khuue). Now together: นี่คือ... (nîi khuue...).
  • Substitution drill: I give a name. You say นี่คือ... with that name. Example: Mike → นี่คือไมค์.
  • Pause-and-produce: I point to someone and say เขาชื่ออะไร (khǎo chûue à-rai). You answer เขาชื่อ... with a name.
S3

Ask who someone is

Teach ใคร and the chunk เขาคือใคร to form who-questions.

Now the question word: ใคร khrai means who. In Thai, the question word stays where the answer would go.

So to ask who is he or she, you say: เขาคือใคร khǎo khuue khrai. Literally: he/she is who?

And to confirm: คุณคือ...ใช่ไหม khun khuue...châi mǎi — you are... right? You already know ใช่ from lesson 1.

Try the full exchange: เขาคือใคร — เขาคือไมค์ — ไมค์ใช่ไหม — ใช่ครับ.

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
ใครkhraiwho
เขาคือใครkhǎo khuue khraiwho is he/she?
คุณคือ...ใช่ไหมkhun khuue...châi mǎiyou are...right?

Drills

  • Listen and repeat: ใคร (khrai). Now the full question: เขาคือใคร (khǎo khuue khrai).
  • Response-building: I ask เขาคือใคร. You answer เขาคือ... with any name you choose.
  • Pause-and-produce: I say a name. You build the confirmation: คุณคือ[name]ใช่ไหม before I show it.
S4

Put it together — a mini identity exchange

Combine all new pronouns and patterns with review vocabulary in a realistic mini-dialogue.

Let's use everything together. Imagine you are at a gathering and you see someone you don't know.

You turn to your friend and ask: เขาคือใคร khǎo khuue khrai — who is that?

Your friend answers: นี่คือแอน nîi khuue Anne. เขามาจากประเทศไทย khǎo maa-jàak prà-thêet thai — she comes from Thailand.

You confirm: แอนใช่ไหม Anne châi mǎi — Anne, right? And your friend says: ใช่ครับ châi khráp — yes.

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
ขอโทษkhǎaw-thôotexcuse me / sorry
ประเทศprà-thêetcountry
ไทยthaiThailand / Thai

Drills

  • Role swap: I play the friend. You ask เขาคือใคร and I answer. Then you confirm with ใช่ไหม.
  • Substitution drill: I change the name and country. You rebuild the full exchange each time.
  • Production challenge: introduce yourself using ฉัน or ผม, then ask who the person next to you is.

RECAP

What the learner should leave with

  • How do you say I if you are male? ผม (phǒm).
  • How do you say this is...? นี่คือ... (nîi khuue...).
  • How do you ask who someone is? เขาคือใคร (khǎo khuue khrai).
  • How do you confirm someone's identity? คุณคือ...ใช่ไหม (khun khuue...châi mǎi).
  • What does คือ mean? It connects a person to their identity — is.
