# Visual Script — M01-L005 (Where and What Questions)

## Frame rule
- Keep all teaching visuals in the left two-thirds.
- The right third stays clear for Nine's talking head.
- Prioritise question frames, location contrast, and short answer cards over decorative scenery.

## Section 1 — Ask what something is with อะไร
- Layout: contrast-board
- Learning focus: this / that plus the what-question chunk
- Highlighted terms:
  - อะไร | à-rai | what
  - นี่ | nîi | this
  - นั่น | nân | that
  - นี่อะไร | nîi à-rai | what is this?
  - นั่นอะไร | nân à-rai | what is that?
- On-screen plan:
  - two large pointer cards: this vs that
  - one highlighted strip showing question word at the end
  - one note: “Thai often places the question word at the end of the chunk”
- Visual support choice: text-only

## Section 2 — Ask where something is with ที่ไหน and อยู่
- Layout: image-anchored
- Learning focus: where question plus here / there answers
- Highlighted terms:
  - ที่ไหน | thîi-nǎi | where
  - อยู่ | yùu | be located / live
  - ที่นี่ | thîi-nîi | here
  - ที่นั่น | thîi-nân | there
  - ห้องน้ำอยู่ที่ไหน | hâawng-náam yùu thîi-nǎi | where is the bathroom?
  - อยู่ที่นี่ / อยู่ที่นั่น | yùu thîi-nîi / yùu thîi-nân | it is here / there
- On-screen plan:
  - one large question card on top
  - two answer cards underneath: here vs there
  - small bathroom-sign or wayfinding image anchored behind the left content area only
- Real-world image helpful: yes
- Search ideas:
  - simple bathroom sign indoor wayfinding
  - school corridor restroom sign Thailand

## Section 3 — Recycle people words and numbers inside location questions
- Layout: drill-stack
- Learning focus: combine question system with review pronouns and digits
- Highlighted terms:
  - คุณอยู่ที่ไหน | khun yùu thîi-nǎi | where do you live / where are you?
  - เขาอยู่ที่นี่ | khǎo yùu thîi-nîi | he / she is here
  - ห้องหนึ่ง-ศูนย์-สามอยู่ที่ไหน | hâawng nùeng-sǔun-sǎam yùu thîi-nǎi | where is room one-zero-three?
  - อยู่ที่นั่น | yùu thîi-nân | it is there
- On-screen plan:
  - stacked prompt-and-answer cards
  - one small review strip showing 1-0-3 as digits from the last lesson
  - clear category split: person question vs place question
- Visual support choice: text-only

## Section 4 — Build a small help-seeking exchange
- Layout: dialogue-ladder
- Learning focus: turn the what/where system into a real service-desk interaction
- Highlighted terms:
  - ขอโทษค่ะ | khǎaw-thôot khâ | excuse me
  - นี่อะไรคะ | nîi à-rai khá | what is this?
  - นี่คือแผนที่ครับ | nîi kheuu phǎaen-thîi khráp | this is a map
  - ห้องน้ำอยู่ที่ไหนคะ | hâawng-náam yùu thîi-nǎi khá | where is the bathroom?
  - ห้องหนึ่ง-ศูนย์-สามอยู่ที่นี่ใช่ไหมคะ | hâawng nùeng-sǔun-sǎam yùu thîi-nîi châi mái khá | room one-zero-three is here, right?
- On-screen plan:
  - reception-desk dialogue ladder
  - map card appears as the object named by นี่อะไร
  - room 103 confirmation card appears at the end
- Real-world image helpful: yes, but keep it secondary
- Search ideas:
  - simple reception desk map brochure close up
  - classroom room sign 103 corridor

## Roleplay beat
- Show one turn at a time on the left side.
- Keep the object question, bathroom question, and room confirmation visually distinct.
- Let อยู่ที่นั่น and ใช่ครับ appear as clean short answer beats.
- Do not allow the map or sign imagery to spill into the right-third camera zone.
