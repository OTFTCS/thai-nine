# Spoken Script — M01-L005

## Opening
Today we build two question tools that make a beginner much more useful very quickly.

One tool asks what something is. The other asks where something is.

These are small questions, but they unlock real help: what is this, where is the bathroom, where is room one-zero-three, where are you?

---

## Section 1 — Ask what something is with อะไร
Start with the question word อะไร | à-rai | what.

Now put it into two pointing chunks:

- นี่ | nîi | this
- นั่น | nân | that
- นี่อะไร | nîi à-rai | what is this?
- นั่นอะไร | nân à-rai | what is that?

At beginner level, do not over-analyse the word order.

Just notice one useful Thai habit: the question word often comes at the end of the chunk.

So instead of building English inside your head, learn these as ready-made question lines.

---

## Section 2 — Ask where something is with ที่ไหน and อยู่
Now build the where-question.

- ที่ไหน | thîi-nǎi | where
- อยู่ | yùu | be located / live
- ที่นี่ | thîi-nîi | here
- ที่นั่น | thîi-nân | there
- ห้องน้ำอยู่ที่ไหน | hâawng-náam yùu thîi-nǎi | where is the bathroom?

อยู่ is a very useful beginner verb.

With a place or object, it often means is located. With a person, it can mean live, stay, or be, depending on context.

That is why the chunk ห้องน้ำอยู่ที่ไหน asks where the bathroom is, while คุณอยู่ที่ไหน can ask where you live or where you are.

For answers, keep them small first.

- อยู่ที่นี่ | yùu thîi-nîi | it is here
- อยู่ที่นั่น | yùu thîi-nân | it is there

---

## Section 3 — Recycle people words and numbers inside location questions
Now connect the new question system to the earlier lessons.

You already know the review word คุณ inside earlier chunks, and you already know เขา | khǎo | he / she.

So now you can ask and answer short location questions about people and places.

- คุณอยู่ที่ไหน | khun yùu thîi-nǎi | where do you live / where are you?
- เขาอยู่ที่นี่ | khǎo yùu thîi-nîi | he / she is here
- ห้องหนึ่ง-ศูนย์-สามอยู่ที่ไหน | hâawng nùeng-sǔun-sǎam yùu thîi-nǎi | where is room one-zero-three?
- อยู่ที่นั่น | yùu thîi-nân | it is there

This is the important module point: the new question words are not isolated grammar.

They plug directly into the people words and number words you already learned.

So the course becomes cumulative. A room number like one-zero-three is no longer just a number list. It becomes part of a real question.

---

## Section 4 — Build a small help-seeking exchange
Now make the lesson sound like real first-contact Thai.

If you need help, you can still start with ขอโทษ to get attention politely.

Then ask what something is or where something is.

A very small interaction is already enough:

- ขอโทษค่ะ | khǎaw-thôot khâ | excuse me
- นี่อะไรคะ | nîi à-rai khá | what is this?
- นี่คือแผนที่ครับ | nîi kheuu phǎaen-thîi khráp | this is a map
- ห้องน้ำอยู่ที่ไหนคะ | hâawng-náam yùu thîi-nǎi khá | where is the bathroom?
- อยู่ที่นั่นครับ | yùu thîi-nân khráp | it is there

One useful review link is that the answer can reuse the older identity frame นี่คือ.... The new question is what changes.

If you are checking a room location, you can also confirm it with ใช่ไหม just like earlier.

That gives you a clear service-style pattern instead of isolated vocabulary cards.

---

## Roleplay — Asking for help at a school reception desk
A: สวัสดีค่ะ ขอโทษค่ะ | sà-wàt-dii khâ khǎaw-thôot khâ | Hello, excuse me.

B: สวัสดีครับ | sà-wàt-dii khráp | Hello.

A: นี่อะไรคะ | nîi à-rai khá | What is this?

B: นี่คือแผนที่ครับ | nîi kheuu phǎaen-thîi khráp | This is a map.

A: ห้องน้ำอยู่ที่ไหนคะ | hâawng-náam yùu thîi-nǎi khá | Where is the bathroom?

B: อยู่ที่นั่นครับ | yùu thîi-nân khráp | It is there.

A: ห้องหนึ่ง-ศูนย์-สามอยู่ที่นี่ใช่ไหมคะ | hâawng nùeng-sǔun-sǎam yùu thîi-nîi châi mái khá | Room one-zero-three is here, right?

B: ใช่ครับ | châi khráp | Yes.

A: ขอบคุณค่ะ | khàawp-khun khâ | Thank you.

B: ไม่เป็นไรครับ | mâi bpen rai khráp | No problem.

---

## Recap
- Use อะไร at the end of a short chunk to ask what something is.
- Use ที่ไหน with อยู่ to ask where something is.
- Learn short location answers first: อยู่ที่นี่ and อยู่ที่นั่น.
- Recycle คุณ, เขา, and the digits from the last lesson so the new questions feel practical, not isolated.
