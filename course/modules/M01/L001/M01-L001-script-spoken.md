# M01-L001 — Hello and Basic Courtesy — Spoken Script

## Section 1: Your first Thai word: สวัสดี

Let's start with the very first word you will ever say in Thai. Listen first.

[THAI: สวัสดี sà-wàt-dii]

This one word covers hello, goodbye, good morning, good evening — all of it. สวัสดี is your safe greeting at any time of day. Walk into a shop, arrive at a hotel, meet a friend — สวัสดี.

Say it with me now: สวัสดี. One more time: สวัสดี. Good.

**Drill 1 — Pause-and-produce:** You step out of a taxi and walk into your hotel in Bangkok. The doorman smiles at you. What do you say?
[PAUSE 5s]
สวัสดี.

---

## Section 2: Polite endings: ครับ and ค่ะ

In Thai, you finish almost every polite sentence with a small word that signals respect. Think of it as the polite full stop at the end of your sentence. Without it, you sound blunt. With it — friendly, respectful.

First: ครับ khráp. Listen.

[THAI: ครับ khráp]

This is the male polite ending. Say it with me: ครับ.

Next: ค่ะ khâ. Listen.

[THAI: ค่ะ khâ]

This is the female polite ending for statements. Say it with me: ค่ะ.

Now let's combine. You already know สวัสดี. Add ครับ: สวัสดีครับ sà-wàt-dii khráp — a polite hello from a male speaker. Add ค่ะ: สวัสดีค่ะ sà-wàt-dii khâ — a polite hello from a female speaker.

**Drill 1 — Situational production:** You walk into a café and the barista looks up. Greet them politely. Use whichever ending fits you — ครับ or ค่ะ.
[PAUSE 5s]
สวัสดีครับ or สวัสดีค่ะ.

---

## Section 3: Thank you and sorry: ขอบคุณ and ขอโทษ

Two more words you will use every single day. Listen first.

[THAI: ขอบคุณ khàawp-khun]

This means thank you. ขอบคุณ. Say it with me: ขอบคุณ. Someone holds a door, hands you a coffee, gives you directions — ขอบคุณ.

Next. Listen.

[THAI: ขอโทษ khǎaw-thôot]

This means sorry or excuse me. ขอโทษ does two jobs — it opens a gentle interruption, and it repairs a small mistake. Need to squeeze past someone? ขอโทษ. Bumped into someone? Also ขอโทษ.

Of course, you will want to say these politely — so add your ending. ขอบคุณครับ. ขอโทษค่ะ.

**Drill 1 — Quick-fire round:** I give you a situation, you respond. A vendor hands you your bag of fruit.
[PAUSE 3s]
ขอบคุณครับ / ค่ะ. You accidentally step on someone's foot on the BTS.
[PAUSE 3s]
ขอโทษครับ / ค่ะ. A taxi driver drops you at your hotel.
[PAUSE 3s]
ขอบคุณครับ / ค่ะ. You need to get the waiter's attention.
[PAUSE 3s]
ขอโทษครับ / ค่ะ. You walk into the hotel lobby. First thing you say?
[PAUSE 3s]
สวัสดีครับ / ค่ะ.

## Section 4: Respond naturally: ไม่เป็นไร, ใช่, ไม่ใช่

You can now greet, thank, and apologise. But what happens when someone thanks or apologises to you? You need responses.

First: ไม่เป็นไร mâi bpen rai. Listen.

[THAI: ไม่เป็นไร mâi bpen rai]

This means it is okay, no problem, never mind. Learn it as one whole phrase — do not try to break it apart yet. Someone says ขอบคุณครับ to you? You say ไม่เป็นไร. Someone says ขอโทษค่ะ? Also ไม่เป็นไร. It smooths the moment warmly. Say it with me: ไม่เป็นไร.

Next: ใช่ châi. Listen.

[THAI: ใช่ châi]

This means yes, that is right. It confirms something is correct. Say it with me: ใช่.

And: ไม่ใช่ mâi châi. Listen.

[THAI: ไม่ใช่ mâi châi]

This means no, that is not right. Say it with me: ไม่ใช่.

Important: ใช่ and ไม่ใช่ answer the fact — is it right or not? ไม่เป็นไร answers the feeling — do not worry about it. Different jobs.

**Drill 1 — Response-building:** A stranger bumps you and says ขอโทษครับ. What do you say back?
[PAUSE 3s]
ไม่เป็นไร. Someone points at your hotel and asks a question. It is your hotel. What do you say?
[PAUSE 3s]
ใช่. They point at a different hotel. That is not yours.
[PAUSE 3s]
ไม่ใช่.

**Drill 2 — Mixed recall:** I describe a situation. You choose from everything you have learned today. Someone greets you at the front desk.
[PAUSE 3s]
สวัสดีครับ / ค่ะ. They hand you your room key.
[PAUSE 3s]
ขอบคุณครับ / ค่ะ. You knock over a glass of water at the desk.
[PAUSE 3s]
ขอโทษครับ / ค่ะ. They smile and wave it off — what are they probably saying?
[PAUSE 3s]
ไม่เป็นไร.

## Section 5: Pronunciation beat — hearing Thai tones

Before we roleplay, let's train your ear. Thai is a tone language — the pitch of your voice changes the meaning of a word.

Listen to two words you already know.

[THAI: ครับ khráp] — that is a high tone. Your voice rises sharply upward.

Now: [THAI: ค่ะ khâ] — that is a falling tone. Your voice starts high and drops down.

Try it yourself. Say ครับ with your voice going up. Now say ค่ะ with your voice starting high and dropping. Can you hear the difference?

Here is another contrast. Listen: ใช่ châi — falling tone, voice drops. Now: ขอบคุณ — the first syllable khàawp has a low tone, voice stays down low. Low tone and falling tone feel different.

Do not worry about mastering all five Thai tones today. Just notice: pitch matters in Thai. We will practise more tones every lesson.

**Drill 1:** Tone echo. I say ครับ khráp — repeat it. Now I say ค่ะ khâ — repeat it. Feel the difference in your voice.

## Roleplay: At the hotel reception desk

Let's put it all together. You have just arrived at a hotel in Bangkok. Walk through this exchange using everything you have learned — try to say each of your lines before I show the answer.

[SCREEN: Roleplay — hotel reception]

**You:** สวัสดีครับ sà-wàt-dii khráp — Hello.

**Staff:** สวัสดีค่ะ sà-wàt-dii khâ — Hello.

**You:** ขอโทษครับ นี่โรงแรมสยามใช่ไหมครับ khǎaw-thôot khráp nîi roong-raem sǎ-yǎam châi mái khráp — Excuse me, is this the Siam Hotel?

**Staff:** ใช่ค่ะ châi khâ — Yes, it is.

Your turn — what do you say to thank her?
[PAUSE 5s]

**You:** ขอบคุณครับ khàawp-khun khráp — Thank you.

**Staff:** ไม่เป็นไรค่ะ mâi bpen rai khâ — No problem.

Time to go — how do you say goodbye?
[PAUSE 5s]

**You:** สวัสดีครับ sà-wàt-dii khráp — Goodbye.

**Staff:** สวัสดีค่ะ sà-wàt-dii khâ — Goodbye.
