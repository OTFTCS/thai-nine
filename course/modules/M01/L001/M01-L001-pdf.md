# M01-L001 — Hello and Basic Courtesy: First Contact in Thai

## What you will be able to say after this lesson
- Target runtime: 9-12 minutes
- Scenario: A learner meets staff or a new person for the first time and needs to sound friendly, respectful, and calm immediately.
- Learner takeaway: By the end, the learner can manage a two-line first-contact exchange politely and recover with basic courtesy phrases.

## Core phrases (Thai / Transliteration / English)
- สวัสดี | sà-wàt-dii | hello
- สวัสดีครับ | sà-wàt-dii khráp | hello (male speaker)
- สวัสดีค่ะ | sà-wàt-dii khâ | hello (female speaker)
- ขอบคุณ | khàawp-khun | thank you
- ขอโทษ | khǎaw-thôot | excuse me / sorry
- ไม่เป็นไร | mâi bpen rai | it is okay / no problem
- ครับ | khráp | male polite ending
- ค่ะ | khâ | female statement ending
- คะ | khá | female question ending
- ใช่ | châi | yes / that is right
- ไม่ใช่ | mâi châi | no / not right

## Teaching notes and explanation cues
- Say hello the Thai way: Teach the core greeting and show that politeness is built into the line, not added as an afterthought.
- The first line you need is สวัสดี sà-wàt-dii. This is your safe, polite hello in a first meeting.
- On its own, สวัสดี already works. But in real interaction, Thai usually sounds warmer and more complete when you add a polite ending.
- Thank, apologise, and soften the moment: Teach the two highest-value courtesy phrases and explain that one form repairs the interaction while the other closes it warmly.
- The next two lines are ขอบคุณ khàawp-khun and ขอโทษ khǎaw-thôot.
- ขอบคุณ closes a small social moment well. You use it after help, service, or a small kindness.
- Pick the right polite ending: Explain what ครับ, ค่ะ, and คะ do so the learner does not memorize them as random decoration.
- Thai polite endings are not meaningless filler. They change how your line lands socially.
- ครับ khráp is the common male polite ending. ค่ะ khâ is the common female ending for statements, and คะ khá is the lighter female ending often heard with questions.
- Answer yes, no, and no problem naturally: Give the learner a small response toolkit so a first encounter can continue instead of stopping at one memorised line.
- Now add three high-frequency response tools: ใช่ châi, ไม่ใช่ mâi châi, and ไม่เป็นไร mâi bpen rai.
- ใช่ means yes or that is right. ไม่ใช่ means not right or no, but it is not aggressive by itself; it just corrects the idea.

## Mini dialogues / role-play
- Learner: ขอโทษครับ สวัสดีครับ | khǎaw-thôot khráp sà-wàt-dii khráp | Excuse me, hello.
- Staff: สวัสดีค่ะ | sà-wàt-dii khâ | Hello.
- Learner: ที่นี่ใช่ไหมครับ | thîi-nîi châi mái khráp | Is this the right place?
- Staff: ใช่ค่ะ | châi khâ | Yes.
- Learner: ขอบคุณครับ | khàawp-khun khráp | Thank you.
- Staff: ไม่เป็นไรค่ะ | mâi bpen rai khâ | No problem.

## Practice drills (self-study)
- Listen and repeat three times: สวัสดี, สวัสดีครับ, สวัสดีค่ะ.
- Teacher points to male or female speaker card; learner chooses the correct full greeting aloud.
- Courtesy drill: learner hears a situation and answers with either ขอบคุณ or ขอโทษ.
- Mini exchange drill: ขอบคุณครับ/ค่ะ -> ไม่เป็นไร.
- Substitution drill: สวัสดี -> สวัสดีครับ / สวัสดีค่ะ.
- Teacher says statement or question context; learner chooses ค่ะ or คะ if speaking in the female role.
- Teacher gives a true or false identity cue; learner answers ใช่ or ไม่ใช่.
- Repair drill: after a small apology, learner responds ไม่เป็นไร.

## Quick recap + memory aids
- Start with สวัสดี and add the right polite ending for a safe first-contact hello.
- Use ขอบคุณ to close the moment well and ขอโทษ to interrupt or repair politely.
- Treat ครับ, ค่ะ, and คะ as meaning-carrying social tools, not decoration.
- Use ใช่ and ไม่ใช่ for basic answers, but use ไม่เป็นไร to soften the moment.
