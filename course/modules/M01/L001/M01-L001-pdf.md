# M01-L001 — Hello and Basic Courtesy

## What you will be able to say after this lesson
- Target runtime: 9-12 minutes
- Scenario: You meet staff or a new person for the first time and need to sound friendly, respectful, and calm.
- Learner takeaway: By the end, you can greet, thank, apologise, respond to thanks, and answer yes or no politely.

## Core phrases (Thai / Transliteration / English)
- สวัสดี | sà-wàt-dii | hello
- ครับ | khráp | polite ending (male)
- ค่ะ | khâ | polite ending (female)
- สวัสดีครับ | sà-wàt-dii khráp | hello (male, polite)
- สวัสดีค่ะ | sà-wàt-dii khâ | hello (female, polite)
- ขอบคุณ | khàawp-khun | thank you
- ขอโทษ | khǎaw-thôot | sorry / excuse me
- ขอบคุณครับ | khàawp-khun khráp | thank you (male, polite)
- ขอโทษค่ะ | khǎaw-thôot khâ | sorry (female, polite)
- ไม่เป็นไร | mâi bpen rai | it is okay / no problem
- ใช่ | châi | yes / that is right
- ไม่ใช่ | mâi châi | no / not right
- ครับ | khráp | polite ending — high tone

## Teaching notes and explanation cues
- Your first Thai word: สวัสดี: Introduce สวัสดี as a standalone word — the universal Thai greeting — before combining it with anything else.
- Let's start with the very first word you will ever say in Thai. Listen first: สวัสดี sà-wàt-dii.
- This one word covers hello, goodbye, good morning, good evening — all of it. สวัสดี is your safe greeting at any time of day.
- Polite endings: ครับ and ค่ะ: Teach ครับ and ค่ะ individually as standalone polite endings, then combine with สวัสดี into polite greeting chunks.
- In Thai, you finish almost every polite sentence with a small word that signals respect. Let's learn two of them now.
- First: ครับ khráp. Listen: ครับ. This is the male polite ending. If you are a male speaker, you add ครับ after your sentences. Say it with me: ครับ.
- Thank you and sorry: ขอบคุณ and ขอโทษ: Teach ขอบคุณ and ขอโทษ individually, then combine each with ครับ/ค่ะ into polite chunks.
- Now two more high-value words. Listen first: ขอบคุณ khàawp-khun. This means thank you.
- ขอบคุณ. Say it with me: ขอบคุณ. You will use this one constantly — after help, after kindness, after service. Someone holds the door? ขอบคุณ.
- Respond naturally: ไม่เป็นไร, ใช่, ไม่ใช่: Teach ไม่เป็นไร, ใช่, and ไม่ใช่ individually so the learner can respond to thanks, apologies, and yes/no situations.
- You can now greet, thank, and apologise. But what happens when someone thanks or apologises to you? You need responses. Let's learn three.
- First: ไม่เป็นไร mâi bpen rai. Listen: ไม่เป็นไร. This means it is okay, no problem, never mind. Learn it as one whole phrase.
- Pronunciation beat: hearing Thai tones: Train the learner's ear to notice tone differences using words from this lesson, focusing on the high tone vs falling tone contrast.
- Before we roleplay, let's train your ear. Thai is a tone language — the pitch of your voice changes the meaning of a word.
- Listen to two words you already know. ครับ khráp — that is a high tone. Your voice rises sharply upward. Now: ค่ะ khâ — that is a falling tone. Your voice starts high and drops down.

## Mini dialogues / role-play
- You: สวัสดีครับ | sà-wàt-dii khráp | Hello.
- Staff: สวัสดีค่ะ | sà-wàt-dii khâ | Hello.
- You: ขอโทษครับ ใช่ไหมครับ | khǎaw-thôot khráp châi mái khráp | Excuse me, is this right?
- Staff: ใช่ค่ะ | châi khâ | Yes.
- You: ขอบคุณครับ | khàawp-khun khráp | Thank you.
- Staff: ไม่เป็นไรค่ะ | mâi bpen rai khâ | No problem.
- You: สวัสดีครับ | sà-wàt-dii khráp | Goodbye.
- Staff: สวัสดีค่ะ | sà-wàt-dii khâ | Goodbye.

## Practice drills (self-study)
- Listen and repeat: สวัสดี sà-wàt-dii. Say it three times.
- Pause-and-produce: you walk into a hotel lobby. What do you say? Say it aloud before I show the answer.
- Listen and repeat: ครับ khráp. Now: ค่ะ khâ. Repeat each three times.
- Substitution drill: I say สวัสดี — you add ครับ. Now I say สวัสดี again — you add ค่ะ.
- Your turn: I point to male or female. Say the correct full greeting: สวัสดีครับ or สวัสดีค่ะ.
- Listen and repeat: ขอบคุณ khàawp-khun. Now: ขอโทษ khǎaw-thôot. Say each three times.
- Discrimination drill: I describe a situation. You choose ขอบคุณ or ขอโทษ — say it aloud. Situation 1: someone helps you carry a bag. Situation 2: you need to pass through a crowd.
- Substitution drill: say ขอบคุณ with ครับ. Now with ค่ะ. Say ขอโทษ with ครับ. Now with ค่ะ.
- Listen and repeat: ไม่เป็นไร mâi bpen rai. Say it three times. Now: ใช่ châi. Three times. Now: ไม่ใช่ mâi châi. Three times.
- Response-building drill: I say ขอโทษครับ — what do you say back? Pause and answer. I say ขอบคุณค่ะ — what do you say back?
- Pause-and-produce: is this สวัสดี? ใช่ or ไม่ใช่? Say it aloud before I show the answer.
- Tone echo drill: I say ครับ khráp — repeat it. Now I say ค่ะ khâ — repeat it. Feel the difference in your voice.
- Minimal pair choice: I say two words. Which one is the high tone ครับ and which is the falling tone ค่ะ? Point before I tell you.
