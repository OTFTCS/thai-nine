# M01-L001 — Hello and Basic Courtesy

## Lesson identity
- **Lesson ID:** M01-L001
- **Module:** M01 — First Contact and Courtesy
- **CEFR:** A0
- **Target runtime:** 9–12 minutes
- **Register target:** Polite formal only

## Primary objective
Learner can greet politely, thank and apologise, choose a safe polite ending, and handle simple yes/no responses in a friendly first encounter.

## Secondary objective
Learner can recycle recent vocabulary while using basic affirmation and negation in short controlled output.

## Scenario / teaching frame
A learner meets staff or a new person for the first time and needs to sound friendly, respectful, and calm immediately. The lesson covers the first five seconds of meeting someone in Thailand.

## Must-teach outcomes
1. Say hello with correct polite ending (สวัสดีครับ/ค่ะ)
2. Thank someone (ขอบคุณ) and know when to use it
3. Apologise or get attention (ขอโทษ) and know when to use it
4. Respond to thanks/apology with ไม่เป็นไร
5. Answer yes/no (ใช่/ไม่ใช่) to simple confirmation questions
6. Choose ครับ, ค่ะ, or คะ correctly

## Core vocabulary (7 items — at limit for A0)
| Thai | Translit | English | Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| สวัสดี | sà-wàt-dii | hello | word |
| ขอบคุณ | khàawp-khun | thank you | word |
| ขอโทษ | khǎaw-thôot | excuse me / sorry | word |
| ครับ | khráp | male polite ending | word |
| ค่ะ | khâ | female statement ending | word |
| ใช่ | châi | yes / that is right | word |
| ไม่ใช่ | mâi châi | no / not right | chunk |

## Pronunciation focus
- **Target:** High tone (khráp) vs falling tone (khâ)
- **Minimal pair:** ครับ vs ค่ะ — different tones on the polite endings
- **Mouth-map anchor:** High tone rises sharply; falling tone starts high and drops

## Teaching constraints
- No prior vocabulary to review (first lesson)
- Max 2-3 new items per section
- 40%+ production drills
- Each new item in 3+ contexts (input flood)
- Pronunciation beat as dedicated section

## Likely learner mistakes
- Forgetting polite endings entirely
- Using ค่ะ when asking a question (should be คะ)
- Confusing ไม่เป็นไร with ไม่ใช่ (different social jobs)
- Flat tone on all words (no tone awareness yet)
