# M01-L001 — Hello and Basic Courtesy

## Lesson identity
- **Lesson ID:** M01-L001
- **Module:** M01 — First Contact and Courtesy
- **CEFR band:** A0
- **Target runtime:** 9–12 minutes

## Primary objective
Greet politely, thank and apologise, respond to thanks, and handle simple yes/no in a friendly first encounter.

## Secondary objective
Recycle recent vocabulary while using basic affirmation and negation in short controlled output.

## Scenario / teaching frame
You meet staff or a new person for the first time and need to sound friendly, respectful, and calm immediately. The lesson builds from single words to polite chunks to short exchanges.

## Must-teach outcomes
1. Produce a polite greeting (สวัสดีครับ / สวัสดีค่ะ)
2. Thank someone politely (ขอบคุณครับ / ขอบคุณค่ะ)
3. Apologise or get attention politely (ขอโทษครับ / ขอโทษค่ะ)
4. Respond to thanks or apology with ไม่เป็นไร
5. Confirm or deny with ใช่ / ไม่ใช่
6. Choose the correct polite ending (ครับ for male, ค่ะ for female)

## Core vocabulary (triplet form)
| Thai | Translit | English |
|------|----------|---------|
| สวัสดี | sà-wàt-dii | hello |
| ครับ | khráp | polite ending (male) |
| ค่ะ | khâ | polite ending (female) |
| ขอบคุณ | khàawp-khun | thank you |
| ขอโทษ | khǎaw-thôot | sorry / excuse me |
| ไม่เป็นไร | mâi bpen rai | it is okay / no problem |
| ใช่ | châi | yes / that is right |
| ไม่ใช่ | mâi châi | no / not right |

## Core chunks
| Thai | Translit | English |
|------|----------|---------|

| สวัสดีค่ะ | sà-wàt-dii khâ | hello (female, polite) |

## Teaching constraints
- Every new word taught individually before appearing in chunks
- Max 3 new standalone words per section
- 40%+ production drills
- Each new item in 3+ contexts across the lesson
- PTM transliteration with inline tone marks only
- No prior vocabulary to review (first lesson)

## Likely learner mistakes
- Omitting ครับ/ค่ะ, which sounds blunt in Thai
- Confusing ใช่/ไม่ใช่ (fact answer) with ไม่เป็นไร (feeling response)
- Pronouncing ขอบคุณ and ขอโทษ with flat English intonation instead of Thai tones
- Using ค่ะ and คะ interchangeably (one is statement, one is question)

## Image / visual notes
Almost entirely text-based lesson. Vocabulary is abstract (greetings, particles, responses). Focus-card and contrast-board layouts with clean triplet cards are most effective. No photographs needed.
