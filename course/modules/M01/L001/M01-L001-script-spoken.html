M01-L001

Spoken Script — Hello and Basic Courtesy

Greet politely, thank and apologise, respond to thanks, and handle simple yes/no in a friendly first encounter.

S1

Your first Thai word: สวัสดี

Introduce สวัสดี as a standalone word — the universal Thai greeting — before combining it with anything else.

Let's start with the very first word you will ever say in Thai. Listen first: สวัสดี sà-wàt-dii.

This one word covers hello, goodbye, good morning, good evening — all of it. สวัสดี is your safe greeting at any time of day.

Say it with me now: สวัสดี. One more time: สวัสดี. Good.

Imagine you walk into a shop in Bangkok. The first thing you say is สวัสดี. Simple, warm, and clear.

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
สวัสดีsà-wàt-diihello

Drills

  • Listen and repeat: สวัสดี sà-wàt-dii. Say it three times.
  • Pause-and-produce: you walk into a hotel lobby. What do you say? Say it aloud before I show the answer.
S2

Polite endings: ครับ and ค่ะ

Teach ครับ and ค่ะ individually as standalone polite endings, then combine with สวัสดี into polite greeting chunks.

In Thai, you finish almost every polite sentence with a small word that signals respect. Let's learn two of them now.

First: ครับ khráp. Listen: ครับ. This is the male polite ending. If you are a male speaker, you add ครับ after your sentences. Say it with me: ครับ.

Next: ค่ะ khâ. Listen: ค่ะ. This is the female polite ending for statements. If you are a female speaker, you add ค่ะ after your sentences. Say it with me: ค่ะ.

Think of ครับ and ค่ะ as the polite full stop at the end of your sentence. Without them, you sound blunt. With them, you sound friendly and respectful.

Now let's combine. You already know สวัสดี. Add ครับ: สวัสดีครับ sà-wàt-dii khráp — that is a polite hello from a male speaker. Add ค่ะ: สวัสดีค่ะ sà-wàt-dii khâ — a polite hello from a female speaker.

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
ครับkhráppolite ending (male)
ค่ะkhâpolite ending (female)
สวัสดีครับsà-wàt-dii khráphello (male, polite)

Drills

  • Listen and repeat: ครับ khráp. Now: ค่ะ khâ. Repeat each three times.
  • Substitution drill: I say สวัสดี — you add ครับ. Now I say สวัสดี again — you add ค่ะ.
  • Your turn: I point to male or female. Say the correct full greeting: สวัสดีครับ or สวัสดีค่ะ.
S3

Thank you and sorry: ขอบคุณ and ขอโทษ

Teach ขอบคุณ and ขอโทษ individually, then combine each with ครับ/ค่ะ into polite chunks.

Now two more high-value words. Listen first: ขอบคุณ khàawp-khun. This means thank you.

ขอบคุณ. Say it with me: ขอบคุณ. You will use this one constantly — after help, after kindness, after service. Someone holds the door? ขอบคุณ.

Next word. Listen: ขอโทษ khǎaw-thôot. This means sorry or excuse me.

ขอโทษ does two jobs. It opens a gentle interruption — excuse me — and it repairs a small mistake — sorry. Need to get past someone? ขอโทษ. Bumped into someone? Also ขอโทษ.

Now combine them with your polite endings. ขอบคุณครับ khàawp-khun khráp — thank you, politely. ขอโทษค่ะ khǎaw-thôot khâ — sorry, politely. You can mix any combination.

  • Substitution drill: say ขอบคุณ with ครับ. Now with ค่ะ. Say ขอโทษ with ครับ. Now with ค่ะ.
S4

Respond naturally: ไม่เป็นไร, ใช่, ไม่ใช่

Teach ไม่เป็นไร, ใช่, and ไม่ใช่ individually so the learner can respond to thanks, apologies, and yes/no situations.

You can now greet, thank, and apologise. But what happens when someone thanks or apologises to you? You need responses. Let's learn three.

First: ไม่เป็นไร mâi bpen rai. Listen: ไม่เป็นไร. This means it is okay, no problem, never mind. Learn it as one whole phrase.

Someone says ขอบคุณครับ to you? You say ไม่เป็นไร. Someone says ขอโทษค่ะ? You also say ไม่เป็นไร. It smooths the moment warmly. Say it with me: ไม่เป็นไร.

Next: ใช่ châi. Listen: ใช่. This means yes, that is right. It confirms something is correct. Say it with me: ใช่.

And: ไม่ใช่ mâi châi. Listen: ไม่ใช่. This means no, that is not right. It tells someone they are mistaken. Say it with me: ไม่ใช่.

Important: ใช่ and ไม่ใช่ answer the fact — is it right or not? ไม่เป็นไร answers the feeling — do not worry about it. Different jobs.

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
ไม่เป็นไรmâi bpen raiit is okay / no problem

Drills

  • Listen and repeat: ไม่เป็นไร mâi bpen rai. Say it three times. Now: ใช่ châi. Three times. Now: ไม่ใช่ mâi châi. Three times.
  • Response-building drill: I say ขอโทษครับ — what do you say back? Pause and answer. I say ขอบคุณค่ะ — what do you say back?
  • Pause-and-produce: is this สวัสดี? ใช่ or ไม่ใช่? Say it aloud before I show the answer.
S5

Pronunciation beat: hearing Thai tones

Train the learner's ear to notice tone differences using words from this lesson, focusing on the high tone vs falling tone contrast.

Before we roleplay, let's train your ear. Thai is a tone language — the pitch of your voice changes the meaning of a word.

Listen to two words you already know. ครับ khráp — that is a high tone. Your voice rises sharply upward. Now: ค่ะ khâ — that is a falling tone. Your voice starts high and drops down.

Try it yourself. Say ครับ with your voice going up. Now say ค่ะ with your voice starting high and dropping. Can you hear the difference?

Here is another contrast. Listen: ใช่ châi — falling tone, voice drops. Now: ขอบคุณ — the first syllable khàawp has a low tone, voice stays down low. Low tone and falling tone feel different.

Do not worry about mastering all five Thai tones today. Just notice: pitch matters in Thai. We will practise more tones every lesson.

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
ครับkhráppolite ending — high tone

Drills

  • Tone echo drill: I say ครับ khráp — repeat it. Now I say ค่ะ khâ — repeat it. Feel the difference in your voice.
  • Minimal pair choice: I say two words. Which one is the high tone ครับ and which is the falling tone ค่ะ? Point before I tell you.
