# Spoken Script — M01-L004

## Opening
Today we are building one of the most practical beginner systems in Thai: the numbers from zero to ten.

Not because you want to do maths, but because numbers show up immediately in real life.

You hear them in room numbers, phone numbers, table numbers, queue numbers, and very simple personal information.

---

## Section 1 — Build the first digit map: 0 to 3
Start with the first four digits you will hear all the time.

- ศูนย์ | sǔun | zero
- หนึ่ง | nùeng | one
- สอง | sǎawng | two
- สาม | sǎam | three

For room numbers and similar codes, Thai usually says each digit one by one.

That is an important idea. We are not always reading one big number. We are often reading a code.

One more note about หนึ่ง. In faster speech, Thai can reduce it later. But for now, keep the full careful form: หนึ่ง | nùeng | one.

Practice: say ศูนย์-หนึ่ง-สอง-สาม.

Now backwards: สาม-สอง-หนึ่ง-ศูนย์.

---

## Section 2 — Add 4 to 7 with pronunciation control
Now add the middle set.

- สี่ | sìi | four
- ห้า | hâa | five
- หก | hòk | six
- เจ็ด | jèt | seven

These are not all shaped the same.

สี่ is long. ห้า is open and clear. หก is short. เจ็ด ends sharply.

If you stretch หก or blur the ending in เจ็ด, you make listening harder for yourself.

Practice: สี่-ห้า-หก-เจ็ด.

Now listen and repeat only the one you hear.

---

## Section 3 — Finish 8 to 10 and ask for a number
Now finish the set.

- แปด | bpàaet | eight
- เก้า | gâao | nine
- สิบ | sìp | ten
- เบอร์อะไร | ber à-rai | what number?

สิบ means ten. But the main skill today is not building bigger numbers yet.

The main skill is hearing a digit clearly and saying it back clearly.

Now add this very useful chunk: เบอร์อะไร | ber à-rai | what number?

เบอร์ is a practical number-label word. You hear it with room numbers, phone numbers, table numbers, and tickets.

Practice question: เบอร์อะไร | ber à-rai | what number?

Practice answer: ห้า-ศูนย์-สอง | hâa-sǔun-sǎawng | five-zero-two.

---

## Section 4 — Use numbers in short real phrases
Now connect the numbers to the people language you already know.

- ฉัน | chǎn | I
- เขา | khǎo | he / she
- ฉันอายุสิบปี | chǎn aa-yú sìp bpii | I am ten years old
- เขาอายุเก้าปี | khǎo aa-yú gâao bpii | he / she is nine years old
- ขอโทษ | khǎaw-thôot | excuse me / sorry

This is the important module point: numbers are not separate from the earlier lessons.

You already know pronouns like ฉัน and เขา, and you already hear คุณ naturally in earlier first-contact exchanges. Now you are adding number information to those same people.

So Thai becomes cumulative: pronoun first, then the age pattern, then other facts such as a room number.

And if you miss the number, do not freeze. Use ขอโทษ | khǎaw-thôot | excuse me / sorry, then ask again.

---

## Roleplay — Class check-in with a room number
A: สวัสดีค่ะ ขอโทษนะคะ คนนี้คือใครคะ | sà-wàt-dii khâ khǎaw-thôot ná khá khon níi kheuu khrai khá | Hello, excuse me, who is this person?

B: คนนี้คือมีนครับ | khon níi kheuu Miin khráp | This is Min.

A: เขาอายุเก้าปีใช่ไหมคะ | khǎo aa-yú gâao bpii châi mái khá | She is nine years old, right?

B: ใช่ครับ | châi khráp | Yes.

A: ห้องเบอร์อะไรคะ | hâawng ber à-rai khá | What is the room number?

B: ห้า-ศูนย์-สองครับ | hâa-sǔun-sǎawng khráp | Five-zero-two.

A: ขอบคุณค่ะ | khàawp-khun khâ | Thank you.

---

## Recap
- Thai room numbers are usually said digit by digit.
- Use pronouns from the previous lesson so numbers describe real people.
- Use อายุ + number + ปี for beginner age practice.
- If you miss a number, recycle a repair phrase and ask again.
