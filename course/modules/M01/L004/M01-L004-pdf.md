# M01-L004 — Numbers 0 to 10

## What you will be able to say after this lesson
- Target runtime: 10-14 minutes
- Scenario: You are checking into a hotel, confirming your room number, and exchanging phone numbers with a new friend.
- Learner takeaway: By the end, you can count to ten, say your age, ask for a number, and give a room number in Thai.

## Core phrases (Thai / Transliteration / English)
- ศูนย์ | sǔun | zero (0)
- หนึ่ง | nùeng | one (1)
- สอง | sǎawng | two (2)
- สาม | sǎam | three (3)
- สี่ | sìi | four (4)
- ห้า | hâa | five (5)
- หก | hòk | six (6)
- เจ็ด | jèt | seven (7)
- แปด | bpàaet | eight (8)
- เก้า | gâo | nine (9)
- สิบ | sìp | ten (10)
- เบอร์อะไร | ber à-rai | what number?
- ฉันอายุ...ปี | chǎn aa-yú ... bpii | I am ... years old
- ห้อง | hâwng | room
- สาม | sǎam | three (rising tone)

## Teaching notes and explanation cues
- Zero, one, two: Teach the first three digits individually so you can begin counting immediately.
- Let's learn to count in Thai. We will start with just three numbers: zero, one, and two.
- Listen first. ศูนย์ sǔun. That means zero. Say it with me: ศูนย์.
- Three, four, five: Teach the next three digits and drill counting from zero to five.
- You already know ศูนย์, หนึ่ง, สอง. Now let's add three more.
- Listen first. สาม sǎam. That means three. Say it with me: สาม.
- Six, seven, eight: Teach digits 6-8 and build counting fluency up to eight.
- Good progress. You can count to five. Now let's add หก, เจ็ด, and แปด.
- Listen first. หก hòk. That means six. Say it with me: หก.
- Nine, ten — the full count: Teach the final two digits and drill the complete 0-10 counting sequence.
- Almost there. Just two more numbers and you will have the full set.
- Listen first. เก้า gâo. That means nine. Say it with me: เก้า. Falling tone — start high, drop down.
- Numbers in real life: Teach three practical chunks that use digits in real daily situations: asking a number, saying age, and giving a room number.
- You can count to ten. Now let's use those digits in real life.
- First chunk: เบอร์อะไร ber à-rai. It means what number? You use this to ask for a phone number or any number. Listen first: เบอร์อะไรครับ.
- Pronunciation: tones in Thai numbers: Train the ear to hear rising versus falling tones using number vocabulary from this lesson.
- Let's train your ear. Thai numbers have different tones, and mixing them up can cause confusion.
- Listen to these two: สาม sǎam — rising tone, your voice lifts up. Now: ห้า hâa — falling tone, your voice starts high and drops.

## Mini dialogues / role-play
- Staff: สวัสดีค่ะ ห้องอะไรคะ | sà-wàt-dii khâ hâwng à-rai khá | Hello. What room?
- You: ห้องห้าครับ | hâwng hâa khráp | Room five.
- Staff: อายุเท่าไหร่คะ | aa-yú thâo-rài khá | How old are you?
- You: ฉันอายุสามสิบปีครับ | chǎn aa-yú sǎam-sìp bpii khráp | I am thirty years old.
- Staff: เบอร์อะไรคะ | ber à-rai khá | What is your number?
- You: ศูนย์ แปด เก้า หนึ่ง สอง สาม | sǔun bpàaet gâo nùeng sǎawng sǎam | 0-8-9-1-2-3.
- Staff: ขอบคุณค่ะ | khàawp-khun khâ | Thank you.
- You: ขอบคุณครับ | khàawp-khun khráp | Thank you.

## Practice drills (self-study)
- Listen and repeat: ศูนย์, หนึ่ง, สอง. Say each one clearly after me.
- Pause-and-produce: count from zero to two in Thai. Go.
- Discrimination drill: I say a number. Is it ศูนย์, หนึ่ง, or สอง? Point to the answer.
- Listen and repeat: สาม, สี่, ห้า. Say each one after me.
- Pause-and-produce: count from zero to five in Thai. Go.
- Substitution drill: I say a numeral. You say the Thai word. 3 — your turn. 1 — your turn. 5 — your turn. 0 — your turn.
- Listen and repeat: หก, เจ็ด, แปด. Say each one after me.
- Pause-and-produce: count from zero to eight. Go.
- Discrimination drill: I say a number between zero and eight. Say the English meaning before I show it.
- Listen and repeat: เก้า, สิบ. Say each one after me.
- Pause-and-produce: count from zero to ten in Thai. The full sequence. Go.
- Substitution drill: I say a numeral, you say the Thai word. 7 — your turn. 10 — your turn. 4 — your turn. 9 — your turn. 2 — your turn.
- Response-building: I ask เบอร์อะไรครับ. Give me a three-digit number using Thai digits. Your turn.
- Substitution drill: say your age using ฉันอายุ...ปี. Try with สิบ, then with เก้า, then with ห้า.
- Substitution drill: say a room number using ห้อง. Try ห้องสาม, ห้องหก, ห้องสิบ.
- Pause-and-produce: I say a room number in English. You say it in Thai. Room 4 — your turn. Room 9 — your turn.
- Tone echo: I say สาม. Repeat. Now I say ห้า. Repeat. Can you hear the tone difference?
- Minimal pair choice: I say two numbers. Which one has the rising tone — สาม or ห้า? Point before I tell you.
