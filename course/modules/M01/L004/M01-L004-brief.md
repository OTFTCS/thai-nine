# M01-L004 — Numbers 0 to 10

- **Module:** M01 — First Contact and Courtesy
- **Level:** A0
- **Target runtime:** 9-12 minutes
- **Primary objective:** Learner can understand and say Thai digits 0-10 clearly enough for age, room numbers, and basic counting.
- **Secondary objective:** Learner can recycle recent pronoun and identity language while handling number pronunciation in short controlled output.
- **Teaching frame:** Practical number use in a room-number exchange plus age phrases that connect directly to the rebuilt Module 1 foundation.

## Must-teach outcomes
1. Hear and say the digits 0-10 with stable pronunciation.
2. Understand that Thai room numbers and similar codes are usually said digit by digit.
3. Use the chunk `เบอร์อะไร` | `ber à-rai` | what number?
4. Build the age pattern `ฉันอายุสิบปี` | `chǎn aa-yú sìp bpii` | I am ten years old.
5. Reuse prior pronoun and courtesy material while handling numbers.

## Teaching constraints
- Keep this as a real lesson, not a digit list.
- Explicitly explain why room numbers are said digit by digit.
- Tie age phrases back to the pronoun lesson so the module feels cumulative.
- Reuse at least two prior-course items naturally.
- Keep visuals in the left two-thirds of the frame.

## Likely learner mistakes
1. Rushing through the digits and flattening contrast between `สี่`, `ห้า`, `หก`, and `เจ็ด`.
2. Assuming room numbers should be read as one larger number.
3. Learning age language without plugging it into `ฉัน` or `เขา`.
4. Forgetting to use repair phrases when a number is missed.

## Image and visual notes
- Most of the lesson should stay text-first.
- One real-world image is still useful in the room-number section: a hotel corridor or room-number sign.
- The final section should stay text-led and show phrase-building from pronoun to age frame.
