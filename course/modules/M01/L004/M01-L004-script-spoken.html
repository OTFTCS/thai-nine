M01-L004

Spoken Script — Numbers 0 to 10

You can understand and say the digits needed for age, room numbers, and basic counting.

S1

The first six digits: 0 to 5

Give you a clean starting map for zero through five and show that Thai codes are usually read digit by digit.

Let's start with the first six digits you will hear constantly in Thailand. Listen first: ศูนย์ sǔun, หนึ่ง nùeng, สอง sǎawng, สาม sǎam, สี่ sìi, ห้า hâa.

Now say them with me: ศูนย์, หนึ่ง, สอง, สาม, สี่, ห้า. Good.

One important thing. For room numbers, phone numbers, and queue numbers, Thai usually says each digit separately. Room 502 is not five-hundred-and-two. It is ห้า-ศูนย์-สอง. Digit by digit, like a code.

Notice that สี่ sìi has a low tone — your voice stays flat and low. Do not lift it up. If you say sǐi with a rising tone, that sounds like the word for colour, not the number four.

Drills

  • Listen and repeat three times: ศูนย์-หนึ่ง-สอง-สาม-สี่-ห้า. Then say the same line backwards: ห้า-สี่-สาม-สอง-หนึ่ง-ศูนย์.
  • Your turn: I say a digit in English. You say the Thai word aloud before I show the answer. Ready? Two. Four. Zero. Five. One. Three.
S2

Finish the set: 6 to 10

Complete the digit set from six to ten, with attention to the short clipped sounds that beginners tend to stretch.

Now add the rest: หก hòk, เจ็ด jèt, แปด bpàet, เก้า gâo, สิบ sìp. Listen first.

Notice the shapes. หก and เจ็ด are short and clipped — your mouth closes quickly. แปด starts with a bp- sound that has no puff of air. เก้า is longer with a falling tone.

Try them with me: หก, เจ็ด, แปด, เก้า, สิบ. Good. Now the full set from zero to ten: ศูนย์, หนึ่ง, สอง, สาม, สี่, ห้า, หก, เจ็ด, แปด, เก้า, สิบ.

If you stretch หก or soften the ending in เจ็ด, it sounds mushy. Keep them sharp. Think of them like a quick tap, not a long note.

Drills

  • Clap drill: clap once per digit from หก to สิบ, keeping หก, เจ็ด, แปด, and สิบ short and controlled.
  • Random recall: I say a number in English. You say the Thai word before I show it. Seven. Ten. Eight. Six. Nine.
  • Substitution drill: I start a room-number code. You finish it. ห้า-ศูนย์-... say สอง. Now สาม-ศูนย์-... say หก. Now เก้า-ศูนย์-... say แปด.
S3

Ask for a number and say your age

Introduce the two practical chunks เบอร์อะไร and ฉันอายุ...ปี so digits become immediately usable in real exchanges.

Now make the digits useful. First, a question you will hear everywhere: เบอร์อะไร ber à-rai — what number? You hear this with rooms, tables, phones, and queue tickets. เบอร์ is a borrowed word for a number label.

Listen and repeat: เบอร์อะไรครับ. เบอร์อะไรค่ะ. Good. Now answer: someone asks เบอร์อะไร and the room is 307. You say สาม-ศูนย์-เจ็ด. Digit by digit.

Next, the age pattern. You already know ฉัน and เขา from the last lesson. Now add: ฉันอายุ...ปี chǎn aa-yú ... bpii — I am ... years old. Drop any number into the slot. ฉันอายุห้าปี. ฉันอายุสิบปี.

Notice there is no word for am in the Thai pattern. You do not say I am five years old. You say I age five year. The pattern is: pronoun, then อายุ, then the number, then ปี.

Drills

  • Prompt drill: I ask เบอร์อะไร. You answer with a digit string. Room 205? สอง-ศูนย์-ห้า. Room 409? สี่-ศูนย์-เก้า. Your turn.
  • Pattern drill: I give you a number. You build the age sentence. Five? ฉันอายุห้าปี. Eight? ฉันอายุแปดปี. Ten? ฉันอายุสิบปี.
  • Substitution drill: now change the pronoun. I say a number and a pronoun. เขา, seven? เขาอายุเจ็ดปี. ผม, nine? ผมอายุเก้าปี.
S4

Numbers in real exchanges

Move from isolated practice into short exchanges that combine numbers with known pronouns, courtesy phrases, and the age pattern, recycling prior material.

Now put everything together. You already know สวัสดี for greeting, ขอบคุณ for thanking, and ขอโทษ for getting attention. You know ฉัน, ผม, คุณ, and เขา for talking about people. Now numbers plug straight into that system.

Imagine someone asks คุณอายุเท่าไหร่ — how old are you? You answer: ฉันอายุแปดปี. Or ผมอายุสิบปี. Same pattern, different pronoun, different number.

If you miss a number, do not freeze. Say ขอโทษครับ or ขอโทษค่ะ, then listen again. A repair phrase keeps the exchange moving.

Let's practise a short exchange. I ask เบอร์อะไร. You answer with a room number. Then I ask อายุเท่าไหร่. You answer with your age pattern. Say your lines before I show the answer.

  • Substitution drill: change the person. เขาอายุสามปี. Now change the number: เขาอายุหกปี. Now change both: ฉันอายุสิบปี.
S5

Pronunciation beat: low tone vs rising tone

Train your ear on the low tone vs rising tone contrast that changes meaning in this lesson's vocabulary, specifically สี่ (four) vs สี (colour).

Before the roleplay, let's sharpen your ear. Thai tones change meaning. You have been practising this since lesson one with ครับ and ค่ะ. Today the key contrast is low tone versus rising tone.

Listen: สี่ sìi — that is a low tone. Your voice stays flat and low, like a calm, steady statement. Now listen: สี sǐi — that is a rising tone. Your voice lifts up from low to high, like asking really? in English.

สี่ with a low tone means four. สี with a rising tone means colour. Wrong tone, wrong word. Try it. Say สี่ sìi — keep it low and flat. Now say สี sǐi — lift it up.

Here is another contrast from this lesson. เก้า gâo — falling tone, means nine. เข้า khâo — also a falling tone, but different first sound. For now, just notice that the tone on เก้า drops down firmly, like saying no with conviction.

Drills

  • Tone echo: I say สี่ sìi. Repeat it with a low tone. Now I say สี sǐi. Repeat it with a rising tone. Can you feel the difference?
  • Minimal pair choice: I say one word. Is it four or colour? Point to the answer, then say the word back to me.

RECAP

What the learner should leave with

  • Look away and count from zero to ten in Thai. Say each digit aloud before looking back: ศูนย์, หนึ่ง, สอง, สาม, สี่, ห้า, หก, เจ็ด, แปด, เก้า, สิบ.
  • How do you ask for a room number or table number? เบอร์อะไร.
  • How do you say I am eight years old? ฉันอายุแปดปี — pronoun, then อายุ, then the number, then ปี.
  • What is the difference between สี่ sìi and สี sǐi? Low tone means four. Rising tone means colour.
  • If you miss a number, what do you say? ขอโทษครับ or ขอโทษค่ะ, then listen again.
