M01-L004

Spoken Script — Numbers 0 to 10

Count 0-10 in Thai, ask and say phone and room numbers, and say your age using digits in real daily contexts.

S1

Zero, one, two

Teach the first three digits individually so you can begin counting immediately.

Let's learn to count in Thai. We will start with just three numbers: zero, one, and two.

Listen first. ศูนย์ sǔun. That means zero. Say it with me: ศูนย์.

Next: หนึ่ง nùeng. That means one. Your turn: หนึ่ง.

And: สอง sǎawng. That means two. Repeat: สอง.

Now let's count together from zero: ศูนย์, หนึ่ง, สอง. Your turn — count from zero to two before I show the answer.

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
ศูนย์sǔunzero (0)
หนึ่งnùengone (1)
สองsǎawngtwo (2)

Drills

  • Listen and repeat: ศูนย์, หนึ่ง, สอง. Say each one clearly after me.
  • Pause-and-produce: count from zero to two in Thai. Go.
  • Discrimination drill: I say a number. Is it ศูนย์, หนึ่ง, or สอง? Point to the answer.
S2

Three, four, five

Teach the next three digits and drill counting from zero to five.

You already know ศูนย์, หนึ่ง, สอง. Now let's add three more.

Listen first. สาม sǎam. That means three. Say it with me: สาม.

Next: สี่ sìi. That means four. Your turn: สี่. Notice the low tone — your voice stays down.

And: ห้า hâa. That means five. Repeat: ห้า. This one has a falling tone — start high, drop down.

Now count from zero to five: ศูนย์, หนึ่ง, สอง, สาม, สี่, ห้า. Your turn — say the whole sequence before I show it.

Drills

  • Listen and repeat: สาม, สี่, ห้า. Say each one after me.
  • Pause-and-produce: count from zero to five in Thai. Go.
  • Substitution drill: I say a numeral. You say the Thai word. 3 — your turn. 1 — your turn. 5 — your turn. 0 — your turn.
S3

Six, seven, eight

Teach digits 6-8 and build counting fluency up to eight.

Good progress. You can count to five. Now let's add หก, เจ็ด, and แปด.

Listen first. หก hòk. That means six. Say it with me: หก.

Next: เจ็ด jèt. That means seven. Your turn: เจ็ด. Notice the short, clipped ending — your tongue hits the roof of your mouth.

And: แปด bpàaet. That means eight. Repeat: แปด. Another clipped ending — the sound stops sharply.

Now count from ศูนย์ to แปด: ศูนย์, หนึ่ง, สอง, สาม, สี่, ห้า, หก, เจ็ด, แปด. Your turn.

  • Discrimination drill: I say a number between zero and eight. Say the English meaning before I show it.
S4

Nine, ten — the full count

Teach the final two digits and drill the complete 0-10 counting sequence.

Almost there. Just two more numbers and you will have the full set.

Listen first. เก้า gâo. That means nine. Say it with me: เก้า. Falling tone — start high, drop down.

And the last one: สิบ sìp. That means ten. Your turn: สิบ.

Now the big moment. Count from zero to ten: ศูนย์, หนึ่ง, สอง, สาม, สี่, ห้า, หก, เจ็ด, แปด, เก้า, สิบ.

Your turn — count from zero to ten before I show the answer. Take your time.

  • Substitution drill: I say a numeral, you say the Thai word. 7 — your turn. 10 — your turn. 4 — your turn. 9 — your turn. 2 — your turn.
S5

Numbers in real life

Teach three practical chunks that use digits in real daily situations: asking a number, saying age, and giving a room number.

You can count to ten. Now let's use those digits in real life.

First chunk: เบอร์อะไร ber à-rai. It means what number? You use this to ask for a phone number or any number. Listen first: เบอร์อะไรครับ.

Your turn: เบอร์อะไรครับ or เบอร์อะไรคะ. Good. If someone asks you เบอร์อะไร, you answer digit by digit: ศูนย์ แปด เก้า.

Next chunk: ฉันอายุ...ปี chǎn aa-yú ... bpii. It means I am ... years old. Put any number in the slot. ฉันอายุสิบปี — I am ten years old. ฉันอายุแปดปี — I am eight years old.

Last chunk: ห้อง hâwng. It means room. Add a number after it: ห้องสาม — room three. ห้องเจ็ด — room seven. ห้องห้า — room five.

Let's practise using all three. Someone asks เบอร์อะไรครับ and you answer with digits. Someone asks your age and you say ฉันอายุ...ปี. You need a room and you say ห้อง plus the number.

  • Response-building: I ask เบอร์อะไรครับ. Give me a three-digit number using Thai digits. Your turn.

  • Substitution drill: say a room number using ห้อง. Try ห้องสาม, ห้องหก, ห้องสิบ.
  • Pause-and-produce: I say a room number in English. You say it in Thai. Room 4 — your turn. Room 9 — your turn.
S6

Pronunciation: tones in Thai numbers

Train the ear to hear rising versus falling tones using number vocabulary from this lesson.

Let's train your ear. Thai numbers have different tones, and mixing them up can cause confusion.

Listen to these two: สาม sǎam — rising tone, your voice lifts up. Now: ห้า hâa — falling tone, your voice starts high and drops.

Try it. Say สาม with your voice going up. Now say ห้า with your voice going down. Can you feel the difference?

Here is another pair: สอง sǎawng — rising tone. And เก้า gâo — falling tone. Rising goes up, falling goes down.

One more contrast: สี่ sìi has a low tone — your voice stays flat and low. Compare with ห้า hâa falling tone — starts high, drops. Say สี่, then ห้า. Low, then falling.

Core triplets

ThaiTransliterationEnglish
สามsǎamthree (rising tone)

Drills

  • Tone echo: I say สาม. Repeat. Now I say ห้า. Repeat. Can you hear the tone difference?
  • Minimal pair choice: I say two numbers. Which one has the rising tone — สาม or ห้า? Point before I tell you.
