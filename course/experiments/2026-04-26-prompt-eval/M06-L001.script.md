# Lesson Brief — M06-L001: Question Particles and Echo Questions

## Lesson Identity

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Lesson ID | M06-L001 |
| Lesson Title | Question Particles and Echo Questions |
| Module Title | Questions, Repair, and Real-World Understanding |
| CEFR Band | A1-A2 |
| Target Runtime | 18–22 minutes |
| Status | Draft |

## Objectives

**Primary:** Learner can ask clearer follow-up questions and echo unfamiliar information using ไหม / หรอ / เหรอ and the echo-question strategy.

**Secondary:** Learner can recycle recent vocabulary while using the repair toolkit (ว่าไงนะ, หมายถึงอะไร, อีกทีได้ไหม) in short controlled output.

## Teaching Frame

**Scenario:** A language-exchange café. Learner meets Mint, a Thai university student. The conversation flows, speeds up, and occasionally goes over the learner's head. The learner uses question particles and repair chunks to stay in the conversation.

**Central insight:** Repair is a communication skill, not a weakness. A learner who knows how to ask "one more time?" or echo back what they heard sounds far more fluent than one who goes silent.

## Must-Teach Outcomes

1. ไหม — standard sentence-final yes/no question particle
2. หรอ — casual confirmation/surprise particle (informal)
3. เหรอ — more emphatic/expressive version of หรอ; also the echo-question particle
4. อีกที — one more time
5. เมื่อกี้ — just now
6. ว่าไง — what did you say? (informal)
7. หมายถึง — to mean
8. แน่ใจ — sure / certain
9. ทำไม — why?
10. Chunk: ว่าไงนะ — what did you say? (softened)
11. Chunk: หมายถึงอะไร — what do you mean?
12. Chunk: อีกทีได้ไหม — could you say that one more time?
13. Echo-question strategy: [word or phrase] + เหรอ? = "Did you say X?" / "X, really?"

## Teaching Constraints

- Teach ไหม, หรอ, and เหรอ as three distinct items — not one merged explanation. The contrast between them is the lesson.
- Teach ว่าไง as an individual word before presenting the chunk ว่าไงนะ.
- Teach หมายถึง individually before building หมายถึงอะไร.
- อีกทีได้ไหม is built from ไม (known), ได้ (assumed partially known), อีกที (new this lesson) — connect explicitly.
- Echo-question strategy: model it with known vocabulary first (ไป, เจอ), then with lesson vocab.
- Review vocab from M05 (ไปด้วยกัน, เจอ, ปกติ, ความชอบ) should appear in drills and roleplay, not as re-teaching.

## Likely Learner Mistakes

1. **Tone on ไหม vs ไม่** — confusing the rising question particle (mǎi) with the falling negation (mâi). This is the #1 pronunciation risk in this lesson.
2. **Using ไหม in casual speech** — it sounds fine but learners should also know หรอ/เหรอ for natural conversation.
3. **Treating ว่าไงนะ as rude** — it isn't. นะ softens it. Learners sometimes avoid direct repair phrases, which stalls conversations.
4. **Literal echoing** — echo questions should sound natural (echo + เหรอ?), not robotic (repeat entire sentence + ไหม?).
5. **ทำไม position** — in Thai, ทำไม can appear at the start or end of a sentence; learners may default to English word order.

## Pronunciation Focus

Minimal pair drill: ไหม (mǎi, rising) vs ไม่ (mâi, falling). These two words are the most likely source of misunderstanding at this level.

## Image and Visual Notes

- Real-world images are helpful for the café setting (roleplay anchor slide).
- Contrast boards work well for ไหม / หรอ / เหรอ.
- The echo-question strategy benefits from a slot-frame visual (e.g., [ _____ ] + เหรอ?).
- The pronunciation beat benefits from a rising/falling arrow diagram over the two words.

## script-spoken.md

# M06-L001 — Question Particles and Echo Questions
**Script for Nine — Spoken Narration**

*Module: Questions, Repair, and Real-World Understanding | CEFR: A1-A2*
*Target runtime: 18–22 minutes*

---

## Opening Hook

You're in a Thai conversation. Someone speaks, and it goes a little fast. Maybe you caught one word, maybe nothing. What do you do?

Most beginners go silent. They smile and nod and hope the conversation moves on. But that's not real communication — and honestly, it doesn't have to be like that.

By the end of this lesson, you'll have five clear tools to stay in the conversation. Question particles, repair phrases, and a strategy called the echo question. Let's go.

---

## Section 1 — Question Particles: ไหม, หรอ, เหรอ

### Teaching Point 1: ไหม

Let's start with the most important question word in Thai. Listen first:

**ไหม — mǎi — ไหม**

This is a sentence-final question particle. You put it at the very end of any statement, and it becomes a yes/no question. Think of it as a spoken question mark.

- ชอบไหม — chôp mǎi — Do you like it?
- ไปไหม — bpai mǎi — Are you going?
- อร่อยไหม — à-ràwy mǎi — Is it delicious?

*[say it with me]* ชอบไหม. Good. It's clean, standard, and works everywhere.

---

### Teaching Point 2: หรอ

Now a softer, more casual version. Listen:

**หรอ — rɔ̌ɔ — หรอ**

หรอ carries a slightly different flavour. Instead of 'yes or no?', it feels more like 'is that so?' or 'oh really?' It's checking in, not demanding an answer. You'll hear it constantly in relaxed Thai conversation.

- ไปหรอ — bpai rɔ̌ɔ — You're going? (casual check)
- อร่อยหรอ — à-ràwy rɔ̌ɔ — Really, it's delicious?

*[listen first, then repeat]* หรอ.

---

### Teaching Point 3: เหรอ

And the most expressive version:

**เหรอ — rə̌ə — เหรอ**

เหรอ is surprised. Emphatic. 'Really?!' Someone tells you something unexpected — เหรอ? is your natural response. It can also be used as an echo particle, which we'll come back to later in the lesson.

*[say it with me]* เหรอ.

---

### Quick Summary + Drill

So here's your trio:

| Thai | Translit | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| ไหม | mǎi | Standard yes/no |
| หรอ | rɔ̌ɔ | Casual: is that so? |
| เหรอ | rə̌ə | Emphatic: really? |

All three go at the end. All three have a rising tone.

**Drill 1 — Echo with เหรอ:**
*Your situation: Mint just told you she went to meet a friend — ผมไปเจอเพื่อน. You're a little surprised. Echo it back with เหรอ.*

Pause now. Try before I show you.

...

**ไปเจอเพื่อนเหรอ?** — You went to meet a friend? Really?

Good. That's the echo in action.

**Drill 2 — Which particle?**
You want to ask a direct yes/no question. Which particle do you use — เหรอ, ไหม, or หรอ?

...

ไหม. The standard yes/no particle.

---

## Section 2 — Repair Words: อีกที, เมื่อกี้, and ว่าไง

### Teaching Point: อีกที

Next word. Listen:

**อีกที — ìik-thii — อีกที**

อีกที means 'one more time'. Break it down: อีก = more / again. ที = time / instance. One more time.

*[repeat after me]* อีกที.

It's polite, neutral, and completely natural. You can use it alone or build it into a longer request — which we'll do later.

---

### Teaching Point: เมื่อกี้

**เมื่อกี้ — mûua-gîi — เมื่อกี้**

This means 'just now' — a moment ago. If someone said something and you missed it, เมื่อกี้ helps you point back to that exact moment.

*[say it with me]* เมื่อกี้.

For example: เมื่อกี้พูดว่าอะไร — What did you say just now?

---

### Teaching Point: ว่าไง

**ว่าไง — wâa-ngai — ว่าไง**

This is very casual and direct: 'what did you say?' ว่า means 'to say'. ไง is an informal question word. Together: what did you say?

*[listen and repeat]* ว่าไง.

Now add นะ at the end. นะ is a softening particle — it makes things friendlier:

**ว่าไงนะ — wâa-ngai-ná — what did you say?**

*[say it as one smooth chunk]* ว่าไงนะ.

ว่าไง is direct. ว่าไงนะ is warm. Both are casual. Both are completely fine. ว่าไงนะ is the one you'll use most.

---

### Section 2 Drills

**Drill 1 — Situational:**
Mint is telling you about her กิจวัตร — her routine — and speaks too fast. You need her to repeat. Say อีกที.

*[your turn]* อีกที.

**Drill 2 — Echo with เหรอ:**
Mint uses the word เมื่อกี้ — just now. You caught that word. Echo it back with เหรอ to show you heard it.

...

**เมื่อกี้เหรอ?** — Just now, really?

That's the echo strategy in a two-word package.

---

## Section 3 — Meaning and Certainty: หมายถึง, แน่ใจ, and หมายถึงอะไร

### Teaching Point: หมายถึง

**หมายถึง — mǎai-thʉ̌ng — to mean**

หมาย = to intend. ถึง = to reach / about. Together: to mean.

*[repeat after me]* หมายถึง.

Now add อะไร — which you already know means 'what' — and you get:

**หมายถึงอะไร — mǎai-thʉ̌ng à-rai — what do you mean?**

*[say it as one chunk]* หมายถึงอะไร.

This is one of the most useful phrases in any language. You don't need to know the vocabulary — you just need to know how to ask what it means. หมายถึงอะไร.

One more time: หมายถึงอะไร.

---

### Teaching Point: แน่ใจ

**แน่ใจ — nɛ̂ɛ-jai — sure / certain**

แน่ = certain. ใจ = heart / mind. Sure in your mind.

*[say it with me]* แน่ใจ.

You'll use it in three main ways:

- แน่ใจไหม? — Are you sure? *(question with ไหม)*
- แน่ใจเหรอ? — Really sure? *(echo surprise)*
- ไม่แน่ใจ — I'm not sure *(negation with ไม่)*

*[your turn]* Say 'I'm not sure' in Thai. ไม่แน่ใจ.

---

### Section 3 Drills

**Drill 1 — Pause and produce:**
Mint uses the word ความชอบ — preference. You're not sure what she means in context. Ask her.

Pause. Say the line before I show it.

...

**หมายถึงอะไร?** — What do you mean?
Or more specifically: **ความชอบหมายถึงอะไร?** — What does ความชอบ mean?

**Drill 2 — Response building:**
You think Mint said ไปด้วยกัน — go together. You're not quite sure. Use แน่ใจ + เหรอ to express gentle surprise.

...

**แน่ใจเหรอ?** — Really sure?

**Drill 3:**
Mint asks: คุณแน่ใจไหม? — Are you sure? You're NOT sure. Answer in Thai.

...

**ไม่แน่ใจ** — I'm not sure.

---

## Section 4 — Why and One More Time: ทำไม, อีกทีได้ไหม, and the Echo Strategy

### Teaching Point: ทำไม

**ทำไม — tham-mai — why?**

Simple, important, universal. ทำไม.

*[repeat]* ทำไม.

It can go at the start or end of a sentence. At this level, put it at the start:

- ทำไมไปด้วยกัน? — Why are going together?
- ทำไมหมายถึงอะไร? — Why? What do you mean?

---

### Teaching Point: อีกทีได้ไหม

**อีกทีได้ไหม — ìik-thii dâai mǎi — could you say that one more time?**

You already know all three parts:
- อีกที = one more time
- ได้ = can / is possible
- ไหม = question particle

Together: *Can I have one more time? Could you repeat that?*

*[say it as one smooth phrase]* อีกทีได้ไหม.

This is a complete, polite request. Add ครับ or ค่ะ if you like. อีกทีได้ไหมครับ/ค่ะ.

---

### The Echo Strategy

Now the most powerful tool in this lesson. And it's simple.

When you hear a word in Thai but miss the rest, or when you want to check you heard correctly — just repeat that word back with เหรอ.

**[word you caught] + เหรอ?**

That's it.

- Mint says: ปกติฉันไปคนเดียว. You caught ปกติ. You say: **ปกติเหรอ?** — Normally, really?
- She says: ไปด้วยกัน. You echo: **ไปด้วยกันเหรอ?** — Go together?
- She says: ความชอบ. You echo: **ความชอบเหรอ?**

What happens next? She explains. She slows down. She gives you more input. The echo tells her you're listening, and it buys you thinking time.

This strategy doesn't need perfect grammar. It needs one word and เหรอ. That's real-world Thai.

## Section 5 — Pronunciation Beat: ไหม vs ไม่

*[allow 60-90 seconds for this section]*

Time for your pronunciation drill. This one is critical.

ไหม — your question particle — and ไม่ — meaning 'not' or 'no' — look almost identical in writing. Same consonant, same vowel. The only difference is tone.

**ไหม (mǎi)** — rising tone. Your voice lifts at the end. ↑

**ไม่ (mâi)** — falling tone. Your voice drops. ↓

Listen: **ไหม** ... **ไม่** ... **ไหม** ... **ไม่**

Mouth map: For ไหม, think of your voice doing a little lift at the end — like when you say 'really?' in English with that uptick. For ไม่, it drops firmly, like saying 'no' with finality.

This matters a lot. ชอบไหม? — Do you like it? That's a yes/no question. ไม่ชอบ — I don't like it. That's a negation. Wrong tone, completely different meaning.

**Discrimination drill:**
I'll say one. You decide: question or negation?

- ไม่ ... *(falling — negation)*
- ไหม ... *(rising — question)*
- ไหม ... *(rising — question)*
- ไม่ ... *(falling — negation)*

And bonus: หรอ and เหรอ are also rising. All three question particles lift up. หรอ — เหรอ — ไหม. Rising family.

Say them out loud one more time: **ไหม — ไม่**. Feel the difference in your voice.

## Roleplay — At the Language Exchange Café

*[set the scene warmly]*

---

**Mint:** ได้เลย! คุณมาที่นี่บ่อยไหมคะ?

**You:** มาบ่อยเหรอ... ใช่ มาบ่อยครับ/ค่ะ ชอบที่นี่มาก

Great! And you — do you like coffee?

*[your turn — answer the question. Notice she used ไหม.]*

*(châawp mâak ləəi lɛ́ɛo khun lâ tham-mai châawp thîi nîi?)*
I like it a lot! And you — why do you like it here?

---

*(châawp ban-yaa-gàat lɛ́ɛo gâaw yàak jəə khon mài-mài bpai dûuai-gan dâai mǎi khrâng nâa?)*

*[your turn — echo ไปด้วยกัน with เหรอ, accept, then check she's sure with แน่ใจ]*

**You:** ไปด้วยกันเหรอ? ได้เลย! แน่ใจนะ?
*(bpai dûuai-gan rə̌ə? dâai ləəi! nɛ̂ɛ-jai ná?)*
Go together? Sure! You're certain, right?
