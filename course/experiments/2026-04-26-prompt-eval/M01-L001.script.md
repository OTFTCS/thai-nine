# Lesson Brief — M01-L001: Hello and Basic Courtesy

## Lesson identity

- **Lesson ID:** M01-L001
- **Module:** M01 — First Contact and Courtesy
- **CEFR band:** A0
- **Status:** Draft

## Target runtime

12–16 minutes (self-paced); 20–25 minutes for live 1:1 with Nine

## Primary objective

Learner can greet someone, thank them, apologize, and say yes or no — all with the correct polite particle — in a short, real-world first encounter.

## Secondary objective

Learner understands the social job of ครับ/ค่ะ/คะ and can choose the right one for their own gender, setting the foundation for polite speech throughout the entire course.

## Scenario / teaching frame

You have just arrived in Thailand. You walk into a small café and have a brief, warm exchange with a staff member. You need to say hello, thank them, and handle a small "oops" moment — exactly the kind of interaction you will have in the first 10 minutes of being in Thailand.

## Must-teach outcomes

1. Learner can say สวัสดี, ขอบคุณ, and ขอโทษ alone and with the correct polite ending.
2. Learner understands that ครับ and ค่ะ/คะ are not words to translate — they are social tags that mark every polite sentence.
3. Learner can respond ใช่ and ไม่ใช่ to a simple yes/no prompt.
4. Learner can produce ไม่เป็นไร as a complete response to ขอโทษ.
5. Learner can deliver a 2-line greeting exchange without hesitation.

## Core vocabulary and chunks — triplet form

| Thai | Translit | English |
|---|---|---|
| สวัสดี | sà-wàt-dii | hello / goodbye |
| ขอบคุณ | khàawp-khun | thank you |
| ขอโทษ | khǎaw-thôot | sorry / excuse me |
| ครับ | khráp | polite particle (male) |
| ค่ะ | khâ | polite particle (female, statement) |
| คะ | khá | polite particle (female, question) |
| ใช่ | châi | yes / that's right |
| ไม่ใช่ | mâi châi | no / that's not right |
| สวัสดีครับ / ค่ะ | sà-wàt-dii khráp / khâ | hello (polite) |
| ขอบคุณครับ / ค่ะ | khàawp-khun khráp / khâ | thank you (polite) |
| ไม่เป็นไร | mâi bpen rai | no problem / it's okay |

## Teaching constraints

- This is lesson 1. Assume zero prior Thai.
- Do not introduce any grammar beyond polite particles and basic yes/no.
- Do not teach the Thai script reading system — use transliteration support throughout.
- All drills must be achievable in 1–3 words of learner output.
- ไม่เป็นไร is taught as a whole chunk; do not decompose it word by word in the main teaching flow.

## Likely learner mistakes

1. **Forgetting the polite particle entirely.** English speakers drop it because English has no equivalent. Address this directly.
2. **Using ครับ as a female speaker, or ค่ะ as a male speaker.** Gender assignment of particles is fixed — make this very clear early.
3. **Using ค่ะ in a question when คะ is correct.** This is a subtle but teachable distinction — tone matters here.
4. **Pronouncing สวัสดี as three even syllables.** The stress and tones make it sound very different. Model it slowly and have learners repeat.
5. **Treating ใช่ as "yes to everything."** In Thai, ใช่ confirms an identity or fact ("that's right") — not the same as agreeing with a verb. Note this briefly.

## Image and visual notes

- A warm, small Thai café or shopfront works well as the opening anchor image.
- A simple split card showing ครับ (male speaker icon) vs ค่ะ (female speaker icon) is the most useful teaching visual in the lesson.
- ไม่เป็นไร works as a dialogue bubble without needing an image — the roleplay context will carry it.
- Avoid stock-photo generic "Thai temple" imagery — this is a spoken language lesson, not a travel feature.

## script-spoken.md

# Script — M01-L001: Hello and Basic Courtesy
**Teacher:** Nine | **Level:** A0 | **Runtime:** 12–16 min

---

## Opening Hook

You know what separates someone who instantly feels at home in Thailand from someone who just looks like a tourist?

Five words. Just five words — and two tiny sounds.

Today you learn those five words. By the end of this lesson you will be able to walk into any Thai space, greet someone warmly, thank them, handle an awkward moment, and say yes or no — all in natural, polite Thai.

Let's go.

---

## Section 1 — Three Core Words

Let's start with the three words you will use every single day in Thailand.

Listen first. Just listen. Notice the shape of each word before you say anything.

---

**Word 1: สวัสดี — sà-wàt-dii — hello / goodbye**

*(Show triplet card: สวัสดี / sà-wàt-dii / hello-goodbye)*

Listen: สวัสดี.

Notice there are three parts: *sà — wàt — dii.* The last syllable is long and a little bright. Don't rush it.

Say it with me: สวัสดี.

One more time: สวัสดี.

One thing to know — this word covers both hello AND goodbye. One word, two jobs. Very efficient.

---

**Word 2: ขอบคุณ — khàawp-khun — thank you**

*(Show triplet card: ขอบคุณ / khàawp-khun / thank you)*

Listen: ขอบคุณ.

The first syllable has a falling tone — *khàawp.* It drops a little, like a gentle sigh. Then *khun* — short and flat.

Say it with me: ขอบคุณ.

Again: ขอบคุณ.

---

**Word 3: ขอโทษ — khǎaw-thôot — sorry / excuse me**

*(Show triplet card: ขอโทษ / khǎaw-thôot / sorry / excuse me)*

Listen: ขอโทษ.

This one does two jobs as well. You say it when you bump into someone — sorry. And you say it to get someone's attention — excuse me. Same word, both situations.

Say it with me: ขอโทษ.

Again: ขอโทษ.

---

**Quick drill — all three**

*(Show summary row of all three cards)*

I say each word. You say it back. Out loud — don't just think it.

สวัสดี … *(pause)* … ขอบคุณ … *(pause)* … ขอโทษ … *(pause)*

Good.

Now a pause-and-produce moment. I give you a situation — you say the right word before I show it.

You bump into someone on a narrow street in Bangkok. What do you say?

*(pause 3 seconds)*

ขอโทษ. That's right. ขอโทษ.

---

## Section 2 — The Polite Particles: ครับ and ค่ะ

Here is the most important habit in this whole course.

In Thai, every polite sentence ends with a small sound. It doesn't translate into English — you can't look it up and find a meaning. It's a *social tag.* It says: I am being friendly. I am being respectful.

Miss it, and you sound cold. Use it, and you sound like someone who genuinely gets Thai culture.

*(Show contrast board: ครับ / male icon // ค่ะ คะ / female icon)*

There are two particles — one for male speakers, one for female speakers.

**If you are male:** ครับ — *khráp.* One syllable, mid tone, short. ครับ.

**If you are female:** ค่ะ — *khâ.* Falling tone, gentle. ค่ะ.

These are not interchangeable. You use the one that matches your gender, and you use it every time you speak politely.

There's one more female form: คะ — *khá.* Rising tone. Female speakers use **ค่ะ** for statements and **คะ** for questions. You will feel this difference naturally — but let's note it now so it doesn't surprise you later.

---

Now let's put the particles together with the words you just learned.

*(Show chunk card: สวัสดีครับ / sà-wàt-dii khráp / hello — male)*

Male greeting: สวัสดีครับ.

*(Show chunk card: สวัสดีค่ะ / sà-wàt-dii khâ / hello — female)*

Female greeting: สวัสดีค่ะ.

Say yours now. Whichever version is yours — say it out loud: สวัสดีครับ or สวัสดีค่ะ.

*(pause 3 seconds)*

Now ขอบคุณ with the particle.

*(Show chunk cards: ขอบคุณครับ and ขอบคุณค่ะ)*

Male: ขอบคุณครับ. Female: ขอบคุณค่ะ.

Say yours. *(pause)*

---

**Situational substitution drill**

Same format now — I give you a situation, you say the full phrase with the right particle.

*(Drill 1)* Nakhon is a Thai man. He sees his teacher in the morning. What does he say?

*(pause 3 seconds)* — สวัสดีครับ.

*(Drill 2)* Mai is a Thai woman thanking a shopkeeper. What does she say?

*(pause 3 seconds)* — ขอบคุณค่ะ.

*(Drill 3)* You are greeting Nine at the start of your lesson. Say hello with your particle.

*(pause 3 seconds)* — สวัสดีครับ or สวัสดีค่ะ — both are correct, depending on you.

---

## Section 3 — Yes, No, and No Problem

Three more items — and these unlock an entire category of responses.

---

**ใช่ — châi — yes / that's right**

*(Show triplet card)*

Listen: ใช่.

Say it with me: ใช่.

This means yes, but more precisely it means *that's right.* You use it to confirm something someone has said. It's not a general-purpose yes for every situation — we'll come back to that in a later lesson. For now: it confirms facts and identity, and it's the right word for the drills in this lesson.

---

**ไม่ใช่ — mâi châi — no / not right**

*(Show triplet card, with ไม่ highlighted)*

Listen: ไม่ใช่.

Say it with me: ไม่ใช่.

See that word at the start — ไม่, *mâi.* That is Thai's main negation word — it means *not.* So ไม่ใช่ is literally *not-right.* This ไม่ will appear in many, many Thai phrases. You just met it for the first time.

---

**ไม่เป็นไร — mâi bpen rai — no problem / it's okay**

*(Show chunk card)*

Here's your third item — and it's a complete sentence on its own.

Listen: ไม่เป็นไร.

Say it with me: ไม่เป็นไร.

This is the perfect reply to ขอโทษ. When someone says sorry to you, this is what you say. Learn it as one complete unit — don't worry about what each part means separately. It's a social reflex: sorry → ไม่เป็นไร.

*(model it again)* ไม่เป็นไร.

---

**Drills — Section 3**

Pause-and-produce. I give you the situation — you say it before I show the answer.

*(Situation 1)* Your Thai teacher bumps your arm and says ขอโทษครับ. What do you say?

*(pause 4 seconds)* — ไม่เป็นไร. Warm and natural.

*(Situation 2)* Someone asks: คุณพูดภาษาไทยได้ไหม — can you speak Thai? You're just starting, so the honest answer is no. Say it politely.

*(pause 3 seconds)* — ไม่ใช่ค่ะ or ไม่ใช่ครับ.

*(Situation 3)* Someone asks if your name is [your name] — ใช่ไหม? Answer yes.

*(pause 3 seconds)* — ใช่ค่ะ or ใช่ครับ.

---

## Section 4 — Mini Roleplay: A Café Encounter

You have got everything you need. Now let's use it all in a real conversation.

This is the kind of exchange that happens every day in Thailand. I'll play the staff member — you play yourself.

Here is how this works: when it's your line, I show you a cue and I want you to say the line out loud before I reveal the answer. Ready?

---

*(Step through dialogue ladder on screen)*

**Staff:** สวัสดีครับ — *sà-wàt-dii khráp* — Hello.

*(Your turn prompt on screen — no answer yet)*

Your turn. Reply with your greeting. Say it now.

*(pause 4 seconds)*

สวัสดีค่ะ or สวัสดีครับ. 

---

**Staff:** ขอโทษครับ — *khǎaw-thôot khráp* — Sorry, excuse me. *(He accidentally bumps you.)*

*(Your turn prompt)*

You know this one. Reply before I show it.

*(pause 4 seconds)*

ไม่เป็นไรค่ะ or ไม่เป็นไรครับ. Warm and natural. He smiles.

---

**Staff:** ขอบคุณครับ — *khàawp-khun khráp* — Thank you.

*(Your turn prompt)*

You could just smile, but let's reply. You can say ไม่เป็นไร again, or just say your particle. Either works.

*(pause 3 seconds)*

ค่ะ or ครับ. Perfect.

---

**Staff:** คุณพูดภาษาไทยใช่ไหมครับ — *khun phûut phaa-sǎa thai châi mǎi khráp* — You speak Thai, right?

*(Your turn prompt)*

He's pleasantly surprised. Answer yes, with your particle. Say it now.

*(pause 4 seconds)*

ใช่ค่ะ or ใช่ครับ. You just confirmed it.

---

Let's run the whole thing one more time at natural speed. This time — say your lines before the subtitles appear. No cards. Just you.

*(Full ladder displayed — run through at natural pace)*

สวัสดีครับ — **สวัสดีค่ะ** — ขอโทษครับ — **ไม่เป็นไรค่ะ** — ขอบคุณครับ — **ค่ะ** — คุณพูดภาษาไทยใช่ไหมครับ — **ใช่ค่ะ**.

That is your first Thai conversation. Well done.

---

## Recap

Before we finish — let's check what you've got. Look away from the screen for these.

1. **Say hello to Nine right now — with your polite particle. Go.**
*(pause)* สวัสดีครับ or สวัสดีค่ะ.

2. **Someone bumps into you and says ขอโทษ. What do you reply?**
*(pause)* ไม่เป็นไร.

3. **How do you say thank you politely — male version, then female version?**
*(pause)* ขอบคุณครับ — ขอบคุณค่ะ.

4. **Someone asks if you are a student. Say yes in Thai. Now say no.**
*(pause)* ใช่ — ไม่ใช่.

5. **What is the difference between ค่ะ and คะ? When does each appear?**
*(pause)* ค่ะ is for statements (falling tone). คะ is for questions (rising tone). Both are female polite endings.

---

## Immersion Tips

Before you go — three quick things.

One: Try using สวัสดีครับ or สวัสดีค่ะ with a real Thai person today. Even in a Thai restaurant, even online. That first real use locks it in better than ten more practice rounds.

Two: Open the course flashcard deck and review today's seven words. These words are in every lesson from here on — the more automatic they feel, the easier everything ahead becomes.

Three: Next time you watch Thai content — a show, a reel, anything — listen for ครับ and ค่ะ. You'll hear them constantly now that you know what they are. Every time you catch one, you're training your ear for the rhythm of polite Thai.

Good work today. สวัสดีค่ะ. สวัสดีครับ. See you in Lesson 2.
