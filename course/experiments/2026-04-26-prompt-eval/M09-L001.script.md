# M09-L001 — Asking the Route
**Spoken Script for Nine**
Module 9: Travel, Transport, and Accommodation | A2
Target runtime: 18–22 minutes

---

## Opening

Hey — welcome to Module 9. This module is all about getting around: transport, travel, finding places. And we're starting right where you'd start in real life — standing on a street corner, needing to ask how to get somewhere.

Here's the situation. Your phone map is loading slowly. You know the name of the shop. You do not know the way. A local walks past. What do you say?

By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to ask ไปยังไงครับ/คะ — how do I get there — follow directions that reference Thai landmarks, and confirm the route with อยู่ใกล้ป้ายรถเมล์ไหม. Let's build that piece by piece.

---

## Section 1: Landmarks on the Road

Let's start with the words you'll hear most when someone gives you directions in Thai. These are your road landmarks — the anchors every direction-giver uses.

**First word: ทาง.**

ทาง. *[say it clearly]* Thaang. Mid tone — flat and steady. ทาง means way or route. You'll hear it in ไปทางนั้น — go that way — or ทางนี้ — this way. Say it with me: ทาง.

**Second word: แยก.**

แยก. Yâek. *[let voice drop on the falling tone]* This is an intersection — any point where roads split or cross. In Bangkok directions, แยก comes up constantly. Hear the falling tone — your voice drops. แยก. Say it back: แยก.

**Third word: เลี้ยว.**

เลี้ยว. Lîaw. *[falling tone again — drop it]* This means turn. The action of turning at a junction. เลี้ยว. Another falling tone. เลี้ยว. Your turn — say it.

*[beat]*

Now let's put these together.

Here's your first real direction chunk: **เลี้ยวตรงแยก** — turn at the intersection.

เลี้ยว = turn. ตรง here means right at. แยก = intersection. All together: เลี้ยวตรงแยก. *[say as one unit, not word by word]* Say it back to me: เลี้ยวตรงแยก.

That chunk is your building block for this module. If someone says เลี้ยวตรงแยก, you know exactly what to do. Let's make it stick.

---

### Drill 1 — Word Echo *(listen-and-repeat)*

Listen to each word once. Then say it out loud. Focus on the falling tone on แยก and เลี้ยว — let your voice drop.

ทาง. *[pause]* Thaang. Way, route.
แยก. *[pause]* Yâek. Intersection.
เลี้ยว. *[pause]* Lîaw. Turn.

*[run through once more at pace]*

### Drill 2 — Swap the Landmark *(substitution)*

You're getting directions. Start with เลี้ยวตรงแยก — turn at the intersection. Now imagine the landmark changes. The local says to turn at the bridge instead.

I'll give you the cue — say the full direction before I show it.

Cue: turn at the intersection. *[pause]* เลี้ยวตรงแยก.

Cue: turn at the bridge — สะพาน, you'll learn this next. *[pause]* เลี้ยวตรงสะพาน.

Good. You just used a word you haven't been taught yet. That's intentional — it's coming right up.

---

## Section 2: Finding the Spot — สะพาน, ป้ายรถเมล์, ใกล้ๆ

Now let's build your landmark vocabulary. When someone gives you directions in Bangkok, they'll name physical things on the street as reference points. You need these words.

**First word: สะพาน.**

สะพาน. Sà-phaan. Bridge. *[two syllables — low then mid]* In Bangkok, bridges over canals and roads are constant direction markers. สะพาน. Say it: สะพาน.

**Second word: ป้ายรถเมล์.**

ป้ายรถเมล์. Bpâai rót mee. Bus stop. Three syllables — listen carefully. ป้ายรถเมล์. The falling tone is on ป้าย — that part means sign or stop. รถเมล์ is the public bus. ป้ายรถเมล์. Your turn.

**Third word: ใกล้ๆ.**

ใกล้ๆ. Glâi-glâi. Nearby. The doubled form ใกล้ๆ means right nearby, quite close — softer and more approximate than ใกล้ alone. Falling tone on both syllables — let your voice drop. ใกล้ๆ. Say it back.

*[beat]*

Now your confirmation chunk. Listen to this whole thing first before you try it:

**อยู่ใกล้ป้ายรถเมล์ไหม.**

Is it near the bus stop? อยู่ means is located. ใกล้ = near. ป้ายรถเมล์ = bus stop. ไหม = yes or no question. All together: อยู่ใกล้ป้ายรถเมล์ไหม. Say it with me. *[model once more slowly, then at pace]*

The useful part of that chunk is the frame: อยู่ใกล้ [landmark] ไหม. Swap the landmark and you can confirm any location — อยู่ใกล้สะพานไหม, อยู่ใกล้แยกไหม. One frame, many uses.

---

### Drill 1 — Landmark Echo *(listen-and-repeat)*

Listen once, then repeat. Pay attention to the falling tone on ป้าย in ป้ายรถเมล์ — and the double fall in ใกล้ๆ.

สะพาน. *[pause]* Sà-phaan. Bridge.
ป้ายรถเมล์. *[pause]* Bpâai rót mee. Bus stop.
ใกล้ๆ. *[pause]* Glâi-glâi. Nearby.

### Drill 2 — Swap the Landmark — Confirmation Frame *(substitution)*

You've been given directions and you want to confirm the landmark. Use อยู่ใกล้...ไหม and swap in each landmark. Say each full question out loud before I show it.

Cue: near the bus stop? *[pause]* อยู่ใกล้ป้ายรถเมล์ไหม.
Cue: near the bridge? *[pause]* อยู่ใกล้สะพานไหม.
Cue: near the intersection? *[pause]* อยู่ใกล้แยกไหม.

Same frame each time — you're just rotating the landmark. Good.

## Section 3: Asking the Route — ไปยังไง and ตรงนี้

Now the heart of this lesson — how to actually ask for directions.

**First word: ไปยังไง.**

ไปยังไง. Bpai yang ngâi. This means how do I get there — or literally, go how?

ไป means go. ยังไง is the colloquial spoken form of อย่างไร — which means how. So ไปยังไง is go how, which in natural English is how do I get there. Listen again: ไปยังไง.

Quick note on ยังไง — it's casual and conversational. It's not rude. It's what real people say on the street. But in any real interaction, add ครับ or คะ at the end for politeness. That gives you:

ไปยังไงครับ — male speaker.
ไปยังไงคะ — female speaker.

Both are correct. Learn both.

**Second word: ตรงนี้.**

ตรงนี้. Trong nîi. Here. Right here.

ตรง means right at or straight. นี้ means this. Together: right here, this spot, this exact place. *[point straight down as you say it]* ตรงนี้. Say it back.

In conversation you can pair them: ไปยังไงจากตรงนี้ครับ — from here, how do I get there? But the shorter ไปยังไงครับ is perfectly natural. Context makes from here understood.

*[beat]*

Now: your turn to produce. Pause the lesson here. You're standing on the street. You want to find the ร้าน — the shop, from your last module. Say the full polite direction question before I show the answer.

*[long pause — hold the cue on screen]*

ไปยังไงครับ/คะ. Well done.

---

### Drill 1 — Pause and Produce *(pause-and-produce)*

Situation: you've just stopped a local in a busy market area. They're looking at you, waiting. What's your question?

*[pause — let learner produce]*

ไปยังไงครับ. Bpai yang ngâi khráp.

### Drill 2 — Hear a Direction and Recall *(response-building — interleaved recall from Section 1)*

You asked the question. The local answers: เลี้ยวตรงแยก.

You heard those words in Section 1. What does the direction mean? Say the English first, then repeat the Thai.

*[pause]*

Turn at the intersection. เลี้ยวตรงแยก.

## Section 4: Pronunciation Beat — ใกล้ vs ไกล

Stop for a moment. There's a tone contrast in this lesson that matters a lot — and I want to deal with it directly before the roleplay.

Two words: ใกล้ and ไกล.

They look similar on the page. They sound almost the same. But they mean opposite things — and if you mix them up when someone is giving you directions, you could end up walking completely the wrong way.

ใกล้ means near. Glâi. Hear the fall? Your voice starts mid-high and drops down. ใกล้. Near.

ไกล means far. Glai. Mid tone — flat and even, no drop at all. ไกล. Far.

The difference is entirely in the tone. Near — voice falls. Far — voice stays level.

Say them both: ใกล้... ไกล. *[model, then pause for learner]*

---

### Drill 1 — Near or Far? Ear Test *(minimal-pair-choice)*

I'll say one word. You say near or far out loud before the answer appears. Trust your ear — don't wait for the screen.

ไกล. *[pause]* Far. Flat tone.
ใกล้. *[pause]* Near. Falling tone.
ใกล้. *[pause]* Near. Falling tone.
ไกล. *[pause]* Far. Flat tone.

### Drill 2 — Tone Echo — Exaggerate the Fall *(tone-echo)*

Repeat each word after me. For ใกล้ — exaggerate the fall. Really let your voice drop. For ไกล — hold it perfectly flat, no movement.

ใกล้. *[pause — model with exaggerated fall]* Voice drops. ใกล้.
ไกล. *[pause — model perfectly flat]* Stay level. ไกล.
ใกล้ๆ. *[pause]* Nearby — double fall. Both syllables drop. ใกล้ๆ.

Good. Keep those two separate. They come up constantly in direction conversations.

## Section 5: Putting the Exchange Together

You've got all the pieces. Now let's run them as a real exchange — the kind you'll have on a Bangkok street corner.

The pattern is three steps.

Step one — you ask: ไปยังไงครับ/คะ.
Step two — the local tells you: เลี้ยวตรงแยก.
Step three — you confirm: อยู่ใกล้ป้ายรถเมล์ไหม.

Ask. Receive. Confirm. That's your whole toolkit for this lesson.

Situation: you're looking for the ร้าน — the shop. You know it's a new สาขา, a new branch, from your last module. You're standing at ตรงนี้. 

Step one: ask how to get there. Pause and say it. *[pause]*

The local says: ไปตรงๆ แล้วเลี้ยวตรงแยก — go straight, then turn at the intersection. You understood เลี้ยวตรงแยก — that's from Section 1.

Step three: confirm the landmark. Is it near the bus stop? Pause and ask. *[pause]*

อยู่ใกล้ป้ายรถเมล์ไหมครับ/คะ. Good.

That three-step rhythm is exactly what you'll use in the roleplay.

---

### Drill 1 — Landmark Rotation — Confirmation Frame *(substitution — interleaved recall)*

Cue: near the bridge? *[pause]* อยู่ใกล้สะพานไหม.
Cue: near the intersection? *[pause]* อยู่ใกล้แยกไหม.

### Drill 2 — Build the Full Exchange *(response-building — all sections recalled)*

Build the complete exchange step by step. Say each Thai line before I show it.

You're looking for the new สาขา — the new branch of the shop.

Step 1: Ask how to get there. Female speaker, polite. *[pause]* ไปยังไงคะ.

Step 2: Local says เลี้ยวตรงแยก. Translate it. *[pause]* Turn at the intersection.

Step 3: Confirm it's near the bus stop. *[pause]* อยู่ใกล้ป้ายรถเมล์ไหมคะ.

Step 4: Local says ใช่ค่ะ อยู่ใกล้ๆ. Is that near or far? *[pause]* Near — ใกล้ๆ has the falling tone.
