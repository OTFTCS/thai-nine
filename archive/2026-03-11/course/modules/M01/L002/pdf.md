# M01-L002 — The Thai Sound System

## What you will be able to say after this lesson
- Distinguish consonant contrast, vowel length, and tone contour with practical beginner listening drills.

## Core phrases (Thai / Transliteration / English)
- ไก่ | gài | chicken
- ไข่ | khài | egg
- ใกล้ | glâi | near
- ไกล | glǎi | far
- ขา | khǎa | leg
- ค่า | khâa | value / fee

## Pronunciation tips (simple and practical)
- Compare ไก่ gài and ไข่ khài.
- One breathy consonant changes meaning immediately.
- ใกล้ glâi means near.
- ไกล glǎi marks far in this lesson contrast set.
- ขา khǎa (leg) and ค่า khâa (value/fee) are not interchangeable.
- Length + tone must be heard together.

## Mini dialogues / role-play
- Tutor: คำนี้คือ ไข่ | kham-níi kheuu khài | This word is egg.
- Learner: ไข่ khài | khài | Egg.
- Tutor: ใกล้ หรือ ไกล | glâi rǔue glǎi | Near or far?
- Learner: ใกล้ glâi | glâi | Near.
- Tutor: ขา หรือ ค่า | khǎa rǔue khâa | Leg or value?
- Learner: ค่า khâa | khâa | Value.

## Practice drills (self-study)
- Point to chicken/egg cards after hearing each word.
- Say gài / khài in alternating pairs.
- Tutor says one; learner points near/far.
- Swap roles and repeat at natural speed.
- Chant khǎa then khâa with clear timing.
- Minimal pair listening at slow speed.
